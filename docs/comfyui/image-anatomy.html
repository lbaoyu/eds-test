ComfyUI · diffusion reference

The anatomy of a
generated image

Every image breaks down into components — and each component is influenced by a specific part of the pipeline. Learn the map, and you stop guessing.

ComfyUI and diffusion models don't paint pixels directly. They combine information from many sources to answer one question:

“What image best satisfies all the guidance?”

To control images well, you need to understand what can be controlled.

Prompt A beautiful woman in a red dress sitting on a balcony at sunset, fantasy castle in the background, cinematic lighting
Negative prompt blurry, low quality, bad anatomy, extra limbs

01 · The components

What an image is made of

Break any image into eight fundamental components. Each one can be steered independently.

01

Identity

The specific person or face, and her features.

02

Pose & gesture

Body position, head tilt, where she's looking.

03

Composition

Framing, subject placement, balance.

04

Perspective

Camera angle, depth, how 3D space is arranged.

05

Lighting

Direction, intensity, shadows, rim light.

06

Colour & mood

Overall colour harmony and emotional tone.

07

Style

Photorealistic? Cinematic? Fantasy? Anime? Painting?

08

Details & texture

Fabric, skin, stone, clouds — the small things.

04 · In practice

Changing one component at a time

Same prompt and seed every time — only the named control changes.

A

Change identity

IPAdapter

Swap the reference face and the person changes — while pose, dress, castle and lighting stay locked.

B

Change pose

ControlNet · OpenPose

Feed a skeleton (far right) and she sits, stands or turns, while the rest of the scene holds steady.

C

Change composition

Crop / Tile / Manual

Reframe the shot — subject left, centred, or distant — rearranging the same elements.

D

Change perspective

Depth ControlNet

Shift the camera and 3D structure via a depth map: lower angle, higher angle, deeper space.

E

Change lighting & mood

Prompt

Change the words — golden hour, moonlight, overcast — and the whole scene re-lights.

F

Change style

Checkpoint or LoRA
PhotorealisticCinematicAnimeOil paintingFantasy art

The exact same scene, rendered through a different model's eyes.

05 · Quick reference

Which tool, when

Start from what you want, not from the node list.

I want a specific person→IPAdapter
I want a specific pose→Pose ControlNet
I want exact composition→Crop, Tile, or Compose in workflow
I want better 3D structure→Depth or Lineart ControlNet
I want a specific style→LoRA or Style Checkpoint
I want better details→Higher resolution, less denoise, hi-res fix
I want different mood / colour→Prompt + Colour grading

06 · Why this matters

The payoff

You stop guessing.
You know which tool to use.
You understand why results change.
You solve problems systematically.

Mastery in ComfyUI isn't about memorising nodes. It's about understanding what each part of the pipeline controls.
