04Priorities

1 · Win fund questions build

On fund questions KBank leads share of voice but is cited from its own pages far less (9.6% owned, mostly K-Asset rather than the main site). The clearest target is product-choice fund selection. Build owned fund content the assistants can read (fund-selection guides by goal, risk and horizon; fund comparisons; RMF / SSF / Thai ESG tax-fund guides), connect K-Asset pages to kasikornbank.com, and get KBank funds well-described on the aggregators the assistants already cite. Detail in the Investment Funds tab.

2 · Defend card questions defend

Cards score 48.2 (88.6 on branded prompts that name KBank), with owned promotion pages as the cited sources and no negative sentiment. The risk is that promotions expire — a citation to an expired promo page is a real risk — and KTC is close behind. Keep cited promotion pages current and monitor weekly. Detail in the Credit Cards tab.

3 · Make pages readable to crawlers

47 of 82 measured pages show less than half their content to an AI crawler (some as little as 1–5%), because the content loads after the page is read. Pre-render the priority card and fund pages so the text is in the page when the crawler reads it. This is an engineering fix, not a content change, and it increases the payoff from the two priorities above.

Setup fixes. Connect a CDN to turn on crawl-access and referral reporting (both blank today). Add KTC, UOB and ttb to competitor tracking — they show zero there but appear with real numbers in Brand Presence, so the list is incomplete.

06Actions

1 · Keep cited promotion pages current

Owned promotion pages are the cited sources behind a 48.2 average and 88.6 on branded prompts. The risk: promotions expire, and a citation to an expired promo page is a real risk. Keep cited pages accurate as campaigns change, and check weekly for visibility drops or new competitor citations.

2 · Pre-render the hard-to-read card pages

Card pages such as travel-tripdotcom, unionpay and plustinum show 5–25% of their content to crawlers. Where the crawler can't read the page, it can't cite it. Pre-render the priority card pages so the text is present on load — no content change needed.

3 · Fill the eligibility & lifestyle gap

Eligibility & lifestyle is the weak card topic at 23.4 — "which card suits me" questions with no clear owned page. Build owned "choose your card" guidance by spending pattern, and earn placement on the card-comparison publishers that already win citations.

05Actions

1 · Build fund content the assistants can read

1,368 fund citations, only 8.9% owned on the top-demand fund-selection topic, and 12 prompts showing no result. The assistants answer confidently from aggregators — they just aren't choosing KBank. Add owned content the assistants can read that answers the questions directly: fund-selection guides by goal, risk and horizon; a fund comparison; and RMF / SSF / Thai ESG tax-fund guides on kasikornbank.com and kasikornasset.com.

2 · Build on K-Asset and connect it to the main site

K-Asset already supplies 86 of KBank's 131 owned fund citations — it is the source of almost all KBank-owned fund citations. But the main site adds only 45, and the two aren't well linked. Strengthen K-Asset fund pages for citation (clear summaries, comparison-ready structure) and link to them from kasikornbank.com investment pages, so the main site appears too.

3 · Get KBank funds onto the aggregators

Fund answers cite aggregators — yuanta mutualfund, my-best, wealthmagik, finnomena. Make sure KBank and K-Asset funds are well represented and accurately described there, so KBank products appear even when the answer cites a third party.

Setup. Connect a CDN to turn on crawl-access and referral reporting, and to confirm new fund pages are readable on publish — so the content build pays off.
