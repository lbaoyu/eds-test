Card Services

Prompt Design Summary and Recommendations

The current Card Services prompt set has a strong foundation and already reflects many of the most common customer support needs. The clearest opportunities now are to reduce overlap, simplify a small number of broad prompts, and move a few questions that sit outside core card servicing.

Overview

The prompt set already covers many of the right card-servicing journeys, especially around fees, payment support, statements, declined transactions, and basic troubleshooting.

The main opportunity is not to expand the category, but to make it more consistent. A smaller group of prompts either overlaps heavily, combines multiple issues in one prompt, or feels more naturally aligned to account servicing, transfers, or digital banking support.

Summary of Recommendations

What is working well

  • The set addresses practical, day-to-day card support needs.
  • Fee, payment, and card-usage prompts are generally strong and customer-friendly.
  • The category already contains a solid service-oriented backbone.

Where to focus next

  • Merge overlapping fee-waiver prompts.
  • Rewrite a few prompts that bundle multiple issues into one question.
  • Move the clearest non-card prompts into more suitable servicing categories.
  • Review a small number of transfer and authentication prompts for category fit.

Category Focus and Scope

Card Services works best when it stays centered on card-related support, such as declined transactions, statements, bill payments, fee explanations, and waiver requests.

Best kept in Card Services

  • Card declines and failed card transactions
  • Annual fee, late fee, and interest-related questions
  • Card statements and card bill payments
  • Card-specific support and troubleshooting

May belong elsewhere

  • Deposit account benefits and bonus interest
  • Transfers and transfer delays
  • PayNow account-linking questions
  • Digital banking authentication support

Recommended category principle

Keep Card Services tightly focused on card-related customer support. Where a prompt is primarily about transfers, account benefits, or digital banking authentication, it should either be placed elsewhere or intentionally supported as part of a broader servicing experience.

Prompts to Retain

The category already includes a strong core of practical card-servicing prompts. These should remain unchanged.

Retain recommendation

Keep the existing core card-service prompts that are already clear, specific, and aligned to card support. The main value comes from refining the smaller set of overlapping or out-of-scope prompts rather than redesigning the category.

Merge Overlapping Prompts

The clearest overlap appears in waiver-related intents and card-decline wording. These should be consolidated into stronger canonical prompts.

Annual Fee Waiver

Before
How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB?
Before
How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?
Before
Will UOB waive my annual fee if I ask?
After
How do I request an annual fee waiver for my UOB credit card?
These prompts all lead to the same core servicing need and should be handled under one canonical intent.

Interest Waiver

Before
Can UOB waive the interest on my credit card?
Before
How do I waive interest charges on my UOB credit card?
After
Can I request a waiver of interest charges on my UOB credit card?
Both prompts ask for the same outcome and should be consolidated.

Late Fee Waiver

Before
How do I waive my UOB late payment fee?
Before
How do I waive UOB late charges?
After
Can I request a waiver of my UOB late payment fee?
This is a single fee-waiver intent expressed in two ways.

Card Decline

Before
Why was my UOB credit card declined or why did my transaction fail?
Before
My UOB card was declined — could it be due to my credit limit or daily limit?
After
Why was my UOB card transaction declined?
This creates one cleaner entry point for card-decline troubleshooting.

Rewrite Broader or Multi-Part Prompts

A small number of prompts would work better if they were simplified into a single clear customer need.

Before
What are the charges on my UOB statement — annual fee, late fee, or interest?
Combines multiple charge types into one question.
After
Why are there fees or interest charges on my UOB card statement?
Simplifies the question while keeping the same customer need.
Before
How do I pay my UOB credit card bill — AXS, bank transfer, or TMRW app?
Hardcodes answer options into the prompt itself.
After
How can I pay my UOB credit card bill?
Cleaner phrasing lets the answer present the available payment methods.
Before
Why was my UOB bill payment or fund transfer declined?
Combines card bill payment and transfer support into one question.
After
Why was my UOB credit card bill payment declined?
Keeps the prompt aligned to Card Services.
Before
I set up the UOB digital token but I still cannot make transfers or payments
Statement-style prompt mixing transfers and payments.
After
I set up my UOB digital token but still cannot complete a payment
Makes the wording more focused, though this area may still need category review later.
Before
I transferred money using UOB but the recipient has not received it yet
This is clearer as a customer issue than as a statement.
After
Why has the recipient not received my transfer yet?
Improves wording, though the prompt still feels closer to transfer support than card servicing.

Prompts to Move Out of Card Services

These prompts sit clearly outside the scope of card servicing and are better placed in an account or deposit-related category.

Prompt Recommended destination Reason
Does my salary credit into UOB ONE Account qualify for bonus interest? Account benefits / deposit account servicing This is an account-benefit eligibility question, not a card-servicing question.
Why hasn't my UOB ONE Account bonus interest been credited this month? Account benefits / deposit account servicing This is an interest-crediting issue related to an account rather than a card.

Prompts to Review Carefully

A small number of prompts could be valid, but they feel closer to transfer support, PayNow support, or digital banking authentication than to core Card Services.

Prompt Suggested handling Reason
Why is my UOB transfer blocked or on hold? Review for category fit Reads more like transfer or security support than card servicing.
Which UOB account is linked to my PayNow and which account receives transfers? Review for category fit Feels more naturally aligned to PayNow and account-routing support.
What is the difference between my UOB digital token PIN, SMS OTP, and card PIN? Review for category fit Crosses card usage with digital banking authentication support.

Conclusion

The Card Services prompt set is already in a good place. The most valuable improvements are focused and practical: keep the strong service core, merge overlapping waiver intents, simplify a small number of broad prompts, and tighten category boundaries.

With those changes, the experience becomes clearer, easier to maintain, and more consistent as a customer-facing card-support journey.
