LLMO Demo Framework by Dashboard

This structure is designed for live demos. Each dashboard has a clear role in the story, so the audience understands not only what the feature shows, but what decision it helps make.

Visibility Scorecard Prompt-Level Evidence Agent Readiness URL-Level Citation Truth Human Traffic & Outcomes
Core framing: Brand Presence tells you whether the brand is showing up in AI answers. Data Insights tells you whether those answers are strong, current, accurate, and grounded in the right source. Agentic Traffic tells you whether AI systems can reach and read the site. URL Inspector tells you which exact pages are winning, being evaluated, or losing citations. Referral Traffic tells you whether that visibility turns into human visits and measurable value.

1. Brand Presence

Use this as the executive scorecard for AI visibility, trend movement, and market standing.

Primary role: visibility scorecard

Role in the story

Brand Presence is the fastest way to establish the baseline. It shows whether the brand appears in AI responses, how that is changing over time, and how the brand compares with others in the same answer space.

What makes the score meaningful

The visibility score is only as strong as the prompt set behind it. To make this view meaningful, the prompt set should cover branded and non-branded prompts, span the customer journey, and include different intents rather than only obvious brand questions.

1

Filters + top-line metrics

Where to navigate

Open Brand Presence and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Visibility Score
  • Brand Mentions
  • Citations
  • Trend movement versus the prior period
Why demo it

It answers the first executive question: "Are we visible in AI answers at all?"

2

Others comparison

Where to navigate

Move to the comparison area on the same dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Weekly mentions comparison
  • Weekly citations comparison
  • Whether the brand is gaining or losing ground
Why demo it

Shows whether visibility is strong relative to other brands, not only in isolation.

3

Sentiment Trend Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to Sentiment Trend Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Positive / neutral / negative distribution
  • Notable changes over time
  • Whether visibility is helping or hurting perception
Why demo it

It adds the quality-of-representation layer to the visibility story.

4

Share of Voice

Where to navigate

Use the Share of Voice view where it is available in the environment.

What to highlight
  • Where the brand leads
  • Where other brands dominate
  • Topic-level competitive standing
Why demo it

It is the cleanest topic-level summary of whether the brand is actually owning the conversation.

Suggested talk track

"I start with Brand Presence because it is the scorecard. It tells us whether the brand is showing up in AI answers, how that is trending, and how we compare with the rest of the market. But I also remind people that the score is only meaningful if the prompt set reflects the real questions that matter."

2. Data Insights

Use this as the prompt-level inspection layer behind Brand Presence for answer quality, source quality, and citation diagnosis.

Primary role: answer-quality check

Role in the story

This is where the story moves from aggregate metrics to prompt-level evidence you can read. Instead of only looking at mentions and citations in the abstract, you can inspect what the model actually returned, how the brand showed up inside the answer, and what source the answer relied on.

What good mentions look like

Through Prompt drill-down, you can validate whether mentions are:

  • Up to date — reflects current content
  • Accurate — facts are right, not partial
  • Complete — nothing important missing
  • Brand-aligned — on-message framing
1

Filters + table scope

Where to navigate

From Brand Presence, move to the Data Insights table.

What to highlight
  • Topic-level versus prompt-level analysis
  • Region / market context
  • Visibility Score, Mentions, Sentiment, Position
  • All Citations versus Owned Citations
Why demo it

This is where the scorecard becomes an inspection layer.

2

What each row lets you inspect

What to look for
  • The exact prompt executed
  • The answer the AI returned
  • How the brand was framed
  • Which sources it relied on
Why demo it

It makes the analysis concrete. You are no longer inferring quality from aggregates alone — you can inspect the actual evidence.

3

Prompt drill-down

Where to navigate

Open the prompt details view from a topic or row that deserves closer inspection.

What to highlight
  • Prompt-level metrics and trends
  • The actual AI response
  • Categorized citations tied to the prompt
Why demo it

This is the clearest proof point in the flow: you can see not only that the brand appeared, but exactly how the answer was built.

4

High-popularity, low-visibility topics

Where to navigate

Sort or filter for high Popularity with weaker Visibility Score.

What to highlight
  • Popularity
  • Visibility Score
  • Mentions
  • Position
Why demo it

It shows where demand exists but brand presence is still underperforming.

5

All Citations vs Owned Citations

Where to navigate

Look for rows where All Citations are healthy but Owned Citations are weak.

What to highlight
  • Topics where the answer is well sourced overall
  • Cases where authority is going to third-party pages instead of owned pages
  • Cases where you are cited, but the answer points to the wrong owned page
Why demo it

This is the clearest source-diagnosis story: the answer may look grounded, but the grounding is still going to the wrong place.

6

Answer-quality validation

What to validate
  • Is the answer current, or stale?
  • Is it well grounded, or weakly grounded?
  • Is it sourced from the right page?
  • Is the representation on-message?
Why demo it

Strong metrics do not guarantee strong answers. This is where visibility becomes an answer-quality check.

Why it matters

Strong metrics don't guarantee strong answers. A mention can be stale, weakly grounded, cited to someone else, or cited to the wrong owned page — this is where the scorecard becomes an answer-quality and source-quality check.

Suggested talk track

"Brand Presence gives us the scorecard, but Data Insights lets us read the actual evidence. We can inspect the exact prompt, the answer the model returned, how the brand was framed, and which sources it relied on. Prompt drill-down is where we validate whether the mention is current, accurate, complete, and on-message."

3. Agentic Traffic

Use this as the agent-readiness layer for access, reliability, performance, and content visibility.

Primary role: can agents reach and use the site?

Role in the story

Pages cannot be cited if agents cannot reliably access and interpret them. Agentic Traffic shows whether AI systems are reaching the site, succeeding on requests, and concentrating on the pages that matter.

Why this matters in a demo

Most companies cannot see this in traditional web analytics. LLM Optimizer surfaces true AI agent traffic through CDN log forwarding, which is why this view is one of the clearest proofs that AI systems are already interacting with the site.

1

Filters + top-line metrics + traffic distribution

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Agentic Traffic and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Agentic Interactions
  • Success Rate
  • Average TTFB
  • Content Visibility
  • Hits by Market / Category / Page Type
Why demo it

It establishes whether AI systems are reaching the site and where attention is concentrated.

2

Content Visibility

Where to navigate

Stay on Agentic Traffic and use the content visibility view, then connect it to page-level analysis lower in the dashboard where available.

What to highlight
  • Whether agents can actually read the content
  • Pages with high demand but weaker visibility
  • Cases where being fetched is not the same as being usable for citation
Why demo it

This is the clearest readiness story: AI traffic alone does not mean the content is readable enough to win citations.

3

User Agent Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to User Agent Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Breakdown by user agent
  • Agent type differences
  • Chatbots versus crawlers versus research agents
Why demo it

It shows that not all AI traffic is the same, and not every agent matters for the same reason.

4

URL Performance Analysis + movers

Where to navigate

Use Top Movers / Bottom Movers, then move into URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Rising and falling URLs
  • Total Hits
  • Unique Agents
  • Top Agent
  • Success Rate
Why demo it

It is the fastest way to identify which pages deserve investigation or optimization.

Suggested talk track

"Agentic Traffic shows whether AI systems are actually reaching the site, but the bigger question is whether they can use what they find. Most companies cannot see this in traditional analytics at all, which is why the CDN-log view is so powerful."

4. URL Inspector

Use this as the page-level citation layer for exact URL performance, citation attempts, and missed opportunity.

Primary role: exact URL and citation performance

Role in the story

URL Inspector is where the story becomes concrete. It shows which exact URLs are being cited, which pages are being evaluated, and which outside URLs are winning authority in the same answer space.

Key pattern to call out

High citation attempts, low citations is one of the best opportunities to surface: models are clearly evaluating the page, but the page is not converting that interest into citations.

1

Filters + overview metrics + trend charts

Where to navigate

Open Domain → URL Inspector and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Unique prompts with owned citations
  • Total unique prompts
  • Unique cited URLs
  • Total times cited
  • Citation Attempts and Referral Hits trends
Why demo it

It is the best URL-level summary before you drill into specific pages.

2

High attempts, low citations

Where to navigate

In Your Cited URLs, find pages with high Citation Attempts and weaker Times Cited, then open details.

What to highlight
  • Citation Attempts
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
Why demo it

It is the strongest URL-level opportunity story: the page is being considered, but it is not winning enough citations.

3

Your Cited URLs + URL Details drill-down

Where to navigate

Go to Your Cited URLs, choose a strong owned page, and open URL Details.

What to highlight
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
  • Agentic Hits
  • Referral Hits
  • Sentiment, trend movement, and prompt analysis in details
Why demo it

It is the clearest bridge between visibility, citation performance, and traffic outcome for a single page.

4

Cited Third-Party URLs + Cited Domains

Where to navigate

Move to Cited Third-Party URLs and Cited Domains.

What to highlight
  • Which outside URLs are winning
  • Which domains dominate the citation space
  • Where third-party authority is outranking owned authority
Why demo it

It gives you the cleanest competitive authority story at both the page and domain level.

Suggested talk track

"If Brand Presence is the scorecard and Data Insights is the inspection layer, URL Inspector is the page-level citation truth. This is where we stop talking in aggregates and start talking about exact URLs, exact demand, and exact opportunity."

5. Referral Traffic

Use this as the human-traffic layer, with Business Impact as the downstream outcome view when analytics is connected.

Primary role: human visits and measurable outcomes

Role in the story

Referral Traffic is about human users who clicked citation links in AI-generated answers. This is where AI discovery gets validated as a real acquisition and engagement channel rather than just a visibility signal.

How to separate the two tabs

Traffic Insights shows the referral behavior itself — sources, landing pages, and engagement signals. Business Impact extends that story into downstream performance when Adobe Analytics is connected.

1

Filters + top-line metrics + referrer breakdown

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Referral Traffic and stay on Traffic Insights.

What to highlight
  • Total Referral Traffic from LLMs
  • Consent Rate
  • Bounce Rate
  • Hits By Referrer
  • Hits By Market / Page Intent
Why demo it

It is the best opener for proving that AI-generated answers are sending real human traffic.

2

Referral Sources Details

Where to navigate

Stay on Traffic Insights and move to Referral Sources Details.

What to highlight
  • Source
  • Visits
  • Bounce Rate
  • Channel
Why demo it

It is the clearest source-quality comparison beyond the chart summary.

3

URL Performance Analysis + URL details

Where to navigate

Scroll to URL Performance Analysis, then open a page's details view if available.

What to highlight
  • URL
  • Traffic
  • LLM Traffic
  • Bounce Rate
  • Consent Rate
  • Page Intent
Why demo it

It is the best landing-page story and the clearest place to inspect whether AI-referred traffic actually engages.

4

Business Impact

Where to navigate

Switch from Traffic Insights to Business Impact when analytics is connected.

What to highlight
  • Page Views
  • Entries
  • Revenue
  • Conversion Rate
  • Avg. Session Duration
  • Pages per Visit
  • Total Orders
Why demo it

It is the strongest executive close: it answers whether AI-driven discovery is commercially meaningful.

Suggested talk track

"Referral Traffic is the human-traffic layer. It shows whether people are actually clicking citation links in AI answers, which sources send the best traffic, and which landing pages perform. Business Impact then extends that into outcomes when analytics is connected."

Recommended full-demo flow

This sequence tells the cleanest story from visibility to measurable impact.

Step 1

Brand Presence

Are we showing up?

Start with visibility, trend movement, sentiment, and market comparison.

Step 2

Data Insights

Are those good answers?

Read the prompt-level evidence, validate answer quality, and diagnose source gaps.

Step 3

Agentic Traffic

Can agents reach and use the site?

Show access, success, speed, and content visibility.

Step 4

URL Inspector

Which exact pages win or miss?

Move from aggregate performance to exact URL-level citation truth.

Step 5

Referral Traffic

Did it create human and business impact?

Close on human traffic first, then downstream impact where available.

Natural next step after the five dashboards

Once the demo has established visibility, answer quality, site readiness, page-level opportunity, and human impact, the most natural handoff is into optimization opportunities and what to fix next.
