LLMO Demo Framework

Use this flow for a clean story: Brand Presence shows visibility, Data Insights explains the score, Agentic Traffic shows whether agents can access and read the site, URL Inspector shows which URLs win citations, and Referral Traffic shows whether that turns into human and business value.

Quick framing: Start with visibility, drill into topics and prompts, confirm AI-agent access, inspect exact URLs, then close on real traffic and business impact.

1. Brand Presence

Use this as the visibility scorecard for brand presence and market standing in AI answers.

Primary role: scorecard

What it does

Brand Presence is the top-level readout. It tells you whether the brand is showing up, how that is trending, and how you compare in market.

What it does not do

It is not the detailed topic or prompt analysis view. For that, continue into Data Insights within Brand Presence.

1

Overview metrics

Where to navigate

Open Brand Presence and stay at the top.

What to highlight
  • Visibility Score
  • Brand Mentions
  • Citations
  • Trend vs prior period
Why demo it

Fastest answer to: "Are we visible in AI answers?"

2

Market Tracking

Where to navigate

Move to Market Tracking.

What to highlight
  • Weekly mentions comparison
  • Weekly citations comparison
  • Whether you are gaining or losing ground
Why demo it

Shows whether performance is strong relative to the market.

3

Sentiment Trend Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to Sentiment Trend Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Positive / neutral / negative trend
  • Meaningful perception changes
Why demo it

Visibility matters, but so does how the brand is represented.

4

Share of Voice

Where to navigate

Go to Share of Voice.

What to highlight
  • Where the brand leads
  • Where competitors lead
  • Topic-level standing
Why demo it

Best topic-level view of whether the brand owns the conversation.

Suggested talk track

"I start with Brand Presence because it is the scorecard. It shows whether we are visible in AI answers, how that is trending, and how we compare in market."

2. Brand Presence drill-down: Data Insights

Use this as the topic and prompt analysis layer inside Brand Presence.

Primary role: drill-down

What it does

Data Insights explains the score. It breaks Brand Presence into the topics and prompts behind the top-line metrics.

What to look for

Strong demand with weak visibility, and strong citation activity where Owned Citations are still low.

1

Data Insights table

Where to navigate

Stay in Brand Presence and move to the Data Insights table.

What to highlight
  • Popularity
  • Visibility Score
  • Mentions
  • Sentiment
  • Position
  • All Citations
  • Owned Citations
Why demo it

This is where the scorecard becomes actionable.

2

High-popularity, low-visibility topics

Where to navigate

Sort for high Popularity and weaker Visibility Score.

What to highlight
  • Demand is present
  • Brand visibility is lagging
Why demo it

Best quick opportunity story.

3

All Citations vs Owned Citations

Where to navigate

Find rows with strong All Citations but weak Owned Citations.

What to highlight
  • Active answer space
  • Citations going elsewhere
Why demo it

Strong authority-gap example.

4

Prompt drill-down

Where to navigate

Click from a topic into a specific prompt.

What to highlight
  • Position
  • Sentiment
  • All Citations
  • Owned Citations
Why demo it

Turns the score into a concrete prompt-level opportunity.

Suggested talk track

"Data Insights is the Brand Presence drill-down. It shows which topics and prompts are driving the score, where we are underrepresented, and where citations are going to someone else."

3. Agentic Traffic

Use this as the AI-agent access and machine-readability layer.

Primary role: access and readability

What it does

Agentic Traffic shows whether AI agents can reach and use your content. It combines access, success, speed, and content visibility signals.

What it does not do

It is not the citation-landscape view. Use URL Inspector for citations and Opportunities for fixes.

1

Filters + top metrics

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Agentic Traffic and stay at the top.

What to highlight
  • Site, Date Range, Category, Platform, Agent Type, User Agent, Success Rate, Content Type
  • Agentic interactions
  • Success rate
  • Average TTFB
  • Content Visibility
Why demo it

Fastest technical read on whether agents can access and use the site.

2

Traffic distribution + response codes

Where to navigate

Stay in the chart area.

What to highlight
  • Hits By Market
  • Hits by Category
  • Hits by Page Type
  • Response Code Breakdown
Why demo it

Shows where AI attention is going and whether errors or redirects are getting in the way.

3

Top Movers / Bottom Movers + User Agent Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to Top Movers, Bottom Movers, and User Agent Analysis.

What to highlight
  • URLs gaining or losing agentic traffic
  • Crawler vs chatbot mix
  • Which page types attract which agent types
Why demo it

Shows whether the right AI traffic is reaching the right content.

4

URL Performance Analysis

Where to navigate

Move into URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Total Hits
  • Unique Agents
  • Top Agent
  • Top Agent Type
  • Success Rate
  • Category
Why demo it

Best page-level technical handoff into Opportunities or URL Inspector.

Suggested talk track

"Agentic Traffic tells us whether AI agents can reach, succeed on, and read the site. Content Visibility is the bridge between raw bot traffic and real machine readability."

4. URL Inspector

Use this as the citation landscape and URL citation-performance layer.

Primary role: citation performance

What it does

URL Inspector shows which exact URLs are winning citations. It covers owned URLs, third-party URLs, and domain-level authority.

What it does not do

These tables read the citation landscape, not page internals. For content visibility and fixes, pair this with Agentic Traffic and Opportunities.

1

Overview metrics + trends

Where to navigate

Open Domain → URL Inspector and stay at the top.

What to highlight
  • Unique prompts with owned citations
  • Total unique prompts
  • Unique cited URLs
  • Total times cited
  • Citation Attempts and Referral Hits from LLMs trends
Why demo it

Best URL-level summary before drilling into winners and misses.

2

High Citation Attempts, low Times Cited

Where to navigate

In Your Cited URLs, look for high Citation Attempts and weaker Times Cited.

What to highlight
  • Citation Attempts
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
Why demo it

Best citation-conversion opportunity story.

3

Your Cited URLs + URL Details

Where to navigate

Open Your Cited URLs, choose a page, then open URL Details.

What to highlight
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
  • Agentic Hits
  • Referral Hits
  • Citation Trends and Prompt Analysis
Why demo it

Best owned-page citation story.

4

Cited Third Party URLs + Cited Domains

Where to navigate

Move to Cited Third Party URLs and Cited Domains.

What to highlight
  • Which outside URLs are winning
  • Which domains dominate
  • Owned vs earned vs competitor mix
Why demo it

Best competitive authority view.

Suggested talk track

"URL Inspector is the citation landscape view. It shows which exact owned URLs are winning, where citation demand exists but conversion is weak, and which outside pages are taking authority."

5. Referral Traffic

Use this as the human clickthrough and business-impact layer.

Primary role: human outcomes

What it does

Referral Traffic measures what happens after AI citations drive clicks. It shows referrers, landing-page performance, and downstream impact.

What it does not do

It is not a citation-generation or page-internals view. Use Brand Presence, URL Inspector, Agentic Traffic, and Opportunities for those questions.

1

Traffic Insights metrics

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Referral Traffic and stay on Traffic Insights.

What to highlight
  • Total Referral Traffic from LLMs
  • Consent Rate
  • Bounce Rate
  • Hits By Referrer
Why demo it

Fastest proof that AI citations are sending real human traffic.

2

Referral Sources Details

Where to navigate

Scroll to Referral Sources Details.

What to highlight
  • Source
  • Visits
  • Bounce Rate
  • Channel
Why demo it

Best source-quality comparison.

3

URL Performance Analysis

Where to navigate

Move to URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight
  • URL
  • Traffic
  • LLM Traffic
  • Bounce Rate
  • Consent Rate
  • Page Intent
Why demo it

Best landing-page performance view.

4

Business Impact

Where to navigate

Switch to Business Impact if Adobe Analytics or GA4 is connected.

What to highlight
  • Page Views
  • Entries
  • Revenue
  • Conversion Rate
  • Avg. Session Duration
  • Pages per Visit
  • Total Orders
Why demo it

Best executive close on commercial value.

Suggested talk track

"Referral Traffic is the human outcome layer. It shows whether AI citations are sending real visitors, which referrers and landing pages perform best, and—when analytics is connected—whether that traffic drives business results."

Recommended demo flow

This sequence tells the cleanest story from visibility to value.

Step 1

Brand Presence

Are we visible?

Start with scorecard metrics, market, sentiment, and Share of Voice.

Step 2

Data Insights

Which topics and prompts explain that score?

Drill into what is driving visibility and where gaps exist.

Step 3

Agentic Traffic

Can agents reach and read the site?

Show access, success, Content Visibility, and response-code patterns.

Step 4

URL Inspector

Which URLs win citations?

Move from broad performance to exact URL-level citation outcomes.

Step 5

Referral Traffic

Did that create real outcomes?

Close on human traffic, landing-page performance, and business impact.
