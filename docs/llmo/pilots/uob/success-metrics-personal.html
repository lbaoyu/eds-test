Trial Success Metrics Recommendation

Recommended Success Metrics for the 100-Prompt LLMO Trial

Based on the audited prompt bank, the most useful trial metrics should measure more than overall visibility alone. This prompt set is strong in comparisons, interest optimization, and product-level depth, but it is also branded-heavy and uneven across journey stages. Success metrics should therefore separate overall performance, discovery performance, and coverage quality so the trial produces learning that is reliable and actionable.

Prompt Set Size
100 prompts
Topic Structure
5 topics, 20 prompts each
Branded Mix
80 branded / 20 unbranded
Best Use of Trial
Baseline, learning, and targeted optimization
Key Principle
Do not judge success on one blended score alone
Executive Summary

How success should be defined for this trial

For this prompt bank, trial success should be defined in three layers:

Layer 1

Visibility performance

How often the brand appears, and how strongly it appears, across the 100 prompts.

Layer 2

Coverage quality

Whether performance is balanced across topics, unbranded discovery, comparisons, and customer journey stages.

Layer 3

Improvement value

Whether the trial gives clear signals about what content, product pages, or messaging should be improved next.

Recommended framing: The trial should not be judged only by "how often the brand appears overall." It should also be judged by where it appears, where it does not, and whether the pattern is useful enough to guide action.
Top Recommendation

Recommended primary success metrics

Primary Metric 1

Overall Prompt Visibility Rate

Measure the percentage of the 100 prompts where the brand appears meaningfully in the response set.

  • Why it matters: gives the clearest top-level baseline for the trial
  • How to use it: track as the headline number, but never use it alone
  • Recommended interpretation: use it as the overall health signal, then break it down by topic and prompt type
Primary Metric 2

Unbranded Prompt Visibility Rate

Measure visibility separately for the 20 unbranded prompts.

  • Why it matters: this is the clearest signal for discovery and category-level competitiveness
  • Why especially important here: the uploaded bank is heavily branded, so unbranded visibility should be protected as a standalone metric
  • Recommended interpretation: treat this as the most important discovery metric in the trial
Primary Metric 3

Branded Prompt Visibility Rate

Measure visibility separately for the 80 branded prompts.

  • Why it matters: shows how well the brand performs when the customer already has brand intent
  • What it tells you: product findability, brand-specific relevance, and support-oriented presence
  • Recommended interpretation: use this as the product-depth and branded-support benchmark
Primary Metric 4

Topic-Level Visibility Consistency

Measure performance across the five topics rather than only across the total bank.

  • Why it matters: a strong average can hide weak topic pockets
  • What good looks like: no major topic underperforms badly relative to the rest
  • Recommended interpretation: use this to prioritize which topics deserve the first optimization actions
Recommendation: If only four metrics can be used in the executive scorecard, use these four first.
Secondary Metrics

Recommended secondary success metrics

Secondary Metric 1

Top-Position Presence Rate

Track not only whether the brand appears, but how often it appears near the top of the response set.

Secondary Metric 2

Comparison Prompt Win Rate

For prompts that compare UOB against named alternatives, track how often the brand is represented favorably or prominently.

Secondary Metric 3

Journey Stage Coverage Rate

Track performance separately across awareness, consideration, decision, activation, and use/support prompts.

Secondary Metric 4

Topic Improvement Rate Over Time

Measure whether weak topics improve after content or messaging updates are made.

Secondary Metric 5

Response Quality / Relevance Review

Add a human review layer for whether appearances are genuinely useful, not just nominal mentions.

Secondary Metric 6

Competitive Share in Alternative Prompts

Track how often competitor brands dominate the comparison and alternative prompts.

Illustrative Targets

How to think about targets in a limited trial

In a trial, targets should be used as directional success bands, not as rigid commitments. The first goal is to establish a trustworthy baseline and identify where improvement actions should focus.

Metric Healthy sign Warning sign
Overall Prompt Visibility Rate Steady baseline with visible improvement over time High volatility or no improvement after optimization work
Unbranded Prompt Visibility Rate Gradual improvement and reduced gap versus branded prompts Persistently weak unbranded performance while branded performance looks strong
Branded Prompt Visibility Rate Strong and stable presence on brand-led prompts Low visibility even when the brand is already in the prompt
Topic-Level Consistency No topic is materially lagging the rest One or two topics remain far weaker than the bank average
Comparison Prompt Win Rate Brand remains visible and competitive in head-to-head prompts Competitors dominate comparison prompts consistently
Journey Stage Coverage Reasonable representation beyond discovery and comparison Performance is good only in one part of the journey
Practical target-setting approach: Set the first reporting cycle as the baseline, then track improvement direction, gap reduction, and topic stabilization rather than aiming for a single absolute score too early.

Conclusion

Final recommendation

For this 100-prompt bank, the most important success metric is not a single blended visibility score. The right success model is a structured scorecard that separates overall presence, unbranded discovery, branded product performance, and topic consistency.

That approach fits this bank's actual shape, protects against misleading averages, and gives the trial the best chance of producing insights that are both credible and actionable.
