The recommendation

Five focused accelerators, packaged into three stages

Rather than a flat menu, the portfolio is five focused accelerators — three must-haves every customer should run, plus two high-priority accelerators that extend the programme for enterprise and live customers.

For positioning, they roll up into three stages: Launch establishes the baseline and readiness, Prove Value ties visibility to business impact, and Scale builds durable governance and recurring optimisation.

The five accelerators

From day-one baseline to durable, scaled adoption

Each accelerator is a short, outcome-driven engagement with a clear owner and a defined set of deliverables. Together they move the customer from measuring the right things, to acting on what the platform surfaces, to proving and sustaining value.

01
Must-have Stage 1 · Launch

AI Visibility Baseline & Prompt Strategy

Primary outcome · Establish the measurement model and the first optimisation backlog.

Best for · Every new customer

What it covers

  • Define the customer's AI visibility goals
  • Build the prompt strategy across branded, non-branded, category, comparison, and high-intent prompts
  • Identify topic clusters and the competitor set
  • Establish the initial baseline and prioritise first opportunities

Sample activities

  • Goals and AI-discovery alignment workshop
  • Prompt and topic mapping session
  • Competitor benchmarking setup
  • Baseline review; first-backlog prioritisation

Deliverables

  • Prompt strategy framework
  • Topic and competitor map
  • Baseline measurement scorecard
  • Prioritised optimisation backlog
  • 30 / 60 / 90-day action plan
Customer value — Faster time to value, meaningful measurement from day one, and shared alignment on what success looks like.
02
Must-have Stage 1 · Launch

GEO Content & Technical Readiness

Primary outcome · Surface and prioritise crawlability, structure, and content fixes.

Best for · Customers with web / content teams

What it covers

  • Review content and page types most relevant to AI discovery
  • Identify crawlability and accessibility issues affecting AI agents
  • Evaluate structure, summaries, FAQs, and schema
  • Prioritise technical, on-site, and off-site improvements

Sample activities

  • Current-state content review
  • AI-agent readiness assessment
  • Opportunity review using product insights
  • Prioritisation workshop with web, SEO, content

Deliverables

  • Content and technical readiness assessment
  • Prioritised recommendations by page type
  • Optimisation brief for content and web teams
  • Implementation roadmap or sprint plan
  • Risk and dependency summary
Customer value — A clear view of what blocks stronger AI visibility, better cross-team prioritisation, and faster movement from insight to action.
03
Must-have Stage 2 · Prove Value

Value Measurement & Executive Scorecard

Primary outcome · Tie visibility improvement to traffic, engagement, and revenue proxies.

Best for · Any customer asking for ROI

What it covers

  • Define the framework across visibility, traffic, engagement, and outcomes
  • Separate leading indicators from business-outcome indicators
  • Build an executive-ready scorecard and narrative
  • Align on reporting cadence and KPI ownership

Sample activities

  • KPI alignment workshop
  • Current reporting review
  • Scorecard design session
  • Attribution and outcome mapping; readout prep

Deliverables

  • Executive scorecard template
  • KPI framework and glossary
  • Reporting cadence and governance plan
  • QBR-ready business impact narrative
  • Measurement-maturity recommendations
Customer value — Stronger executive alignment, clearer linkage to business priorities, and easier renewal and expansion conversations.
04
High priority Stage 3 · Scale

Operating Model & Governance for AI Discovery

Primary outcome · Create durable ownership across SEO, content, PR / comms, analytics, and web.

Best for · Enterprise / global customers

What it covers

  • Define ownership across the functions involved in AI visibility
  • Clarify who manages prompts, content, measurement, escalation, reporting
  • Establish governance for optimisation reviews
  • Support enterprise-scale alignment across regions or brands

Sample activities

  • Cross-functional stakeholder workshop
  • Current-state ownership and workflow review
  • RACI development session
  • Governance and cadence design

Deliverables

  • Cross-functional RACI
  • Governance framework
  • Review cadence and meeting structure
  • Escalation and approval model
  • Recommended operating model
Customer value — Less ambiguity, faster decisions, and greater consistency for enterprise and global programmes.
05
High priority Stage 3 · Scale

Optimisation Coaching Sprints

Primary outcome · Turn ABV insights and recommendations into recurring optimisation actions and measurable progress.

Best for · Customers already live

What it covers

  • Review ABV dashboard insights and product-generated opportunities on a recurring cadence
  • Prioritise the highest-value actions across visibility, content, technical readiness, and traffic impact
  • Help teams decide what to act on now, monitor, or defer
  • Coach teams on building a repeatable optimisation rhythm after go-live
  • Track progress against agreed KPIs to demonstrate ongoing value

Sample activities

  • Review recent findings across visibility, prompt and topic performance, opportunities, and agentic / referral traffic
  • Prioritise the next optimisation actions by impact and effort
  • Align owners across SEO, content, analytics, and web
  • Check progress on previously recommended actions
  • Capture outcomes, lessons, and next-step priorities

Deliverables

  • Sprint agenda and review framework
  • Prioritised optimisation action plan
  • Ownership tracker for agreed actions
  • Progress readout against visibility, traffic, and impact KPIs
  • Next-sprint recommendations
Customer value — Stronger post-go-live adoption, clearer prioritisation of what to do next, better use of ABV insights, a repeatable optimisation cadence instead of one-time setup, and ongoing evidence of progress for stakeholder reviews.
