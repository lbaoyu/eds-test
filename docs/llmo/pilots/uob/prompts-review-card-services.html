Main Opportunity

The current topic structure may be a little more detailed than necessary for a trial-sized prompt set. With 24 topics for 39 prompts, many topics contain only one prompt.

Potential impact What this can lead to
Less intuitive navigation Users may need to scan many narrow topic names instead of a smaller set of clearer buckets.
Lighter topic-level insight Topics with only one prompt provide less useful signal for reviewing coverage or performance by topic.
More maintenance over time As new prompts are added, a very detailed taxonomy can become harder to keep consistent.
This is not a prompt-quality issue. The prompts themselves are mostly strong. The suggestion is simply to consider a slightly broader topic structure for easier trial management.

Prompt Recommendations

Merge Annual fee waiver process

How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB? How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?

These are very close in intent and could be combined into one canonical prompt.

How do I request a UOB credit card annual fee waiver?

Merge Late fee waiver process

How do I waive my UOB late payment fee? How do I waive UOB late charges?

These are also very similar and could be consolidated.

How do I request a waiver for my UOB late payment fee?
Beyond these small overlaps, the rest of the set is best left largely unchanged for the trial.

Topic Structure Suggestion

A lighter structure could make the trial easier to review and expand later. Rather than keeping many narrow top-level topics, a smaller set of broader categories could be used, with specific distinctions handled as subtopics or tags.

Current pattern Simpler trial-friendly approach
Separate waiver-related topics Use a broader Waivers topic with subtopics for annual fee, interest, and late fee
Multiple narrow payment topics Use a broader Payments topic with subtopics for due date, channels, charges, and statements
Multiple narrow transfer topics Use a broader Transfers topic with subtopics for PayNow, pending, and security hold

Recommended Trial Shape

Cancellation Payments Waivers Transactions Transfers Self Service Rewards Promotion Eligibility

This would keep the set well within the 50-prompt trial limit, preserve the useful prompt coverage already present, and create a slightly cleaner framework for testing and future expansion.

Final Recommendation

Keep most prompts unchanged Merge only the clearest duplicates Consider a simpler topic structure for the trial

For the trial phase, the best approach is selective refinement rather than broad change: retain the current prompt set, merge the few clearly overlapping prompts, and consider modest topic consolidation so the library is easier to use and assess.
