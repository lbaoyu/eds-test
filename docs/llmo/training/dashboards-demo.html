Adobe LLM Optimizer

Demo Framework by Dashboard

This version sharpens the story for all five dashboards so each one has a clear job in the demo. The goal is not just to show features, but to show how each screen answers a specific business question.

Brand Visibility Answer Quality Agent Readiness URL-Level Truth Human Traffic & Impact
Recommended overall story: Brand Presence tells you whether you show up. Data Insights tells you whether those are actually good answers. Agentic Traffic tells you whether AI systems can access and read your content. URL Inspector tells you which exact pages are winning or missing citations. Referral Traffic tells you whether any of that turns into real human traffic and business value.

1. Brand Presence

Use this as the scorecard and market narrative.

Primary role: visibility scorecard

Sharpened message

Brand Presence is the executive summary of AI visibility. It tells you whether the brand is appearing in AI answers, how that is trending, and how it compares to the market.

What it is not

It is not the place to validate answer quality at a granular level. A strong score here is useful, but it still needs to be checked in Data Insights.

1

Filters + top-line metrics

Where to navigate

Open Brand Presence and stay at the top of the page.

What to highlight

  • Visibility Score
  • Brand Mentions
  • Citations
  • Trend movement versus prior period

Why demo it

Fastest answer to: "Are we showing up in AI answers at all?"

2

Market tracking

Where to navigate

Stay on Brand Presence and move to the competitor or market comparison area.

What to highlight

  • Weekly mentions comparison
  • Weekly citations comparison
  • Whether the brand is gaining or losing ground

Why demo it

Shows whether visibility is good relative to competitors, not just in isolation.

3

Sentiment Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to Sentiment Trend Analysis.

What to highlight

  • Positive / neutral / negative distribution
  • Trend direction over time

Why demo it

Visibility matters, but quality of representation matters too.

4

Share of Voice

Where to navigate

Go to the Share of Voice view on the same dashboard.

What to highlight

  • Where the brand leads
  • Where competitors dominate
  • Topic-level competitive standing

Why demo it

Best topic-level summary of whether the brand truly owns the conversation.

Suggested talk track

"I start with Brand Presence because it is the scorecard. It tells us whether the brand is visible in AI answers, how that is trending, and whether we are winning or losing versus competitors. Then sentiment and share of voice help show whether that presence is strong and strategically meaningful."

2. Data Insights

Use this as the answer-quality and opportunity-analysis layer.

Primary role: answer-quality check

Sharpened message

Strong metrics do not guarantee strong answers. A mention can be stale, weakly grounded, or sourced from the wrong page. Data Insights is where the scorecard becomes an answer-quality check.

What it answers

Are we showing up in the right topics, with the right position, and with the right source grounding?

1

Filters + table scope

Where to navigate

From Brand Presence, move to the Data Insights section or table.

What to highlight

  • Topic-level versus prompt-level analysis
  • Region / market context
  • Search and export controls

Why demo it

This is where Brand Presence stops being a scorecard and becomes an inspection layer.

2

High-popularity, low-visibility topics

Where to navigate

Sort or filter for high Popularity with weaker Visibility Score.

What to highlight

  • Popularity
  • Visibility Score
  • Mentions

Why demo it

Best quick way to show where demand exists but brand presence is underperforming.

3

All Citations vs Owned Citations

Where to navigate

Look for rows with strong All Citations but weak Owned Citations.

What to highlight

  • Topics where answers are well sourced overall
  • But those citations are going to someone else

Why demo it

Best authority-gap story: the answer may look healthy, but the grounding is going to the wrong source.

4

Prompt drill-down

Where to navigate

Click from a topic into a specific prompt.

What to highlight

  • Position
  • Sentiment
  • Citations tied to the prompt
  • Executions or sample size where shown

Why demo it

This is the clearest proof that a "good metric" still needs an answer-quality validation.

Suggested talk track

"Brand Presence gives us the scorecard, but strong metrics do not always mean strong answers. Data Insights is where we check whether those answers are actually useful, well grounded, and sourced from the right place."

3. Agentic Traffic

Use this as the agent-readiness and access layer.

Primary role: can agents access and read the site?

Sharpened message

Pages cannot be cited if agents cannot read them. Agentic Traffic is where you show whether AI systems are actually reaching the site, succeeding on requests, and seeing the content that matters.

What it proves

It proves AI demand is real, but more importantly, it shows whether that demand is landing on accessible, readable, citable content.

1

Filters + top-line metrics + traffic distribution

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Agentic Traffic and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight

  • Agentic Interactions
  • Success Rate
  • Average TTFB
  • Hits by Market / Category / Page Type

Why demo it

Establishes both health and where AI attention is concentrated.

2

Content Visibility

Where to navigate

Stay on Agentic Traffic and use the Content Visibility area.

What to highlight

  • Whether agents can actually read the page content
  • Pages with high demand but weak visibility

Why demo it

This is the must-demo GEO moment: being crawled is not the same as being readable.

3

User Agent Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to User Agent Analysis.

What to highlight

  • Breakdown by agent type
  • Page type by agent type
  • Differences between chatbots, crawlers, and other agent classes

Why demo it

Shows that not all AI traffic is the same, and not all agents matter for the same reason.

4

URL Performance Analysis + movers

Where to navigate

Use Top Movers / Bottom Movers, then move into URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight

  • Rising and falling URLs
  • Total Hits
  • Unique Agents
  • Top Agent
  • Success Rate

Why demo it

Best place to identify which exact pages deserve optimization effort.

Suggested talk track

"Agentic Traffic shows whether AI systems are actually reaching the site, but the most important question is whether they can read what matters. Content Visibility is the key bridge from raw bot activity to real citation opportunity."

4. URL Inspector

Use this as the page-level truth layer.

Primary role: exact URL performance and citation opportunity

Sharpened message

URL Inspector is where the story becomes page-level and concrete. It shows which exact URLs are being cited, which ones are being evaluated but not chosen, and which outside URLs are winning authority in the same answer space.

Key opportunity pattern

High attempts, low citations is one of the best demo moments: LLMs are clearly evaluating the page, but the page is not converting that demand into citations.

1

Filters + overview metrics + trend charts

Where to navigate

Open Domain → URL Inspector and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight

  • Unique prompts with owned citations
  • Total unique prompts
  • Unique cited URLs
  • Total times cited
  • Citation Attempts / Referral Hits trends

Why demo it

Best URL-level summary before drilling into individual pages.

2

High attempts, low citations

Where to navigate

In Your Cited URLs, find pages with high Citation Attempts and weaker Times Cited, then open details.

What to highlight

  • Citation Attempts
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In

Why demo it

Strongest opportunity story: demand exists, but citation conversion is weak.

3

Your Cited URLs + URL Details drill-down

Where to navigate

Go to Your Cited URLs, choose a strong owned page, and open URL Details.

What to highlight

  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
  • Agentic Hits
  • Referral Hits
  • Sentiment, citation trends, and prompt analysis in details

Why demo it

Best owned-page analysis flow and the clearest bridge between visibility, traffic, and explanation.

4

Cited Third Party URLs + Cited Domains

Where to navigate

Move to Cited Third Party URLs and Cited Domains.

What to highlight

  • Which outside URLs are winning
  • Which domains dominate
  • Competitor versus earned versus owned mix

Why demo it

Best competitive authority story at the page and domain level.

Suggested talk track

"If Brand Presence is the scorecard and Data Insights is the quality check, URL Inspector is the page-level truth layer. This is where we stop talking in abstractions and start talking about exact URLs, exact demand, and exact citation opportunity."

5. Referral Traffic

Use this as the human-validation and business-outcomes layer.

Primary role: did AI visibility produce real visits and value?

Sharpened message

Citations matter most when they produce qualified human visits. Referral Traffic is where LLM visibility gets validated as a real acquisition and engagement channel.

What it proves

It proves whether AI discovery is actually driving humans to the site, which sources are best, which landing pages work, and whether that traffic creates business impact.

1

Filters + top-line metrics + AI source breakdown

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Referral Traffic and stay on Traffic Insights.

What to highlight

  • Total Referral Traffic
  • Referral Traffic from LLMs
  • Consent Rate
  • Bounce Rate
  • Hits By Referrer

Why demo it

Best opener for proving that LLMs are sending real human traffic.

2

Referral Sources Details

Where to navigate

Stay on Traffic Insights and scroll to Referral Sources Details.

What to highlight

  • Source
  • Visits
  • Bounce Rate
  • Channel

Why demo it

Best table-based source quality comparison, beyond the chart summary.

3

URL Performance Analysis + URL details

Where to navigate

Stay on Traffic Insights, scroll to URL Performance Analysis, then open a page's details view if available.

What to highlight

  • URL
  • Traffic
  • LLM Traffic
  • Bounce Rate
  • Consent Rate
  • Page Intent

Why demo it

Best landing-page action story and the clearest place to inspect whether AI-referred traffic actually engages.

4

Business Impact

Where to navigate

Switch from Traffic Insights to Business Impact.

What to highlight

  • Page Views
  • Entries
  • Revenue
  • Conversion Rate
  • Avg. Session Duration
  • Pages per Visit
  • Total Orders

Why demo it

Strongest executive close: it answers whether AI-driven discovery is commercially meaningful.

Suggested talk track

"Referral Traffic is the human validation layer. It proves whether AI discovery is sending real visitors, which platforms send the best traffic, which landing pages work, and whether any of that leads to business outcomes."

Recommended full-demo flow

This sequence tells the cleanest story from visibility to business value.

Step 1

Brand Presence

Are we showing up?

Start with visibility, competitor context, perception, and share of voice.

Step 2

Data Insights

Are those good answers?

Validate quality, grounding, topic gaps, and source authority.

Step 3

Agentic Traffic

Can agents read the site?

Show access, success, visibility, and page-level readability.

Step 4

URL Inspector

Which exact pages win or miss?

Move from abstract scores to page-level citation truth.

Step 5

Referral Traffic

Did it create real outcomes?

Close on human traffic, landing-page performance, and ROI.
