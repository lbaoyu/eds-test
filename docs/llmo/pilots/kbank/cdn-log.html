Why CDN Logs Matter

Foundation: raw signals

Traditional analytics tell you what people did after they arrived. CDN logs let Adobe understand what AI agents did before that click ever happened.

What Adobe captures from CDN logs

  • URL visited
  • User agent
  • Status code
  • Referrer & host header
  • Time to first byte (TTFB)
  • Request method & timestamp
  • Content type

Why this is essential

  • Shows whether AI crawlers can actually reach and read your content
  • Reveals issues hidden from standard analytics, especially when bots do not execute JavaScript like human browsers
  • Provides the raw signal for Agentic Traffic, Referral Traffic, and AI-readiness opportunities
  • Lets Adobe prioritise fixes on real agent demand, not guesswork
Bottom line: without CDN log forwarding, Adobe LLM Optimizer cannot populate the Agentic Traffic or Referral Traffic dashboards with the insights they are designed to provide.
2

What Agentic Traffic Tells You

AI crawlers & bots

Agentic Traffic shows how AI agents, crawlers, and chatbots interact with your site — the visibility layer most teams do not have today.

What teams can see

  • Total AI-agent requests and success rate
  • Performance signals such as TTFB
  • Which pages and content types AI agents access most
  • Breakdowns by agent, platform, market, and page

Why this matters

  • Identifies pages AI uses to build answers about your brand
  • Highlights blocked, broken, or hard-to-read content
  • Prioritises technical fixes such as pre-rendering or AI accessibility
  • Supports prompt, content, and page prioritisation from real crawl behaviour
3

What Referral Traffic Tells You

Human visitors from AI

Referral Traffic shows the human side of AI discovery: visitors who arrive after clicking through from AI-generated answers, citations, or external referral sources.

What teams can see

  • Which external AI or referral sources drive visits (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Copilot, Claude…)
  • Which landing pages receive that traffic
  • Regional and page-level traffic patterns
  • With Adobe Analytics or GA4: engagement, conversion, and revenue in the Business Impact view

Why this matters

  • Connects AI visibility to real user clicks
  • Shows whether AI-discovered traffic is valuable, not just present
  • Helps teams invest in the pages and topics that drive both visibility and business outcomes
