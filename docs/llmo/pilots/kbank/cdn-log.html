Why CDN Logs, Agentic Traffic, and Referral Traffic Are Essential

CDN log forwarding is not just a setup step — it is the foundation that allows Adobe LLM Optimizer to see how AI systems access your site, connect that behavior to human visits, and turn those signals into prioritized optimization opportunities and measurable business impact.

Why CDN Logs Matter

Traditional analytics tell you what people did after they arrived. CDN logs help Adobe understand what AI agents did before that click ever happened.

What Adobe captures from CDN logs

  • URL visited
  • User agent
  • Status code
  • Referrer
  • Host header
  • Time to first byte (TTFB)
  • Request method
  • Timestamp
  • Content type

Why this is essential

  • Shows whether AI crawlers can actually reach and read your content
  • Reveals issues hidden from standard analytics, especially when bots do not execute JavaScript like human browsers
  • Provides the raw signal for Agentic Traffic, Referral Traffic, and AI-readiness opportunities
  • Lets Adobe prioritize fixes based on real agent demand, not guesswork
Bottom line: without CDN log forwarding, Adobe LLM Optimizer cannot populate the Agentic Traffic or Referral Traffic dashboards with the traffic insights they are designed to provide.

What Agentic Traffic Tells You

Agentic Traffic shows how AI agents, crawlers, and chatbots interact with your site. This is the visibility layer that most teams do not have today.

What teams can see

  • Total AI-agent requests
  • Success rate of agent requests
  • Performance signals such as TTFB
  • Which pages and content types are most accessed by AI agents
  • Breakdowns by agent, platform, market, and page

Why this matters

  • Identifies pages that AI uses to build answers about your brand
  • Highlights blocked, broken, or hard-to-read content
  • Helps prioritize technical fixes such as pre-rendering or improving accessibility for AI agents
  • Supports better prompt, content, and page prioritization based on real crawl behavior

What Referral Traffic Tells You

Referral Traffic shows the human side of AI discovery: visitors who arrive after clicking through from AI-generated answers, citations, or external referral sources.

What teams can see

  • Which external AI or referral sources drive visits
  • Which pages receive that traffic
  • Regional and page-level traffic patterns
  • With Adobe Analytics or GA4 integration: engagement, conversion, and revenue in the Business Impact view

Why this matters

  • Connects AI visibility to real user clicks
  • Shows whether AI-discovered traffic is valuable, not just present
  • Helps teams invest in the pages and topics that drive both visibility and business outcomes
