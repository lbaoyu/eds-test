That means strict HKTB brand presence is useful but secondary, while prompt intent coverage, owned citation capture, referral traffic, and machine accessibility are the primary indicators of LLMO success.

Interpretation guardrail

"HKTB not mentioned" does not automatically mean "HKTB lost." A destination answer can still be a win when the model cites DiscoverHongKong pages or sends traffic to them. Executive takeaway

HKTB already wins when the prompt explicitly asks for official or trusted tourism guidance. The larger opportunity is to expand from official-source success into generic destination authority so that broad Hong Kong travel questions also resolve to DiscoverHongKong content. That is the consistent lens for this account: grow owned destination capture, not just brand-name mentions.

Consistent reading framework

  • Layer 1: prompt intent — what kinds of travel questions are being asked?
  • Layer 2: strict brand presence — does the answer explicitly surface HKTB?
  • Layer 3: destination authority — does the answer cite DiscoverHongKong pages?
  • Layer 4: machine access — can crawlers and chatbots reliably fetch the right destination pages?

What prompt analysis says clearly

  • Official / board-seeking prompts are already won: 100% strict brand coverage, 94.1% citation coverage, and 82.4% owned URL coverage.
  • Generic destination prompts are the main gap: only 12.5% strict brand coverage, 17.5% citation coverage, and 7.5% owned URL coverage.
  • First-time visitor demand is the largest prompt pool and remains undercaptured, which makes it the highest-priority editorial growth area.
  • Comparison / vs prompts are currently unwon in the measured set, with 0% brand visibility and 0% owned citations.

What HKTB is genuinely winning

  • Official-trust intent is already won and should be defended, not treated as the main expansion area.
  • Seasonal Travel & Events is the strongest proof that HKTB can own both the brand layer and the destination authority layer.
  • Where to stay and Food Discovery already show that destination authority can outperform strict brand presence.
  • 73.9% of cited prompts already include owned URLs, so the core problem is expanding citation-worthy visibility into more prompts, not rebuilding authority from zero.

What should change in the narrative

  • Do not frame the account as weak because strict brand coverage is 38.6%.
  • Do frame the account as strong in official intent, underpenetrated in generic destination demand.
  • Do treat owned URL coverage, prompt citation coverage, and generic prompt capture as the real levers.
  • Do position prompt analysis as the reason editorial investment should focus on first-time visitor, comparison, and broad planning journeys.

Priority opportunities

Priority 1

Expand generic first-time visitor capture

The prompt inventory is heaviest in first-time visitor questions, but the measured prompt performance is weak. This is the clearest place to turn large demand into owned destination authority.

Priority 2

Build comparison and peer-city decision content

Comparison-style prompts are effectively unwon. HKTB should publish clearer Hong Kong-vs-Singapore and Hong Kong-vs-peer-city content designed to be directly citable by LLMs.

Priority 3

Turn planning hubs into canonical prompt destinations

Travel guide, attractions, and trip-planner hubs already attract citations and machine traffic. These should be strengthened as canonical destinations for broad travel-planning prompts.

Priority 4

Reduce crawl friction on the pages that matter most

Redirect and error cleanup will improve the consistency with which the best travel pages can be fetched and cited. For this account, technical hygiene and prompt performance are directly linked.

30-day action plan

  1. Split reporting into strict HKTB entity KPIs and destination authority KPIs.
  2. Build or strengthen generic Hong Kong planning pages for first-time visitors, broad things-to-do, and city comparisons.
  3. Expand where-to-stay and food decision content because those prompt clusters already show conversion potential.
  4. Reduce redirect, 403, and 404 friction on the most heavily machine-accessed URLs.
  5. Concentrate citation authority around travel-guide, attractions, events, neighbourhood, and trip-planner hubs.

Measurement to track next

  1. Strict brand coverage: useful, but secondary.
  2. Any citation coverage: improve beyond 40.4%.
  3. Owned URL coverage: improve beyond 29.8%.
  4. Owned citation share: move above 19.3%.
  5. Generic destination prompt visibility: improve from the current 6.8% average on non-official prompts.

Methodology & source scope

This revised HTML snapshot is based on the uploaded HKTB files only: overview PDF, prompt inventory, prompt-level insight tables, share-of-voice tables, owned URL citations, third-party domains, trending URLs, agentic traffic datasets, response-code breakdowns, referral traffic, and user-agent summaries. The main methodological choice in this version is interpretive rather than mathematical: HKTB is treated as a destination-promotion publisher whose success should be judged by prompt-to-destination authority, not by brand mentions alone.
