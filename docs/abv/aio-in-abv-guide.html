Section 2

Mental models to learn first

1) This is not "SEO with a new label"

The category is about how brands are represented in AI-generated answers — mentions, citations, sentiment, platform spread, answer framing, and discoverability — not only traditional blue-link rankings.

2) Semrush AIO is the outside-in layer

Think of Semrush as the external market scanner: what prompts matter, how competitors are winning, where topic gaps exist, and which opportunities deserve attention first.

3) Adobe is the inside-out layer

Adobe is strongest at owned-surface activation and measurement: edge/source optimization, agentic and referral traffic, and downstream business impact.

4) Prompt quality determines measurement quality

A bad prompt set gives a false picture of AI visibility. A good prompt set includes branded, non-branded, comparison, task, commercial, and journey-stage coverage.

5) Think in loops, not dashboards

The durable motion is discover → prioritize → activate → measure → repeat. A visibility score matters only when it changes decisions and behavior.

6) Treat market and operational scores as different lenses

AIO is the market-wide lens. LLMO is the operational lens. They are both useful, but they are not interchangeable and should not be directly compared.

Concepts and terms that matter

GEO Generative Engine Optimization. The broader discipline of improving how brands appear in AI-generated answers.
AI Visibility How often and how well a brand appears across AI search/chat surfaces. The top-line concept the category tries to improve.

Mention The AI answer names the brand. Basic visibility signal.
Citation The AI answer references the brand's content or site as a source. Usually higher-value than a mere mention.

Agentic traffic AI agents and bots visiting owned pages. A leading signal that AI systems are reading site content.
Referral traffic Human traffic arriving from AI-generated citations or answers. Bridges visibility work to business outcomes.
Optimize at Edge Serve AI-friendly changes at the CDN/edge layer. Fast and reversible way to improve agent-visible content.
Optimize at Source Change the origin content itself. Best when the human-facing experience should also change.
Closed-loop measurement Connecting optimizations to traffic, engagement, conversion, or revenue. Critical for proving value beyond visibility reports.

Domain-level = whole site Page-level = one URL Topic-level = one subject area Prompt-level = one user question

Semrush AIO modules — what each one is really for

Visibility Overview

Baseline the category and your starting position.

Prompt Research

Narrow the universe to prompts that matter.

Competitor Research

Find where competitors are winning and you are absent.

Prompt Tracking

Monitor movement on the prompts you chose to care about.

Add demand and trend context to prioritization.

Module Primary job Best question Best output
Visibility Overview Market-level baseline How visible are we right now across platforms and topics? Visibility score, mentions, citations, sentiment, platform spread
Prompt Research Prompt-universe reduction Which prompts and intent clusters are worth tracking? Prioritized prompt candidates and topic clusters
Competitor Research Gap detection Where do competitors appear and we do not? Missing/weak/shared/strong/unique competitive map
Prompt Tracking Ongoing scorekeeping Are we improving on the monitored prompts? Trend view for the chosen prompt program
Traffic Analysis Demand context Which topics or prompts show meaningful demand and momentum? Trend-informed prioritization

Most important module lesson

Prompt Research is usually the pivotal step. Without a well-formed prompt set, every later visibility number becomes less meaningful.

Most important competitor lesson

The Missing / Weak / Shared / Strong / Unique framing is one of the easiest ways to teach non-experts how competitive GEO actually works.

Section 5

Measurement model — how to avoid the most common confusion

Core rule: AIO and LLMO are both useful, but they answer different questions. Keep them as separate reporting streams.
Lens What it measures Best reporting cadence Best audience
Semrush AIO Market-wide visibility across a much broader prompt universe Quarterly / strategic reviews Executives, category owners, strategy leads
LLM Optimizer Operational visibility within the curated prompt program plus traffic and business impact Weekly / monthly operating reviews SEO, content, growth, operations, implementation teams

Use AIO when the question is strategic

  • Are we gaining or losing share in the category?
  • Which competitors are moving faster?
  • Which topics matter most in the market?
  • How is AI sentiment evolving?

Use LLMO when the question is operational

  • Which opportunity should we fix first?
  • Did the page-level or prompt-level program improve?
  • Did agentic or referral traffic change?
  • Did the work affect engagement, conversion, or revenue?
Safe phrasing to teach teams:
"AIO is the strategic market lens."
"LLMO is the operational scoreboard."

Section 6

Optimization layers — edge vs source

Optimize at Edge

AI-friendly changes are served at the CDN/edge layer, typically to targeted AI user agents. This is fast, reversible, and useful when the main goal is improving agent-visible content without waiting for full origin changes.

FastReversibleAgent-focused

Optimize at Source

The origin content itself is updated. This is the stronger choice when the human-facing experience should also improve and when permanent editorial or structural improvements are desired.

DurableHuman-visibleEditorial

Practical teaching rule: teams should not obsess over which layer is "more real." The right question is which layer produces the intended outcome fastest and most safely.
Question Best answer
Which system should track improvement after edge or source changes? Both — use LLMO for operational and business impact, and AIO for market-level movement.
Should edge and source be combined into one score? No — treat outcomes as combined program lift unless the changes were staged separately.
Can you attribute lift cleanly to edge vs source if both changed together? Usually no — not without staged rollout or controlled comparison.

Section 7

Attribution — why LLMO opportunities map more directly to LLMO than to AIO

Direct linkage: LLMO

LLMO opportunities typically point to specific owned pages, content structures, crawlability issues, summaries, FAQs, or edge/source changes. That makes them easier to tie to the curated prompt program, agentic traffic, referral traffic, and business outcomes.

Indirect linkage: AIO

AIO tracks a broader market and competitive prompt universe. Improvements can show up as better share of voice, citations, or sentiment, but usually as market movement, not as clean 1:1 proof that one opportunity caused one score change.

Best teaching sentence:
"LLMO opportunities usually have direct operational attribution. Their effect on AIO is usually second-order: broader market lift, not clean single-opportunity causality."
If the question is… Use this first Why
Did this opportunity improve the monitored program? LLMO Closest to the change and best aligned to prompt, page, and outcome tracking.
Did our category position improve after several rounds of optimization? AIO Best view for market-wide share and competitor movement.
Can we isolate edge vs source contribution? Only if staged Otherwise most signals show combined lift.

Section 8

Customer-facing enablement — the Semrush side

Safe, customer-ready positioning:
"Semrush gives Adobe Brand Visibility the market intelligence layer: where your brand is winning or losing across AI search, which prompts and topics matter most, how competitors are performing, and where to focus optimization effort first."

60-second executive version

AI is increasingly where customers start. Semrush helps brands understand how they show up across that market, which competitors are winning, and which discovery moments matter most. Adobe then helps operationalize that insight and connect it to outcomes.

SEO / digital lead version

The Semrush side answers the next-level question beyond "AI visibility matters": where exactly are we losing, against whom, and on which prompts or topics? That creates a more defensible roadmap.

Cross-functional version

Most teams have internal analytics, but not a category-level view of AI visibility. Semrush fills that gap with prompt intelligence, competitor benchmarking, sentiment, and topic opportunity discovery.

Discovery questions that open the right conversation

  • How are you measuring how your brand shows up in AI-generated answers today?
  • Do you have a competitive view, or mostly an internal view?
  • How confident are you that your current prompt set reflects real demand and commercial intent?
  • Which products, categories, or use cases matter most for AI-driven discovery this year?
  • Can you tell where competitors are being cited and you are not?
  • When visibility shifts, can you tell whether it's demand, competition, or content gaps?

Section 9

Objection handling — Semrush side

"We already have SEO tools."

Response: That can actually increase the value here. Traditional SEO tools may help with rankings and keyword intelligence, but the Semrush side adds the AI prompt intelligence, competitor AI share of voice, and market-wide context that most SEO stacks do not surface cleanly.

"Why not just use Adobe's side?"

Response: Adobe is strongest at activation and measurement on owned surfaces. Semrush provides the broader market signal — which prompts matter, where competitors are winning, and which topics deserve action.

"Can't we just build our own prompt list?"

Response: You can curate prompts, but the hard part is prioritization and coverage. Semrush helps reduce guesswork by bringing larger-scale market and intent signals into prompt selection.

"Do you have actual private prompts from ChatGPT?"

Response: No platform has access to private LLM conversation logs. The value here comes from high-scale market signals and realistic proxies that make prioritization and competitive analysis stronger.

"Is this one unified product now?"

Response: The right framing today is a combined solution with complementary strengths: Semrush for market intelligence and Adobe for optimization and business measurement, with further integrated experience evolving.

"We care about outcomes, not more dashboards."

Response: That is precisely why the Semrush side matters. It makes prioritization better. Adobe then closes the loop by showing whether those choices changed traffic, engagement, conversion, or revenue.

Best objection-handling posture: listen for whether the concern is about value, feasibility, or ownership, then answer at that level instead of jumping straight to features.
