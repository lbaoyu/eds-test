What it is

An advisory engagement that turns capability into outcomes

Use Case Mapping to Solution Capability helps the customer develop, prioritise and align their Adobe Brand Visibility use cases and KPIs to specific product capabilities — producing one clear, measurable roadmap. Everything in the engagement builds toward a single artefact: the Use Case & Value Map.

The job to be done

Connect “what ABV can do” to “what the business is trying to achieve” and “how we’ll know it worked.” Your role is to draw that line and make it measurable.

The engagement is collaborative: customers do not need fully formed use cases beforehand. You expand early ideas into prioritised, measurable value opportunities.

The artefact at the centre

The Use Case & Value Map is the through-line of the whole engagement: business goal → use case → ABV capability → KPI → value. The phases and workshops that follow exist to build and prioritise it.

Hold the map up as the target from the first session, and return every conversation to it.

How to deliver it

A six-phase model across 40 hours

A suggested structure for the time-boxed engagement. Adjust the split to the customer, but keep the through-line: discover → develop use cases → develop KPIs → map to capability → complete and present the value map.

0

Scope & prepare

~4h · pre-work

Confirm the ABV scope with the CSM, qualify the signals, and gather inputs: ABV access, the current AI-visibility baseline, the prompt library, a competitor citation snapshot, priority topics and segments, and Adobe Analytics / CJA for AI-origin traffic. Line up stakeholders across Marketing/SEO, Brand, Digital PR and Content, plus a leadership sponsor.

Output: Engagement charter, input pack, confirmed stakeholders and agenda.
1

Discover & assess maturity

~6h

Working session to capture business objectives, how ABV is being used today, and the customer’s current maturity. Surface where the brand is present, absent, or misrepresented in AI answers and where agents can’t read the content — tying each gap to a business cost.

Output: Maturity baseline and a prioritised gap inventory mapped to business impact.
2

Use Case Development Workshop

~8h

Diverge then converge: reframe gaps and ambitions into candidate ABV use cases, then prioritise on impact × effort. Expand early ideas rather than filtering them — breadth is the value the customer can’t generate alone.

Output: Prioritised use case backlog (must/should/could) — the left-hand column of the value map.
3

KPI Development Workshop

~8h

For each priority use case, define measurable KPIs — mentions, citations, content visibility, AI share of voice, agentic vs referral traffic, then engagement and pipeline/revenue. Agree a baseline (from ABV and CJA), a target, the data source, an owner and a review cadence.

Output: A prioritised KPI set per use case — the right-hand column of the value map.
4

Map capabilities & complete the value map

~8h

Tie each use case to the specific ABV capability that delivers it, resolve the recurring “which lever” questions, and assemble use cases, capabilities and KPIs into the completed Use Case & Value Map with best-practice notes.

Output: The completed Use Case & Value Map.
5

Value map readout & next-best-action

~6h

Finalise the value map, sequence a prioritised next-best-action plan aligned to roadmap priorities, and run the executive readout so the customer leaves with a roadmap they can act on and be measured against.

Output: Final Use Case & Value Map and prioritised next-best-action roadmap.

AI attribution in three questions

A simple way to structure measurement across the funnel — ABV provides the evidence at each step, with Adobe Analytics & CJA closing the loop to revenue.

1

Discovery

“Did AI assistants help people discover us?”

ABV shows which questions and scenarios bring the brand into AI conversations — and where presence moved from “rarely mentioned” to “consistently recommended.”

2

Visit

“Did those recommendations actually send people to us?”

ABV shows referral traffic from LLMs to product, comparison / “why us,” and “get started” pages — alongside the agentic traffic crawling them.

3

Outcome

“Did those AI-origin visits contribute to our goals?”

ABV shows the revenue contribution of AI-driven traffic by connecting agentic and referral sessions to downstream conversion.

How to specify each KPI

For every measure on the value map, agree five things in the KPI Development Workshop: baseline (from ABV and CJA, illustrative if data isn’t yet available), target, data source, owner, and review cadence. Keep measures directional rather than absolute, and pair each AI metric with its organic counterpart so gains are real.

Facilitation

Running the two workshops

These are the engine that builds the value map. Both are collaborative working sessions — you facilitate and expand, you don’t prescribe or build.

Use Case Development Workshop

Reframe each gap and ambition as an outcome, then a candidate use case, and prioritise on impact × effort. Capture more than you’ll action; the prioritised set becomes the use-case column of the value map.

FE tip: when a customer says “we don’t have use cases”, that’s the workshop, not a blocker. Start from where competitors beat them in AI answers and which topics matter most to revenue.

KPI Development Workshop

For every priority use case, attach at least one measure (mentions, citations, content visibility, share of voice, agentic vs referral traffic) with a baseline, target, data source, owner and cadence, using ABV and CJA.

FE tip: a use case without a measure is an opinion. Pair each AI metric with its organic counterpart, and don’t let a use case leave the room unmeasured.

Stay in lane

Guardrails

!What this engagement is — and isn’t

  • Advisory only. No hands-on configuration, code implementation, troubleshooting, or ongoing optimisation.
  • Time-boxed to 40 hours of Adobe effort. Scope the workshops to fit, and park execution-level detail for the appropriate follow-on motion.
  • Not for customers who already have fully developed use cases and are seeking execution or optimisation — route those to the right accelerator or services engagement.
  • Adobe Brand Visibility is the focus here. Cross-solution synergies can be noted in the value map, but are not implemented within this engagement.

Who delivers it

Global Field Engineering, often in partnership with the CSM during the use case development phase. Customers do not need fully developed use cases before the engagement — the process is collaborative and helps expand early ideas into actionable value opportunities.
