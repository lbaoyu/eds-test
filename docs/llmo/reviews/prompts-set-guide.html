Building a Strong Prompt Bank for Trial Success

A prompt bank is one of the most important ingredients in a successful trial. It defines the customer questions you want to test, the business areas you want to learn from, and the visibility opportunities you want to improve.

Goal

Turn customer questions into a structured learning system

Framework

Categories → Topics → Prompts

Best Mindset

Clarity and focus matter more than volume

Outcome

Better interpretation, stronger learning, clearer next steps

Presenter note: Open by positioning the prompt bank as the foundation of the trial. The goal of the deck is to teach why structure matters and how to make categories, topics, and prompts work together.
Slide 2

What a prompt bank is really doing

A prompt bank is not just a list of questions. It is a structured model for deciding what the trial will test and what the results will be able to teach you.

Coverage

Defines what is included

It sets the business areas, customer scenarios, and questions that will shape the trial.

Structure

Organizes the learning

It helps you interpret results by cluster instead of looking at disconnected prompts one by one.

Signal quality

Shapes usefulness

If the prompt bank is vague or fragmented, the output becomes harder to interpret and act on.

Simple teaching line: The prompt bank defines what your trial can learn.
Presenter note: This slide sets the mental model. Emphasize that the prompt bank affects both scope and interpretation, not just content volume.
Slide 3

The core hierarchy: category, topic, and prompt

Strong prompt banks keep these three layers distinct. When they are blurred together, the bank becomes harder to build, review, and improve.

Level 1

Category

A broad business area or domain.

Examples: Business Banking, Card Services, Loans, Wealth

Level 2

Topic

A scenario cluster within that business area.

Examples: Account Opening, Fees and Waivers, Disputes, App Access

Level 3

Prompt

An individual customer-style question.

Example: How do I request a credit card annual fee waiver?

Why this matters

  • Categories define strategic scope.
  • Topics organize related customer needs.
  • Prompts create the question-level signals.
Presenter note: This is the key teaching slide. Spend time here. Many weak prompt banks result from confusing categories, topics, and prompts.
Slide 4

What makes a strong category or topic?

Categories should be broad business areas. Topics should be customer scenario clusters. Topics are often the most important layer because they make results interpretable.

Strong category
  • Represents a real business domain
  • Can support multiple topics
  • Is clear and easy to name
  • Would matter if performance changed
Weak category
  • Too narrow and really just a topic
  • Based on internal jargon
  • Too broad to organize well
  • Too minor to justify its own structure
Strong topic Why it works Weak topic version Why it struggles
Fees and Waivers Clear cluster of related customer needs Annual Fee Waiver Request Form Timing Too narrow for a trial-scale topic
Cross-border Payments Supports multiple related prompts International Transfer Cut-off Before Friday Holiday Too specific to stand alone
App Access and Login Groups related digital access issues Forgot App Password With New Device More useful as a prompt than a topic
Presenter note: Explain that categories answer "what part of the business?" while topics answer "what kind of customer scenario?"
Slide 5

What makes a strong prompt?

A good prompt should sound like a real customer question, be specific enough to interpret, and be distinct enough to add value to the bank.

Natural

Sounds like a real question, not a keyword phrase.

Specific

Includes enough context to reduce ambiguity.

Focused

Centers on one scenario or need.

Useful

Likely to generate interpretable signals.

Distinct

Adds something meaningfully different.

Broad vs stronger prompt

Too broad

What is the best business bank account?

Too general to interpret clearly.

Stronger

What is the best business bank account for startups in Singapore?

Audience and scenario are defined, making the output more useful.

Presenter note: Use this slide to show that prompt writing quality is not cosmetic. Specificity changes how useful the trial becomes.
Slide 6

Common mistakes to avoid

Most weak prompt banks do not fail because the business questions are wrong. They fail because the structure and prompt design are not disciplined enough.

Mistake 1

Using topics as categories

A narrow issue becomes a top-level bucket when it should sit inside a broader business area.

Mistake 2

Using prompts as topics

A single narrow question is labeled like a cluster, creating fragmentation.

Mistake 3

Making topics too broad

One catch-all topic absorbs many unrelated questions and becomes hard to interpret.

Mistake 4

Making prompts too generic

Broad prompts create noisy results and weak next steps.

Useful diagnostic question: If you removed the prompt text and looked only at the topic names, would the structure still make sense to a stakeholder?
Presenter note: This slide is useful for workshops. Ask attendees to identify which mistake they see most often in their own first draft.
Slide 7

How to build the bank step by step

Building a prompt bank is an iterative process. Start with the right scope, define the hierarchy clearly, refine the weakest parts, and keep the first version stable long enough to learn from it.

1

Choose scope

Start with the business area or journey that matters most.

2

Set categories

Choose clear top-level domains.

3

Create topics

Group related customer scenarios.

4

Draft prompts

Write realistic, specific questions.

5

Review quality

Remove overlap and merge weak structures.

6

Stabilize

Protect the baseline before major changes.

Working principle: The goal is not the most prompts. The goal is the most useful learning structure for the trial you are running.
Presenter note: Emphasize that the first version should be refined enough to be credible, but not endlessly edited before the trial begins.
Slide 8

Trial readiness checklist

Before finalizing the prompt bank, confirm that the structure is clear, the prompts are useful, and the bank is focused enough for the size of the trial.

Category checklist
  • Is each category a real business area?
  • Can it support several topics?
  • Is the name clear and useful?
  • Would performance here matter?
Topic checklist
  • Does each topic represent a true scenario cluster?
  • Is it broad enough for several prompts?
  • Is it narrow enough to stay interpretable?
  • Have we avoided too many one-prompt topics?
Prompt checklist
  • Do the prompts sound natural?
  • Are broad prompts specific enough?
  • Have we removed overlap?
  • Does each prompt clearly fit its topic?
Final takeaway: Strong prompt banks are built by separating the roles of categories, topics, and prompts clearly. Categories define business scope, topics organize customer scenarios, and prompts provide the specific questions that create useful learning.
Presenter note: Close by reinforcing that quality comes from structure. If the hierarchy is strong, the trial is much more likely to produce useful insights.
