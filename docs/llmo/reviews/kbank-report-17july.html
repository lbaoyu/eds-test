04Priorities

1 · Win fund ownership build

Fund visibility rose to 32.9 and fund-selection recovered strongly (one no-result prompt this period, down from eight), but ownership is still thin — counting K-Asset, KBank owns 8.6% of fund citations, and the main site alone just 3.7%. The clearest target is product-choice fund selection. Build owned fund content the assistants can read (fund-selection guides by goal, risk and horizon; fund comparisons; RMF / SSF / Thai ESG tax-fund guides), connect K-Asset pages to kasikornbank.com, and get KBank funds well-described on the aggregators the assistants already cite. Detail in the Investment Funds tab.

2 · Defend card questions defend

Cards strengthened to 54.6 (91.1 on branded prompts), with owned promotion pages as the cited sources and no negative sentiment. The risk is unchanged: promotions expire, and a citation to an expired promo page is a real risk — and KTC is still second on every card topic. Keep cited promotion pages current and monitor weekly. Detail in the Credit Cards tab.

3 · Make cited pages readable to crawlers

Among owned pages with readability data, 15 of 43 show less than half their content to an AI crawler — and some of the worst offenders are heavily cited. The main credit-card promotion index is cited 17 times yet only 19% readable; plustinum is cited 15 times at 24%. Pre-render the priority card and fund pages so the text is in the page when the crawler reads it. This is an engineering fix, not a content change, and it increases the payoff from the two priorities above.

Setup. Connect a CDN to turn on crawl-access and referral reporting (both still blank). 06Actions

1 · Keep cited promotion pages current

Owned promotion pages are the cited sources behind a 54.6 average and 91.1 on branded prompts. The risk: promotions expire, and a citation to an expired promo page is a real risk. Keep cited pages accurate as campaigns change, and check weekly for visibility drops or new competitor citations.

2 · Pre-render the hard-to-read card pages

The promotion index (cited 17×, 19% readable) and plustinum (cited 15×, 24%) show a fraction of their content to crawlers, along with travel-tripdotcom, mastercard-platinum and the-passion. Where the crawler can't read the page, it can't fully cite it. Pre-render these so the text is present on load — no content change needed.

3 · Fill the eligibility & lifestyle gap

Eligibility & lifestyle is the weaker card topic at 32.7 — "which card suits me" questions with no clear owned page. It rose from 23.4 last period; keep building owned "choose your card" guidance by spending pattern, and earn placement on the card-comparison publishers that already win citations.

05Actions

1 · Build fund content the assistants can read

Fund visibility rose to 32.9 and fund-selection to 35.8, but main-site owned share on the top-demand topic is still 3.9% (8.6% for the fund domain counting K-Asset), and five prompts show no result. The assistants answer confidently from aggregators — they just aren't choosing KBank's own pages. Add owned content the assistants can read that answers the questions directly: fund-selection guides by goal, risk and horizon; a fund comparison; and RMF / SSF / Thai ESG tax-fund guides — plus thematic and regional fund pages (AI, technology, US) where no-result prompts cluster — on kasikornbank.com and kasikornasset.com.

2 · Build on K-Asset and connect it to the main site

K-Asset supplies most of KBank's owned fund citations, but the main site adds little and the two aren't well linked. Strengthen K-Asset fund pages for citation (clear summaries, comparison-ready structure) and link to them from kasikornbank.com investment pages, so the main site appears too — and so its content is credited as owned.

3 · Get KBank funds onto the aggregators

Fund answers cite aggregators — yuanta mutualfund, my-best, finnomena, wealthmagik. Make sure KBank and K-Asset funds are well represented and accurately described there, so KBank products appear even when the answer cites a third party.

Setup. Connect a CDN to turn on crawl-access and referral reporting, and to confirm new fund pages are readable on publish — so the content build pays off.
