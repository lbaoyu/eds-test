North star

What Adobe Brand Visibility means in practice

The cleanest way to understand the product story is to separate discovery and prioritization from activation and proof.

Semrush AIO helps teams understand where the brand is winning or losing across AI search and the broader market: prompts, topics, competitors, sentiment, and visibility gaps.

Adobe helps teams act on that insight on owned surfaces and connect changes to agentic traffic, referral traffic, engagement, and business outcomes.

Primary Semrush job
Find the right problems

Identify the prompts, topics, and competitor gaps worth caring about.

Primary Adobe job
Drive the right actions

Surface opportunities, deploy changes, and prove whether they mattered.

Best AIO question
"Where are we losing?"

Category, prompt cluster, platform, and competitor visibility questions.

Best LLMO question
"Did this work?"

Opportunity execution, page/prompt improvement, and business impact questions.

Foundational learning

Mental models to internalize before anything else

If these six ideas are clear, the rest of the product story becomes much easier to teach and defend.

1) This is not just SEO relabeled

Traditional SEO asks how content ranks in search. This category asks how a brand is represented, cited, summarized, and recommended in AI answers.

2) Semrush is the outside-in layer

Think of Semrush AIO as the market scanner: demand signals, prompt universe, competitive movement, category gaps, and topic opportunity.

3) Adobe is the inside-out layer

Think of Adobe as the owned-surface action layer: optimization, delivery, monitoring, and business measurement.

4) Prompt quality determines signal quality

Visibility scores only mean something if the prompt set is well designed. Bad prompts create false clarity.

5) Think in loops, not dashboards

The durable operating model is discover → prioritize → activate → measure → repeat. Dashboards matter only if they improve the next decision.

6) Keep market and operational scores separate

AIO and LLMO are both valuable, but they are not one merged metric system. Use each for the question it is best suited to answer.

Teaching shortcut: if someone is confused, tell them: "Semrush helps choose what matters. Adobe helps improve what was chosen and prove the effect."
Vocabulary

Module deep dive

The Semrush AIO modules, taught in the simplest useful way

Each module is easiest to understand by the question it answers, not by its UI label.

Visibility Overview

Job: establish the market baseline.

  • How visible is the brand right now?
  • Which platforms or topic areas are strong or weak?
  • Is the problem broad, narrow, or competitor-specific?

Prompt Research

Job: narrow the prompt universe.

  • Which prompts actually matter enough to track?
  • Which intent clusters are commercially meaningful?
  • Which prompts are noise versus signal?

Competitor Research

Job: show where competitors are winning.

  • Where are they cited and we are not?
  • Which topic clusters are contested?
  • What is missing, weak, shared, strong, or unique?

Prompt Tracking

Job: monitor the curated program over time.

  • Are we improving on the prompts we chose?
  • Are trends moving in the right direction?
  • Did changes in the program show up in visibility?

Traffic Analysis

Job: add demand and trend context.

  • Which topic clusters appear to have momentum?
  • Where is demand forming or shifting?
  • How should we prioritize with trend context in mind?

Topics / Prompts workbench

Job: turn strategy into operational selection.

  • Inspect prompts and topic clusters directly.
  • Decide what belongs in the monitored program.
  • Prepare handoff into Adobe activation workflows.
Most important lesson in the module stack: Prompt Research and Competitor Research are where the strategy gets built. Prompt Tracking is where the program gets monitored. Do not use tracking as your first discovery step.
Workflow

How the pieces fit together in the real operating model

This is the simplest workflow to teach internally and externally. It keeps Semrush and Adobe in their best roles.

1

Baseline the market

Use AIO to understand the current visibility picture across category, topic, and competitor context.

2

Reduce the prompt universe

Select prompts and topic clusters that actually deserve attention.

3

Prioritize gaps

Use competitor research to find the most meaningful missing and weak areas.

4

Activate in Adobe

Use LLMO/ABV operational capabilities to surface opportunities and deploy improvements.

5

Measure in both lenses

Use LLMO for operational proof and AIO for market movement over time.

Semrush does not replace Adobe. It improves the quality of what Adobe is asked to optimize.

Measurement model

How to explain AIO vs LLMO without creating confusion

This is the area where teams most often collapse two good systems into one misleading story.

Lens What it is best at Best audience Best cadence
Semrush AIO Strategic market-wide visibility, competitor position, topic context, sentiment, share of voice. Executives, category owners, strategic leads. Quarterly reviews, category strategy discussions, narrative building.
LLM Optimizer / ABV operational layer Curated prompt program performance, opportunity execution, owned-surface outcomes, traffic and business impact. SEO, content, growth, operations, implementation teams. Weekly and monthly operating reviews, optimization readouts.

Use AIO when the question is strategic

  • Are we gaining or losing share in the category?
  • Which competitors are strengthening?
  • Which topics matter most in the market?
  • How is sentiment evolving?

Use LLMO when the question is operational

  • Which opportunity should we act on first?
  • Did the monitored program improve?
  • Did agentic or referral traffic move?
  • Did the work connect to engagement or revenue?
Best one-line teaching rule: AIO is the strategic market lens. LLMO is the operational proof lens.
Optimization strategy

Edge vs source optimization — how to explain the difference simply

Teams often over-focus on the layer itself instead of the purpose of the change. Teach the goal first, then the layer.

Optimize at Edge

Use the edge layer when the priority is to make AI-facing improvements quickly, safely, and reversibly. This is especially useful when teams want to improve what AI agents can consume without waiting on full source edits.

Fast Reversible Agent-focused

Optimize at Source

Use the source layer when the origin content should change permanently, especially when the human-facing experience and editorial reality also need to improve.

Durable Editorial Human-visible
Practical rule: the right layer is the one that gets the intended effect fastest, safest, and most sustainably for the use case.
Question Recommended answer
If optimizations happen at both edge and source, which system should track improvement? Track in both lenses. Use LLMO for operational outcomes and AIO for market-level movement.
Should edge and source be treated as separate scorecards by default? No. Unless they were staged separately, most outcome signals should be treated as combined lift.
Which layer should get credit if both changed at once? Usually neither in isolation. Report the improvement as program impact unless controlled sequencing exists.
Attribution guidance

Why LLMO opportunities map more directly to LLMO than to AIO

This is an important nuance for internal messaging, reporting expectations, and customer education.

Direct linkage in LLMO

LLMO surfaces specific opportunities on owned pages and tracks a curated prompt program. Because the opportunity, page, prompt set, and traffic outcomes are close together, attribution is usually more direct.

Indirect linkage in AIO

AIO tracks a broader market and competitive prompt universe. If an opportunity works, the impact may appear as better share of voice, citations, or sentiment, but usually as broader market movement rather than clean 1:1 causality.

LLMO opportunities usually have direct operational attribution.
Their effect on AIO is usually second-order: broader market lift, not precise single-opportunity proof.

Use LLMO to prove

  • This opportunity was deployed
  • This monitored prompt set improved
  • This page or program drove traffic or business lift

Use AIO to prove

  • Category standing improved
  • Competitor gaps narrowed
  • Market-level visibility moved over time

Attribution gets stronger when

  • The change was staged clearly
  • The topic cluster is narrow and important
  • The monitored prompt program was built from AIO research
Customer-facing enablement

How to talk about the Semrush side with customers

Start with the role Semrush plays, then connect it to the customer's current blind spots.

Semrush gives Adobe Brand Visibility the market-intelligence layer: where your brand is winning or losing across AI search, which prompts and topics matter most, how competitors are performing, and where to focus optimization effort first.

Executive opener

AI is increasingly where customers start. The Semrush side shows how the brand appears in that market, which competitors are winning, and where the biggest visibility gaps are.

SEO / digital opener

If you already know AI visibility matters, the next question is where exactly you are losing, against whom, and on which prompts or topics. That is where Semrush adds value.

Cross-functional opener

Most teams have internal analytics. Few have a strong category-level view of AI visibility. Semrush fills that gap.

Best discovery questions

  • How are you measuring your brand's presence in AI answers today?
  • Do you have a competitive view or mostly an internal view?
  • How confident are you in the prompt set you are prioritizing?
  • Can you tell where competitors are being cited and you are not?
  • Which use cases matter most for AI-driven discovery this year?

Best customer-safe framing

  • Semrush = market context and prioritization
  • Adobe = activation and measurement on owned surfaces
  • Together = insight to action
  • Do not oversell full metric unification
Objection handling

Direct responses to common Semrush-side objections

Answer the concern at the right level: value, trust, feasibility, or duplication.

"We already have SEO tools."

That can strengthen the fit. The Semrush side here is not just another ranking view. It adds AI prompt intelligence, market-wide competitive visibility, sentiment, and topic discovery.

"Why not just use Adobe's side?"

Adobe helps execute and measure on owned surfaces. Semrush helps identify what matters in the market and where competitors are winning. Without that context, teams optimize against a narrower picture.

"Can't we build our own prompt list?"

You can build a list. The harder part is knowing whether the list is complete, commercially meaningful, and aligned to how the market is actually behaving. That is where Semrush adds leverage.

"We care about outcomes, not more dashboards."

Exactly. The Semrush side improves the quality of prioritization. Adobe then helps connect those choices to traffic, engagement, and business results.

"Is this one unified product today?"

The cleanest answer is: it is a combined solution with complementary strengths. Semrush provides the market-intelligence layer; Adobe provides the optimization and proof layer.

"Do you have actual private prompts from ChatGPT?"

No. The value is not private log access. The value is strong market and intent signals that make prompt selection, competitive analysis, and prioritization more intelligent.

Best posture in objections: do not rush into feature defense. First identify whether the customer is questioning why this matters, whether it overlaps, or whether they can trust the signal.
