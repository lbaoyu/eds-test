Prompt Design Summary and Recommendations

The Card Services prompt set already provides a strong UOB-specific support experience. The best next-step improvements are to reduce overlap, simplify a small number of broader prompts, and tighten category boundaries so the set feels consistently focused on card servicing.

Overview

The prompt set already covers many of the most important card-servicing journeys, especially around fees, statements, payment support, declined transactions, disputes, and rewards.

The main opportunity is not to expand the category, but to make it more consistent. A smaller group of prompts overlaps heavily, combines multiple issues into one question, or feels more naturally aligned to account servicing, transfers, or digital banking support.

Summary of Recommendations

What is working well

  • The set addresses practical, day-to-day card support needs.
  • The strongest prompts are already clear, service-led, and easy to route consistently.
  • The category works well as a UOB-specific help experience.

Where to focus next

  • Merge overlapping fee-waiver prompts.
  • Rewrite a few prompts that bundle multiple issues into one question.
  • Move the clearest non-card prompts into more suitable servicing categories.
  • Review a small number of transfer and authentication prompts for category fit.

Branded vs. Unbranded

For Card Services, the recommended approach is to keep the prompt set mostly branded. This category behaves like a UOB card support experience, not a broad banking discovery journey.

Why branded works best here

  • Customers are usually asking about a specific UOB card, statement, bill payment, or fee.
  • Branded prompts improve clarity and reduce ambiguity.
  • They make routing and answer handling more consistent.
  • They reinforce that the experience is a UOB servicing flow, not a general banking guide.

What this means in practice

  • Use UOB by default in Card Services prompts.
  • Add clear card context where helpful, such as UOB credit card or UOB credit card statement.
  • Keep prompts operational and service-led.
  • If a prompt is really about transfers, account benefits, or digital banking support, move or review it instead of trying to brand it harder.

Bottom line

For this category, branded should be the standard and unbranded should be the exception. That is the cleanest fit for a UOB card FAQ.

Merge Overlapping Prompts

The clearest overlap appears in waiver-related intents and card-decline wording. These should be consolidated into stronger canonical prompts.

Annual Fee Waiver

Before
  • How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB?
  • How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?
  • Will UOB waive my annual fee if I ask?
After
How do I request an annual fee waiver for my UOB credit card?
These prompts all lead to the same servicing need and should be handled under one canonical intent.

Interest Waiver

Before
  • Can UOB waive the interest on my credit card?
  • How do I waive interest charges on my UOB credit card?
After
Can I request a waiver of interest charges on my UOB credit card?
Both prompts ask for the same outcome and should be consolidated.

Late Fee Waiver

Before
  • How do I waive my UOB late payment fee?
  • How do I waive UOB late charges?
After
Can I request a waiver of my UOB credit card late payment fee?
This is a single fee-waiver intent expressed in two ways and benefits from clearer card context.

Card Decline

Before
  • Why was my UOB credit card declined or why did my transaction fail?
  • My UOB card was declined — could it be due to my credit limit or daily limit?
After
Why was my UOB card transaction declined?
This creates one cleaner entry point for card-decline troubleshooting.

Rewrite Broader or Multi-Part Prompts

A small number of prompts would work better if they were simplified into a single clear customer need and kept tightly within the card-support context.

Before After Reason
What are the charges on my UOB statement — annual fee, late fee, or interest? Why are there fees or interest charges on my UOB credit card statement? Simplifies the question while keeping the issue clearly tied to a card statement.
How do I pay my UOB credit card bill — AXS, bank transfer, or TMRW app? How can I pay my UOB credit card bill? Lets the answer present the payment methods instead of hardcoding them into the prompt.
Why was my UOB bill payment or fund transfer declined? Why was my UOB credit card bill payment declined? Removes the transfer path and keeps the prompt aligned to Card Services.
I set up the UOB digital token but I still cannot make transfers or payments I set up my UOB digital token but still cannot complete a payment Makes the wording more focused, though this area may still need category review later.
I transferred money using UOB but the recipient has not received it yet Why has the recipient not received my transfer yet? Improves wording, though the prompt still feels closer to transfer support than card servicing.

Conclusion

The Card Services prompt set is already in a good place. The most valuable improvements are focused and practical: keep the strong branded service core, merge overlapping waiver intents, simplify a small number of broader prompts, and tighten category boundaries.

With those changes, the experience becomes clearer, easier to maintain, and more consistent as a customer-facing UOB card-support journey.
