Prompt Design Summary and Recommendations

The current prompt set has a strong foundation and already covers the key business banking needs that matter most to customers. The strongest opportunities now are to improve consistency, simplify overlap, and refine a small set of prompts that rely on subjective language.

Overview

The prompt set already addresses the right customer journeys across account opening, account selection, digital banking, payments, cross-border needs, financing, and trade. The core opportunity is not to add more content, but to make the existing content more structured and easier to guide consistently.

Most prompts are already fit for purpose. The main areas for improvement are concentrated in a smaller subset where prompts are either too subjective, partially overlapping, or more sensitive from a policy and experience-design perspective.

Summary of Recommendations

What is working well

  • Operational and product-led prompts are generally strong and usable as-is.
  • Coverage is broad enough to support both discovery and servicing needs.
  • UOB-specific prompts are particularly strong where they focus on process, product capability, or fees.

Where to focus next

  • Rewrite prompts using best, better, or good.
  • Merge only the most obvious overlapping discovery prompts.
  • Clarify where topics are intentionally branded, unbranded, or mixed.
  • Review a very small number of sensitive comparison and eligibility prompts before use.

Topics, Branded and Unbranded Considerations

Some topics are naturally stronger when they stay fully branded, while others can support a broader discovery style. The goal is not to force every topic into one format. The goal is to make each topic feel intentional.

Best kept mostly branded

These topics work best when the prompts stay clearly tied to UOB products, processes, or services.

  • Digital Banking and Access
  • Payments and Collections
  • Fees and Minimum Balance
  • Trade and Financing

Can support a mixed journey

These topics can work well with a broader discovery prompt followed by a more UOB-specific next step.

  • Account Opening and Eligibility
  • Business Account Comparison
  • Multi-currency and Cross-border

Recommended topic design principle

Keep a topic mostly branded when the answer depends on a UOB product, fee, process, or policy. Use a broader, more exploratory style only where it genuinely helps customers start with a need and then move naturally toward a UOB-specific solution.

Rewrite Subjective Prompts

These prompts are the clearest improvement area. The existing versions rely on subjective wording, while the recommended versions guide customers more clearly by focusing on suitability, features, or decision criteria.

Before
What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore?
Uses a superlative and invites broad ranking.
After
What digital business banking features should a business look for in Singapore?
Shifts to feature-based discovery and clearer decision support.
Before
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore?
The word "good" is vague and open-ended.
After
What type of business account is suitable for SMEs in Singapore?
Makes the prompt more precise and fit-based.
Before
What is the best business bank account in Singapore?
Too broad and implies a universal ranking.
After
What should a business consider when choosing a business bank account in Singapore?
Focuses on decision criteria instead of a one-size-fits-all answer.
Before

After

Keeps the customer need while making the answer more objective.

Conclusion

The prompt set is already in a strong position. The most valuable improvements are focused and manageable: retain the strong branded prompts, refine the subjective selection prompts, merge only the obvious overlaps, and make topic structure more intentional where broader discovery and UOB-specific guidance sit together.

With these changes, the set becomes clearer, easier to maintain, and more consistent as a customer-facing business banking experience.
