Storefront Strategy

A modern storefront designed for speed, discovery, and flexibility

For KrisShop, the strongest path is not one frontend pattern for every page. It is one cohesive storefront built from the right capabilities for each experience: Edge Delivery Services for experience-led pages, Commerce Storefront for shopping journeys, React for app-like tools, server-side rendering for discoverability, and Adobe LLM Optimizer support for AI-era product visibility.

View the recommended approach See what Commerce Storefront adds

What this approach delivers

The recommended model gives KrisShop a practical way to improve customer experience now while creating a stronger foundation for future growth.

  • Faster campaign and brand experiences
  • Stronger product discovery across search, listing, and detail pages
  • A safer phased path to modernize the storefront
  • Better readiness for AI-assisted product discovery

Why the storefront decision matters

KrisShop is not only deciding how pages are built. It is shaping how one storefront supports premium brand presentation, product discovery, shopping journeys, richer digital services, and future AI-driven discovery. The answer therefore needs to balance customer experience, content agility, commerce depth, and long-term flexibility.

Brand and campaign agility Editorial pages, seasonal launches, and premium retail moments need fast publishing and lightweight delivery.
Commerce performance Search, category, product, cart, and checkout pages need commerce-aware behavior and strong shopper flow.
Richer digital services Account tools, loyalty-linked experiences, and utility flows need application-style interaction only where it adds value.
Future discovery readiness High-value pages need to be understandable by both search engines and AI-driven discovery systems.

A clear solution approach

The most effective model is to match each part of the storefront to the capability it needs most. This creates one consistent customer experience without forcing one technology pattern to do every job.

Experience need Typical examples Best-fit solution Primary benefit
Experience-led pages Homepage, campaigns, landing pages, editorial and storytelling pages Edge Delivery Services Fast page delivery, strong content agility, and cleaner publishing workflows
Commerce-led journeys Search, PLP, PDP, minicart, cart, checkout Commerce Storefront Better product discovery, stronger shopping flows, and commerce-aware storefront behavior
App-like modules Account tools, loyalty-linked modules, dashboards, utilities React Rich interaction and workflow support where the experience behaves more like an application than a page
Discoverability-critical pages High-value product and category pages SSR / pre-render + Adobe LLM Optimizer Better search visibility, stronger rendered meaning, and better AI-readability
In simple terms: the recommendation is not "choose one technology over another." It is to use the right solution for the right customer experience while keeping the storefront unified.

What each part of the solution contributes

The model works because each capability solves a different customer-facing problem well. The goal is not one universal implementation pattern. The goal is to create a storefront that is easier to operate, easier to grow, and better aligned to the job each page needs to perform.

Edge Delivery Services

Best for experience-led publishing

  • Strong for campaigns, landing pages, storytelling, and fast-changing homepage content
  • Supports a lightweight, performance-first page model
  • Improves speed for content updates and iteration
  • Acts as the experience layer rather than the full shopping layer
Commerce Storefront

Best for shopping journeys

  • Supports search, product listing, product detail, cart, and checkout behavior
  • Aligns the storefront to products, categories, pricing, and merchandising
  • Creates more consistency across discovery and purchase paths
  • Provides a stronger modernization path than rebuilding every commerce page from scratch
React

Best for app-like interaction

  • Useful for dashboards, loyalty flows, profile centers, and utility-rich modules
  • Well suited to local state, task flows, and richer interaction
  • Strong where the experience behaves like a tool or service
  • Not necessary as the default answer for every storefront page

What Commerce Storefront adds

Commerce Storefront is more than a visual layer. It brings structured shopping behavior, product-aware rendering, and a clearer route to modernize the most valuable commerce journeys.

Ready-made shopping foundations

It gives KrisShop a structured starting point for core shopping journeys instead of requiring every flow to be custom-built.

  • Product listing pages
  • Product detail pages
  • Minicart and cart behavior
  • Checkout flows
  • Authentication and related account journeys

Product-aware rendering

It is designed around the mechanics of shopping rather than generic page presentation.

  • Category and product-driven layouts
  • Pricing-aware rendering
  • Search and discovery behavior
  • Merchandising-aligned experience patterns

Alignment to commerce services

It works with the services that shape product discovery and shopping experience quality.

  • Catalog-driven experiences
  • Search and discovery alignment
  • Recommendations support
  • Merchandising integration

More flexibility without full reinvention

KrisShop can adapt storefront behavior without turning every commerce page into a fully custom build.

  • Extension points for tailored journeys
  • Styling flexibility
  • Content enrichment options
  • Room for differentiated retail experiences

Better use of delivery effort

Using storefront foundations changes where engineering effort goes.

  • Less time spent recreating baseline shopping behavior
  • More time on differentiated brand and retail experiences
  • Cleaner focus on curated collections and premium merchandising
  • Lower pressure to solve every commerce page as a custom application build

A stronger route to modernization

It lets KrisShop improve commerce-facing surfaces without redesigning the whole digital estate at once.

  • Supports phased rollout
  • Improves high-value discovery surfaces first
  • Preserves flexibility for future backend evolution
  • Creates a clearer target architecture over time

Why experience delivery alone is not enough for shopping journeys

Edge Delivery Services is a strong answer for the experience layer, but shopping journeys need more than fast page delivery alone.

  • Commerce-led pages need product-aware composition, category-aware rendering, and shopper flow consistency
  • They also need alignment to search, merchandising, recommendations, cart, and checkout behavior
  • That is why Commerce Storefront should be treated as a distinct part of the overall solution

Why a blended model is stronger than a single-pattern approach

Each option becomes limiting when it is stretched beyond the problem it solves best. A blended model creates a better balance across customer experience, operational simplicity, and long-term flexibility.

Approach What it does well Where it becomes limiting
Experience-only Fast content-led publishing and strong campaign agility Does not by itself provide the full shopping behavior needed for search, product discovery, and conversion
