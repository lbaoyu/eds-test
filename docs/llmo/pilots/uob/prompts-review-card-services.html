Why Thin Topics Are a Problem

Problem Why it matters
Weak topic-level signal If many topics contain only 1 prompt, topic reporting becomes thin and less meaningful.
Harder navigation Users must scan many narrow topic names instead of a smaller set of intuitive buckets.
False sense of coverage A large topic count can look like broad coverage even when many topics are only minor variants.
Poor scalability As more prompts are added, the taxonomy can become noisy and harder to maintain consistently.
Weaker analysis quality Thin topics make it harder to compare performance, spot gaps, or prioritize areas for improvement.
The issue is not that the prompts are too similar. The issue is that the topic layer is too granular for the size of the library.

Prompt Quality Assessment

The prompts themselves are generally well structured. Payment timing, disputes, pending transactions, blocked transfers, login issues, and rewards questions are already separated in useful ways and should mostly remain as they are.

Merge Annual fee waiver process pair

How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB? How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?

These are effectively the same process question and should be consolidated.

How do I request a UOB credit card annual fee waiver?

Merge Late fee waiver pair

How do I waive my UOB late payment fee? How do I waive UOB late charges?

These are near-identical and should be reduced to one version.

How do I request a waiver for my UOB late payment fee?

Where the Taxonomy Is Too Thin

Current pattern Better structure
Separate topics for annual fee, interest, late fee, and waiver process Parent topic Waivers with subtopics or tags for fee type and process type
Multiple narrow payment topics Parent topic Payments with subtopics for due date, channels, charges, statement, and waiver flow
Multiple narrow transfer topics Parent topic Transfers with subtopics for PayNow, pending, and security hold
Many single-prompt topics across the library Use broader intent buckets and preserve specificity as metadata rather than topic names

Recommended Topic Model

A cleaner taxonomy would keep the prompts but reduce the number of top-level topics. A practical structure would likely be:

Cancellation Payments Waivers Transactions Transfers Self Service Rewards Promotion Eligibility
This would preserve coverage while making the library easier to browse, analyze, and scale. The recommended change is mainly to the topic taxonomy, not to the prompts themselves.

Final Assessment

Retain most prompts unchanged Merge only true duplicates Consolidate the topic taxonomy

The prompt set is in good shape. The more important issue is structural: for a 39-prompt library, 24 topics is likely too fragmented. The best improvement is to simplify the topic model into broader intent buckets and keep the narrower distinctions as subtopics or tags.
