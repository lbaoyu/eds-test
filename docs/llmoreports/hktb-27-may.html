Executive takeaway

HKTB is already the strongest named entity in the competitive set, but the more important signal is that DiscoverHongKong is functioning as a meaningful destination authority layer. The current gap is not "make the model say HKTB more often" in isolation; it is to make more generic Hong Kong travel journeys resolve to HKTB-owned pages even when the user never asks for the tourism board by name.

What changed in this readout

  • Brand visibility is now treated as a strict entity KPI, not the main success metric.
  • Owned citation coverage is elevated because destination publishers can win without brand-name mention.
  • Referral and agentic traffic matter more here than for a standard consumer brand because DiscoverHongKong is the product surface.
  • Generic destination prompts now matter more than branded or official-source prompts when judging growth headroom.

What HKTB is genuinely winning

  • Official-trust intent is a core strength. Prompts that ask for trusted or official guidance strongly favor HKTB and DiscoverHongKong.
  • Seasonal Travel & Events is the cleanest win zone, with 100% brand coverage, 100% citation coverage, and 100% owned URL coverage.
  • Where to stay and Food Discovery show stronger destination authority than raw brand coverage suggests, because citations appear more often than the HKTB name itself.
  • Owned capture is healthier than the brand KPI alone implies: among prompts that generated any citations, 73.9% included HKTB-owned URLs.

Where the current dashboard will understate performance

  • Generic Hong Kong travel prompts can still be strategically valuable even if the answer never says "HKTB."
  • Strict brand coverage at 38.6% should not be interpreted as "HKTB only wins 38.6% of travel demand." It only measures explicit brand surfacing.
  • 6 prompts generated citations without a brand mention, proving that destination authority and brand visibility diverge in this use case.
  • Destination comparison and first-time visitor queries remain the clearest headroom because those prompts often discuss Hong Kong without inviting an official tourism-board answer by default.

Referral and destination authority signals

  • 900 tracked referral visits arrived from LLM traffic in the uploaded referral table.
  • The referral URLs are almost entirely destination pages — homepage, events, neighbourhoods, food, tours, themed travel, and Muslim travel content.
  • This supports the core interpretation that DiscoverHongKong can create value even when the answer is framed around Hong Kong rather than HKTB.
  • Low bounce on most listed referral URLs suggests that once users do land on the site, the pages are broadly aligned with travel intent.

Localization and canonicalization opportunity

  • Several strong content concepts exist across multiple locale variants, which is useful for market coverage but can split authority signals.
  • Trip planner, index, event, and iconic-experience pages appear across multiple market paths.
  • Consolidate internal linking toward preferred locale-aware canonicals and reduce unnecessary redirect dependence.
  • Make alternate-language and alternate-market relationships explicit so machines understand they are coordinated, not competing pages.

Priority opportunities

Priority 1

Shift the KPI center from brand mention to destination authority

For this customer, the main success condition is that DiscoverHongKong pages become the preferred cited source for Hong Kong travel questions. Keep strict brand metrics, but do not use them alone to judge performance.

Priority 2

Own generic first-time visitor and comparison journeys

First-time visitor questions and Hong Kong-vs-other-city prompts have the widest gap between overall intent and HKTB capture. These should be treated as the main editorial expansion zones.

Priority 3

Turn destination hubs into canonical citation endpoints

Travel guide, attractions, events, neighbourhoods, and planning hubs already attract machine traffic and citations. These pages should be strengthened as stable, evergreen answer destinations with clearer structured guidance and fewer path variants.

Priority 4

Reduce crawl friction on high-demand travel pages

With meaningful 301, 403, and 404 share in the response mix, technical cleanup will improve the consistency with which models can fetch, parse, and trust HKTB content.

30-day action plan

  1. Split reporting into strict HKTB entity KPIs and destination authority KPIs.
  2. Build or strengthen generic Hong Kong planning pages for first-time visitors, itinerary building, and city comparisons.
  3. Expand where-to-stay and food decision pages so they can compete directly with publisher explainers.
  4. Reduce redirect, 403, and 404 friction on the most heavily machine-accessed URLs.
  5. Concentrate citation authority around travel-guide, attractions, events, neighbourhood, and trip-planner hubs.

Measurement to track next

  1. Strict brand coverage: still useful, but secondary to destination authority for this account.
  2. Owned URL coverage: improve beyond 29.8%.
  3. Owned citation share: move above 19.3%.
  4. Owned share of cited prompts: improve beyond 73.9%.
  5. Generic destination prompt visibility: improve the current 6.8% average on non-official prompts.
