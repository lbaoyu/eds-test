Section 1

Summary

The current prompt bank is well suited to a focused trial. It is tightly scoped, commercially relevant, and organized around meaningful business-banking use cases. The strongest aspect of the set is its concentration on one market and one business area, which helps preserve depth and makes early trendlines easier to interpret.

The main improvements are concentrated in a small number of places rather than spread across the whole bank. Specifically, one topic is currently broader than ideal, several prompts are more general than they need to be, and one small cluster of prompts could be made more distinct from one another.

Recommendation in one sentence: Keep the overall trial structure, but refine the broadest prompts and sharpen a few topic boundaries before or early in the trial.

Section 3

Key issues to address

The issues below are the most important ones to fix because they are the most likely to reduce clarity or create noisy results. They are also concentrated enough that they can be addressed without changing the overall structure of the trial.

1. One topic is broader than ideal

Business Account Basics currently combines several different types of "best account" and general fit questions. This makes it less precise than the other topics.

2. A few prompts are too general

Several prompts are broad enough to generate generic list-style answers instead of more focused and actionable outputs.

3. One small cluster overlaps

A few prompts in the digital banking area are close in meaning and could be made more distinct.

Issue Where it appears Why it matters Recommended response
Broad topic scope Business Account Basics A broad topic can make it harder to interpret results and decide what to improve. Narrow the topic focus or split out the broadest prompts.
Broad prompt wording Mostly in general account-selection prompts Very general prompts tend to produce more generic answers. Add more context such as business type, company size, or operational need.
Prompt overlap Digital banking / access cluster Overlapping prompts use up space that could go to more differentiated questions. Differentiate them by task, user need, or workflow.
Topic placement One cross-border account-choice prompt in comparison topic Cleaner topic grouping improves reporting clarity. Move it to the multi-currency / cross-border topic.
Important context for this trial: A more brand-forward prompt mix can be completely reasonable in a limited pilot. In this case, the more important issue is not the ratio itself, but whether the current mix is aligned to the trial goal and whether the broadest prompts are sharp enough.

Section 5

Specific prompt-level recommendations

The prompts below are the clearest candidates for revision because they are either broad, overlapping, or slightly less neutral than the rest of the set.

Current prompt What to improve Why Recommended direction
What is the best business bank account in Singapore? Make it more specific Very broad prompts often lead to generic answers that are harder to interpret. Add context such as:
  • for startups
  • for SMEs with international payments
  • for low-fee business banking needs
What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore? Narrow the use case This is currently a wide market-scan question rather than a focused test case. Reframe around a concrete need such as:
  • online onboarding
  • payment approvals
  • multi-user access
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore? Add business context The prompt is useful but still broad enough to invite generic answers. Specify a business profile, such as early-stage companies, overseas suppliers, or high transaction volume.
Which bank is best for small business banking in Singapore? Use selectively This can be a useful benchmark prompt, but it is broader than the rest of the set. Keep one such broad benchmark if desired, but avoid having several near-equivalents.
Why should I choose UOB for my business bank account? Clarify its purpose This is more persuasive in tone than the rest of the bank. Either keep it explicitly as a brand-perception prompt or rewrite it in a more neutral way.
Should I choose a local SGD business account or a multi-currency business account? Move to a better-fitting topic The prompt is strong; it simply fits better in cross-border account decisioning. Move it to Multi-currency and Cross-border.
What is UOB Infinity? Reduce overlap It overlaps with other general UOB Infinity prompts. Keep one broad introductory prompt, then use the remaining prompts for more specific workflows.
What can I do with UOB Infinity? Differentiate by workflow It is conceptually close to the other Infinity prompts. Refocus toward a clearer task such as approvals, payroll, bulk transfers, or administration.
Best editing principle: Replace broad prompts with narrower variants that reflect a clear user need, business type, or operating context.
Section 6

Recommended next steps

The most effective approach is to make a limited number of edits with high impact, rather than changing the whole set before the trial begins.

Priority 1

Tighten the broadest prompts

Focus first on the generic "best account" and "best digital banking options" prompts.

Priority 2

Sharpen one topic boundary

Refine the scope of Business Account Basics so it behaves more like a focused decision cluster.

Priority 3

Differentiate the digital banking cluster

Keep one broad product-introduction prompt and make the others more task-specific.
