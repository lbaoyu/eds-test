Section 3

Trial-aware interpretation of the branded mix

In a mature prompt program, a strongly branded mix could indicate weak generic discovery coverage. In a small trial, however, a higher branded ratio can be a deliberate and valid choice.

Why it can be valid

A limited trial often needs to concentrate prompts around the most business-critical, high-confidence visibility questions. That naturally can produce a more branded mix.

What it helps with

A brand-forward set is useful for establishing an initial baseline around priority brand presence, citations, and product/service recommendation patterns.

What it leaves out

The tradeoff is that the trial will say less about broader unbranded discovery, category capture, and competitor displacement in more generic search moments.

Question Assessment for this file
Is the high branded share automatically a flaw? No. In a limited pilot, it can be intentional and appropriate.
Is the current 76% branded share defensible? Yes, if the trial objective is baseline validation for UOB visibility in priority Singapore business-banking moments.
Would the mix be weaker for a broader GEO program? Yes. A mature or scaled program should likely add more unbranded category-discovery prompts.
What should be done now? Document the intent. Keep the mix if it matches the trial objective; expand unbranded coverage later if the scope broadens.
Revised portfolio principle: For a trial, the correct audit question is not "Does the bank match an ideal long-term branded/unbranded benchmark?" It is "Does this mix fit the trial objective and prompt budget?"

Section 7

Individual prompt review — targeted refinements

Most prompts are usable as-is. The highest-value changes are concentrated in a small subset of prompts that are broad, mildly overlapping, or slightly too leading.

Prompt Issue Why it matters Suggested direction
What is the best business bank account in Singapore? Too broad Likely to generate generic and variable answers with low diagnostic value. Narrow by segment or need, e.g. startups, exporters, firms with payroll needs, or SMEs with cross-border payments.
What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore? Too broad This behaves more like a wide market scan than a focused measurement prompt. Add business context, such as digital onboarding, payment approvals, or multi-user access needs.
Why should I choose UOB for my business bank account? Leading Still usable in a branded trial, but less neutral than the rest of the bank. If neutrality is important, rewrite as a comparison or selection prompt. If kept, treat it explicitly as a brand-perception baseline prompt.
What is UOB Infinity? Overlap Overlaps with adjacent Infinity prompts and may not add much unique signal alone. Keep only if you want a top-of-funnel brand explainer prompt. Otherwise, shift to more task-oriented variants.
What can I do with UOB Infinity? Overlap Conceptually close to the other Infinity prompts. Refocus on a concrete workflow such as approvals, bulk transfers, payroll, or collections administration.
Should I choose a local SGD business account or a multi-currency business account? Topic placement Good prompt, but more naturally belongs under the cross-border decision space. Move to Multi-currency and Cross-border or create a more explicit account-fit topic structure.
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore? Broad Useful, but could still produce generic list-style output. Narrow by company stage, payment volume, foreign currency needs, or startup vs established SME context.
Prompt-level recommendation: Revise a small set of broad prompts first. This preserves trial stability while improving signal quality where it matters most.
