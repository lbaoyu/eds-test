Overall Assessment

What the current usage tells us

Concise readout

Cathay's current LLMO setup is generating real signal, but that signal is concentrated in a relatively small share of the total prompt library. This is encouraging: it shows Cathay already has a workable set of high-value prompts, and the next phase should focus on making the program more precise, more locally relevant, and easier to scale.

In practice, the strongest prompts are the ones that sound closest to genuine customer questions. They are specific, decision-oriented, and tied to clear service or product moments. The weaker areas are broader, more generic, or less consistently structured.

What is working

Current strengths

Positive signals
  • High-intent prompts are performing best, especially those tied to card choice, card usage, fees, and app interactions.
  • Brand and service-oriented prompts are driving the clearest visibility, which is a strong sign that Cathay can win where customer intent is well defined.
  • The current level of usage is already sufficient to identify repeatable winners and create a focused optimization roadmap.
  • Card-related journeys are a clear anchor for future expansion because they already show meaningful traction.
CUBE card Overseas card usage Annual fee questions CUBE app tasks

What needs attention

Highest-priority gaps

Priority fixes
  • Market setup is misaligned: the library is using US instead of TW.
  • Performance is concentrated: only part of the completed prompt set is generating meaningful mentions.
  • Prompt structure is inconsistent: taxonomy and labeling are not always clean enough for strong reporting discipline.
  • Coverage is uneven: cards are well represented, while adjacent banking journeys have lighter coverage.
Most urgent action: correcting the market from US to TW should come first. It is foundational to improving relevance, customer language alignment, and competitive context.
Usage quality

What the prompt mix suggests

Where to focus next

Cathay does not appear to have a quantity problem. The prompt library is already large enough. The main issue is that the strongest results come from a narrower, better-shaped subset, while the rest of the library adds less signal than expected.

This points to a straightforward next step: move from prompt expansion to prompt refinement. The priority should be to keep the winning themes, improve prompt wording and structure, remove weaker or overly broad prompts, and then selectively expand into the next most important banking journeys.

Bottom line

Customer-facing summary

Final takeaway

Cathay United Bank has already built a credible LLMO foundation with clear early wins. The immediate opportunity is to make the program more locally relevant, more structured, and more strategically focused.

  • Keep and strengthen the prompts already working in cards, overseas usage, fees, and app tasks.
  • Fix the market setup so the program reflects Taiwan rather than the US.
  • Shift from volume to quality by refining the core prompt set and reducing noise.

With those adjustments, Cathay should be well positioned to turn today's early traction into a stronger and more scalable visibility program.
