Bottom-line summary

The uploaded bank is good enough to run as a trial. The issues are concentrated rather than widespread. The bank does not need a redesign. It needs a small set of deliberate refinements in the places where measurement quality is most likely to be noisy.

What is strong
  • Clear single-market focus: Singapore business banking.
  • Balanced topic counts: 5–7 prompts per topic.
  • Good coverage of opening, fees, payments, cross-border, and financing.
  • Strong fit for a constrained trial where depth matters more than breadth.
What needs attention
  • The topic Business Account Basics is too broad.
  • A few prompts are generic list-style prompts that will likely produce noisy answers.
  • The UOB Infinity cluster has mild overlap.
  • The category label is internal-facing.
What not to over-correct
  • The branded-heavy ratio is not automatically a flaw because this is a trial.
  • The current topic distribution is mostly sound.
  • The bank already has enough structure to establish a first baseline.
  • The right move is selective refinement, not wholesale rebalance.
Recommended stance: Preserve the current trial architecture, but tighten the broadest prompts and clean up the most obvious overlap points before measurement starts.

Section 3

Category-level finding

There is only one category in the file. That is a strength in a limited trial because it concentrates prompt budget on one business line. The issue is not scope; the issue is label clarity.

Issue: category label is internal-facing

Current label: GWB Business Banking

Why this is an issue: "GWB" is likely meaningful to internal teams, but it weakens clarity in an audit, report, or customer-facing review. Category labels work better when they are explicit, external, and self-explanatory.

Recommendation: Rename the category to one of the following:

  • Business Banking Singapore
  • SME Business Banking Singapore
  • Singapore Business Accounts and Banking
Not an issue: Having just one category is appropriate here. For a 50-prompt trial, this is a positive design choice.

Section 5

Prompt-set findings

At portfolio level, the most important nuance is that this is a trial set. That changes how the branded mix should be interpreted.

What is not a problem

High branded share can be intentional

76% of the prompts mention UOB. In a mature long-term program that might signal weak generic discovery coverage. In a small trial, however, it can be a deliberate strategy to establish a baseline around priority branded questions.

That means the branded mix is not automatically an audit failure.

What still needs clarification

The intent should be explicit

The issue is not the ratio itself. The issue is whether the ratio is intentional and documented. If the trial goal is branded baseline measurement, the current ratio is defensible. If the goal is broader generic discovery coverage, the bank would need more unbranded prompts in a later phase.

Section 6

Exact prompt-level issues, rationale, and recommendations

This is the most direct section for editing. Each issue below identifies the exact prompt, the specific problem, why that problem matters, and what to do next.

Prompt Issue Specific rationale Recommendation
What is the best business bank account in Singapore? Too broad This prompt is so generic that the model can answer from many different frames: startup banking, SME banking, fee sensitivity, digital onboarding, cross-border needs, or pure reputation. That makes the output less stable and less actionable. Rewrite with a narrower frame, e.g.:
  • What is the best business bank account in Singapore for startups?
  • What is the best business bank account in Singapore for SMEs with international payments?
  • What is the best low-fee business bank account for small companies in Singapore?
What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore? Too broad This behaves like a generic category scan. It lacks user context, company type, or operational need. Results may be unstable and broad rather than diagnostic. Anchor it to a use case, e.g.:
  • Which digital business banking platforms in Singapore are best for online account opening?
  • Which digital business banking options in Singapore are best for SMEs with maker-checker approvals?
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore? Still broad Better than "best bank account," but still wide enough to produce list-style responses without clearly isolating one need state. Add decision context:
  • What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore that need low fees?
  • What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore with overseas suppliers?
Which bank is best for small business banking in Singapore? Broad comparative prompt Strong commercial intent, but still broad enough to produce generic bank-ranking outputs. Keep only if you want one broad market benchmark prompt. Otherwise, narrow by business type or requirement.
Why should I choose UOB for my business bank account? Leading prompt This prompt strongly favors a positive UOB framing. It is less neutral than the rest of the bank, which makes it weaker for comparative measurement. Two valid options:
  • Keep it if the purpose is explicit brand-perception baseline.
  • Rewrite it more neutrally, e.g. "What should SMEs compare when choosing a business bank account in Singapore?"
Should I choose a local SGD business account or a multi-currency business account? Right prompt, wrong topic This is a good, specific decision prompt. The problem is classification: it is currently more aligned to cross-border needs than general comparison. Move it from Business Account Comparison to Multi-currency and Cross-border.
What is UOB Infinity? Overlap This is a valid explainer prompt, but it overlaps with "What can I do with UOB Infinity?" and "How do I use UOB Infinity for business banking?" Keep only one broad explainer prompt, then use the rest of the prompt budget for workflow-level prompts.
What can I do with UOB Infinity? Overlap This is broader than needed and semantically close to the other Infinity prompts. Replace with a more differentiated task prompt, e.g.:
  • Can UOB Infinity support multi-user approvals for business payments?
  • How do I use UOB Infinity for bulk payroll or supplier payments?
