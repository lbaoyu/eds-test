Executive takeaway

KBank already leads the competitive set in every tracked topic, but its owned surfaces are under-captured relative to the total citation market. In plain terms: KBank is being talked about more than rivals, yet third-party pages still do most of the explaining for generic finance and comparison prompts.

#1 share of voice in 6/6 topics Branded prompt visibility: 93.7% Non-branded prompt visibility: 38.1% Only 50.0% of prompts cite owned KBank pages

What this means

  • Brand demand is healthy: branded prompts are nearly fully captured.
  • Generic discovery is the real unlock: unbranded prompts drive the largest headroom.
  • Citation capture is fragmented: K PLUS and credit-card authority is split across duplicate URL variants.
  • Third-party explainers set the narrative in fund education, card comparisons, and merchant-promo queries.

Readout: KBank ranks #1 in all six topics. The strongest quality zone is Deals & application intent at 72.4% average visibility, while the weakest discoverability sits in new investment ideas and eligibility & lifestyle fit, both below 30%.

Where KBank is strongest

  • Credit card promotions and product-fit prompts are the clearest win zone, including cashback, restaurant deals, online shopping promotions, and card-tier reviews.
  • Deals & application intent shows the highest average visibility at 72.4%, with full prompt coverage and 39 owned citations across just 8 prompts.
  • Action & conversion prompts are also fully visible, which means KBank is consistently present when users ask how to start or where to transact.
  • Sentiment is healthy: visible mentions are 60.8% positive, 39.2% neutral, and 0% negative in the analyzed prompt set.

Where KBank is leaking opportunity

  • Generic comparison prompts such as no-annual-fee cards, cashback comparisons, and points mechanics have multiple 0% visibility examples.
  • Fund education gaps appear in Thai ESG, ETF basics, money-market explainers, REIT ideas, and China/global fund selection.
  • Eligibility and credit basics underperform on prompts like why an application was rejected, what minimum payment means, and card-vs-cash-card differences.
  • Merchant-specific offer intent can be lost to competitors when the query anchors on a partner brand such as Trip.com or Starbucks.

Canonicalization opportunity

Several high-value pages appear multiple times because of path and casing variants. That likely dilutes citation consolidation and weakens a single canonical authority signal.

  • https://www.kasikornbank.com/th/personal/digital-banking/pages/kplus.aspx appears in 4 URL variants and accumulates 32 citations when normalized.
  • https://www.kasikornbank.com/th/personal/creditcard/pages/creditcard.aspx appears in 4 URL variants and accumulates 23 citations when normalized.
  • https://www.kasikornbank.com/en/personal/digital-banking/pages/kplus.aspx appears in 4 URL variants and accumulates 21 citations when normalized.

Recommended fix

  • Standardize one canonical URL for each major K PLUS and credit-card hub.
  • Harmonize uppercase/lowercase path variants and redirect deprecated forms.
  • Concentrate internal links, structured data, and promotion modules on canonical hubs.
  • Use those hubs as the primary citation targets from supporting article and promo pages.

Priority opportunities

Priority 1

Own generic card-comparison explainers

Build or strengthen pages for no-annual-fee cards, cashback logic, points mechanics, and merchant-benefit comparisons. Several prompts in this cluster currently sit at 0% visibility while publishers such as CardGuruTH, th.my-best, SiamLanCard, and KTC capture the citations.

Priority 2

Expand fund education for Thai ESG, ETF, REIT, and global funds

KBank leads share of voice overall, but specific educational prompts still miss. Thai ESG and REIT prompts show 0% visibility examples while SCBAM, Bangkok Bank, Navtable, Principal, and market-news sources are being cited instead.

Priority 3

Turn action intent into owned conversion paths

Action/conversion prompts already surface KBank consistently, but owned citation capture is still modest. Add clearer eligibility, required-docs, approval-speed, and apply-now modules directly inside K PLUS and credit-card hub pages.

Priority 4

Close the eligibility and "credit basics" content gap

Prompts about failed applications, minimum payment, and card-vs-cash-card basics are weak or absent. These are high-intent moments where KBank can replace generic finance explainers with authoritative owned guidance.

30-day action plan

  1. Canonicalize the top K PLUS and credit-card hubs and redirect case/path duplicates.
  2. Publish a generic comparison cluster for cashback, points, no-fee, Starbucks, and travel-booking prompts.
  3. Launch fund explainer pages for Thai ESG, ETF types, REIT selection, and global diversification.
  4. Add FAQ blocks tuned to Thai-language eligibility and troubleshooting prompts.
  5. Reinforce internal linking from promo pages to stable evergreen hubs that should become citation anchors.

Measurement to track next

  1. Owned citation share: move above 8.7%.
  2. Non-branded visibility: improve from 38.1%.
  3. Position quality: expand first-position wins beyond 26.6% of visible prompts.
  4. Zero-visibility prompt count: reduce across comparison, education, and eligibility themes.
  5. Weekly trend stability: reverse the wk19 → wk21 visibility softening.
