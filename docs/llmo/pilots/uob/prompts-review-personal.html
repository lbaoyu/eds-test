Executive Summary

Overall assessment

This is a well-organized prompt bank with a clear category, disciplined topic sizing, and a strong focus on the Singapore savings-account market. It is particularly strong in comparison prompts, interest-maximisation scenarios, and product-specific UOB One / UOB Stash depth.

The main opportunities are not structural failure points. They are mostly quality and balance opportunities: a high branded concentration outside the discovery topic, several prompts that read more like labels than natural customer questions, some overlap in comparison families, and lighter coverage in onboarding, activation, and broader post-selection customer journeys.

Strengths

What this prompt bank already does well

1. Strong top-level discipline

The bank has one clear category and five balanced topics. That makes it easy to interpret results by cluster and avoids the fragmentation problem seen in weaker prompt sets.

2. Good market realism

The prompts reflect realistic Singapore savings-account behavior: salary crediting, card spend, emergency funds, fixed deposits, T-bills, and account comparison against local competitors.

3. Strong product-depth where it matters

The UOB One Account and UOB Stash Account topics provide meaningful product depth, especially around qualification logic, interest mechanics, and common product comparisons.

4. Good comparison coverage

The set does a good job representing comparison moments across DBS Multiplier, OCBC 360, Standard Chartered Bonus$aver, fixed deposits, and T-bills.

5. Useful discovery layer exists

The "Best Savings Accounts in Singapore" topic gives the bank a meaningful unbranded discovery layer, which is valuable for category-level visibility testing.

6. Even topic distribution

Twenty prompts per topic creates a neat baseline for comparison and prevents one topic from being accidentally under-represented.

Key Findings

Main issues and opportunities

Branded versus unbranded coverage is too concentrated

The overall mix is 80 branded and 20 unbranded. That is not automatically wrong for a trial, but almost all unbranded discovery is concentrated in a single topic. The remaining topics are overwhelmingly branded.

  • Best Savings Accounts in Singapore: 19 unbranded, 1 branded
  • Comparison & Alternatives: 19 branded, 1 unbranded
  • Maximising Interest & Eligibility: 20 branded
  • UOB One Account: 20 branded
  • UOB Stash Account: 20 branded

This means category-level discoverability is represented, but it is not distributed across the broader bank. If the trial goal includes broader market visibility, the current design may overconcentrate that learning in only one topic.

Prompt quality is mostly solid, but several prompts read like labels rather than customer questions

A number of prompts use comparison-label phrasing such as "Compare between..." or noun-phrase styles such as "UOB One Account compared with fixed deposit rates...". These are understandable, but they are less natural than a direct customer question.

This matters because the best prompts usually sound like something a real customer would say spontaneously, not something written as a worksheet heading.

Comparison coverage is strong, but some comparison families are too dense

The bank includes many useful comparison prompts, but several clusters are close to each other in meaning. This is especially visible around:

  • UOB One vs DBS Multiplier
  • UOB One vs OCBC 360
  • UOB One / UOB Stash vs fixed deposit
  • UOB One vs UOB Stash

This is not duplication in the strict sense, but it does create some density in one intent family while other parts of the journey are less covered.

Journey coverage is strongest in discovery, comparison, and optimization — lighter in onboarding and ongoing service

The bank is very strong in the early and middle parts of the journey: category research, product comparison, and how to maximize interest. It is lighter in:

  • application and opening steps
  • activation and account setup
  • ongoing account management beyond interest optimization
  • service scenarios such as changing account usage patterns, missing criteria, or account maintenance actions

The bank does include some opening and setup prompts, but they are scattered rather than clearly structured.

One topic-placement issue stands out

"How to open a UOB joint account" appears inside Best Savings Accounts in Singapore. That prompt is more operational and product-action oriented than the rest of that topic, so it feels slightly misplaced.

A few prompts are too vague or too time-sensitive

Most prompts are usable, but a handful would benefit from being sharpened. Examples:

  • Is UOB Stash Account good? — too vague
  • Walk me through the exact tiers of the UOB One Account interest structure as of 2026. — overly time-bound and likely to age quickly
  • Compare UOB Stash Account with fixed deposit — understandable but not natural as a customer question

Recommendations

Priority recommendations

1. Spread unbranded discovery more deliberately

Keep the strong discovery topic, but add a small number of unbranded prompts into comparison and decision-oriented areas so category-level learning is not isolated in only one topic.

2. Rewrite label-style prompts into natural questions

Replace prompts that begin with Compare, noun-phrase labels, or overly scripted wording with direct customer-style questions.

3. Reduce density in repeated comparison families

Keep the strongest comparison prompts, but use some freed-up slots to cover underrepresented onboarding, setup, and account-use scenarios.

4. Strengthen journey coverage

Add more prompts for decision-stage and post-selection scenarios, such as opening steps, setup actions, and maintaining eligibility over time.

5. Re-home misplaced prompts

Move operational prompts like joint-account opening into a more suitable topic structure, rather than leaving them inside broad discovery topics.

6. Remove or update time-sensitive phrasing

Where possible, avoid prompts that hard-code time references unless that is the explicit purpose of the test.

Final View

Conclusion

This is a strong starting prompt bank for the Personal Savings & Deposit Accounts category in Singapore. It already shows discipline in structure, market realism, and product-specific depth. The biggest opportunity is to make the bank more balanced as a learning instrument: less concentrated in branded product mechanics, slightly less dense in repeated comparisons, and more complete across the customer journey.

With a focused refinement pass, this bank can become not only structurally strong, but also more representative of how customers discover, compare, choose, open, and use savings accounts in practice.
