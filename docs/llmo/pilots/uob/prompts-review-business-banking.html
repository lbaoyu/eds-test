UOB Business Banking Prompt Quality Review

This review covers all meaningful prompt-quality outcomes — not just merge or consolidation. The goal is to make only real, defensible suggestions. If a prompt is already valid, realistic, and useful, it should stay unchanged. A change is recommended only when it clearly improves prompt quality, removes redundancy, or sharpens intent without changing the underlying user need.

Scope: prompt quality, not just dedupe Region: Singapore Prepared: 2026-06-26 Review posture: conservative

Review Position

This report uses a deliberately conservative standard. It does not assume that prompts using words like best or good automatically need rewriting, and it does not treat same-topic prompts as duplicates by default.

Keep as-is For prompts that are already realistic and well-formed
Light edit Only where wording can be tightened without changing intent
Merge Only for true duplicates
No forced rewrites If a prompt is already valid, leave it alone
Core principle: suggest a change only when it is a real, valid improvement. The review should be additive and selective, not corrective by default.

How Suggestions Are Decided

Keep as-is when

  • The prompt reflects a realistic customer question.
  • The wording already matches a valid comparison or decision intent.
  • No change would materially improve clarity, specificity, or coverage.

Suggest a light edit when

  • The prompt is valid, but a small wording change would make it cleaner.
  • The intent can be preserved while improving precision.
  • The prompt is slightly broader or vaguer than it needs to be.

Suggest a merge when

  • Two prompts are essentially the same user question.
  • Keeping both does not add distinct segment, product, or use-case coverage.
What this report avoids: over-rewriting, over-deduping, and changing prompt intent just to make the set look more standardized.

These are the clearest true duplicates in the reviewed subset. They are both branded account-selection prompts for the same payment need.

Suggested merged version: Which UOB business account is best for cross-border or international payments?

Keep as-is Broad digital comparison prompt

What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore?

This is a realistic, valid prompt. It should not be rewritten simply because it uses the word best. It already expresses a clear comparison intent.

Optional light edit Broad generic business account prompt

What is the best business bank account in Singapore?

This is still a valid prompt. A change is optional, not required. If a cleaner version is preferred, the goal should be to make the decision frame slightly clearer without changing the core intent.

Optional alternative: Which business bank accounts in Singapore are best suited to different business needs?

Prompts That Should Stay Separate

These prompts are related by topic, but they still represent different coverage and should not be merged.

Prompt Why it should stay separate
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore? Distinct SME segment coverage.
Which bank is best for small business banking in Singapore? Provider-level framing; broader than a single account-selection prompt.
Which business bank account is best for startups in Singapore? Distinct startup segment.
Which UOB business account is better for an established SME? Branded plus established-SME segment; different from generic SME or startup prompts.
Which Singapore business bank account has low fees? Fee-led selection criterion.
What is the best business account for USD transactions in Singapore? Currency-specific operational need.
Which Singapore bank is best for multi-currency or cross-border business banking? Broader international banking capability than USD-only or payment-specific prompts.
What is the best bank for trade finance for SMEs in Singapore? Trade finance is its own decision area, not just a business-account variant.
