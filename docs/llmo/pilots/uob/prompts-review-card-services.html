Section 1

Summary

The current prompt bank covers many important card-service customer needs, including payments, waivers, disputes, transaction issues, login and digital access, transfers, and cancellation scenarios. The overall subject matter is strong and clearly grounded in real service journeys.

The main issue is not the prompt ideas themselves. The main issue is structure. For a 39-prompt trial, the bank is split into 24 topics, which makes the library much more granular than necessary. Many topics contain only one prompt, which reduces depth and makes the topic structure harder to interpret.

Recommendation in one sentence: Keep the current prompt themes, but consolidate the topic structure so that each topic represents a stronger cluster of related customer questions.

Section 3

Key issues to address

The primary opportunity is structural. The bank contains many useful prompt ideas, but the topic design is too fragmented for the size of the prompt set.

1. Topic structure is too granular

The bank has 24 topics for 39 prompts, and many of those topics contain only a single prompt.

2. Several topics are sub-cases, not standalone topics

Some topics are narrow variations of larger themes, such as cancellation, payments, waivers, and transfers.

3. A few prompts overlap in meaning

There are a small number of prompts that are close enough in purpose that they should be consolidated or differentiated.

Issue Where it appears Why it matters Recommended response
Too many singleton topics Across cancellation, payments, transfers, self service, and rewards Topics with only one prompt often do not create strong enough clusters for interpretation. Merge narrow sub-topics into broader topic families.
Over-segmentation Examples include Payments — Interim Statement, Payments — Replaced Card, Payments — Channels, Transfers — PayNow Over-segmentation makes the bank look more complex without adding enough analytical value. Group closely related prompts under broader themes such as Payments, Transfers, and Self Service.
Prompt overlap Waivers — Annual Fee, Waivers — Late Fee, and some transaction-support prompts Near-duplicate prompts use up space that could be spent on more differentiated questions. Consolidate similar prompts or make each one more distinct in scope.
One-off edge topics Rewards — ONE Account, Cancellation — Waiver Rejected These may be valid prompts, but they are too narrow to stand as separate topics in a small trial set. Move them into broader parent topics rather than keeping them as standalone topics.
Important context for this trial: The issue is not that the bank covers too many service themes. The issue is that the current themes are divided into too many small topic buckets for the size of the prompt set.
Section 4

Topic-by-topic review

The strongest improvement would come from simplifying the topic model. Several topics are already strong enough in concept, but many would work better if merged into larger, more durable topic clusters.

Current topic pattern Assessment Specific rationale Recommendation
Payments — Before Payment Strong This is a clear, coherent topic focused on balance, due date, and minimum payment questions. Keep as a core payment topic.
Waivers — Annual Fee / Late Fee / Interest Useful, but can be simplified The waiver prompts are relevant, but the topic structure could be more compact while preserving the prompt distinctions. Consider one broader Waivers and Charges topic with sub-coverage inside it.
Transactions — Disputes / Pending / Decline / Failed / Promotion Eligibility Too fragmented These are all valid transaction-related themes, but splitting them into many small topics creates unnecessary fragmentation. Merge into 2–3 stronger groups, such as Transaction Problems, Disputes and Unauthorised Charges, and Rewards Eligibility.
Transfers — PayNow / Pending / Security Hold Too granular Each of these currently has only one prompt. They are better treated as one transfer-related cluster. Combine into a single Transfers topic.
Self Service — Digital Token / Login / Singpass Strong theme, fragmented execution The underlying theme is strong, but login, token, and verification do not need separate one-prompt topics. Merge into a single App Access and Verification topic.
Cancellation / Cancellation — Instalments / Cancellation — Waiver Rejected Too granular These are cancellation-related scenarios, but they are too narrow to stand alone in a 39-prompt trial. Merge into one broader Cancellation and Account Closure topic.
Rewards — ONE Account Useful but isolated This is relevant, but as a single-prompt topic it sits better under a broader rewards or eligibility topic. Move into a larger Rewards and Eligibility topic.
Suggested target structure: Instead of 24 topics, aim for something closer to 8–12 stronger topics for a 39-prompt trial set.
Section 5

Specific prompt-level recommendations

Most prompts are realistic and usable. The main prompt-level opportunity is not rewriting everything, but reducing overlap and making sure each prompt earns its place.

Current prompt What to improve Why Recommended direction
How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB?
How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?		 Reduce overlap These prompts are very close in meaning and may not provide enough differentiated value as separate items. Keep one broader version, or rewrite the second to focus on process, timing, or eligibility.
How do I waive my UOB late payment fee?
How do I waive UOB late charges?		 Consolidate or differentiate These are effectively the same question with minor wording variation. Keep one, or make one more specific to process, turnaround time, or approval criteria.
Why was my UOB bill payment or fund transfer declined?
Why was my UOB credit card declined or why did my transaction fail?		 Clarify scope These are related but currently blur payment decline, transfer decline, and transaction failure into overlapping problem areas. Separate one into card transaction failure and the other into bank transfer or bill payment failure.
What is the difference between my UOB digital token PIN, SMS OTP, and card PIN? Keep as a differentiator This is a useful educational prompt and adds distinct value within the app-access theme. Keep as is.
My UOB annual fee waiver was rejected — what are my options? Move into a broader topic The prompt is valid, but the topic around it is too narrow as a standalone branch. Keep the prompt, but place it within a broader waivers topic.
I got a replacement UOB card — do I pay to the old card number or new card number? Keep, but regroup This is a strong service prompt, though too narrow to justify a standalone topic. Move under a broader payments topic.
Which UOB account is linked to my PayNow and which account receives transfers? Keep, but regroup Useful and specific, but topic structure is too granular. Move under a consolidated transfers topic.
Why hasn't my UOB ONE Account bonus interest been credited this month? Keep, but align better A valid question, but it sits awkwardly between rewards and account benefit eligibility themes. Place under a broader rewards / eligibility cluster.
Best editing principle: Keep strong prompt ideas, but reduce low-value wording variants and make each prompt more clearly distinct in purpose.
Section 6

Recommended next steps

The most effective next step is to simplify the topic model first. That will improve the structure of the bank without requiring a major rewrite of the prompt content itself.

Priority 1

Consolidate the topic structure

Reduce the number of topics by grouping narrow sub-cases into larger service themes.

Priority 2

Remove low-value wording variants

Where two prompts ask nearly the same thing, keep one or differentiate them more clearly.

Priority 3

Keep the service scope intact

The coverage itself is good; the main change needed is structure, not scope.

Priority Action Reason Expected benefit
High Reduce the topic count from 24 to a more compact set of broader service themes Too many singleton topics weaken the structure of the bank Stronger topic clusters and cleaner reporting

Final recommendation: Keep the current prompt themes, simplify the topic architecture, remove the most obvious wording duplicates, and then hold the bank stable long enough to establish a useful first baseline.
