B2 Two real gaps ◐ Indicative

Two questions sit behind the data. When people name Cathay, how is the AI talking about it? When they don't, is the AI choosing it at all? The answers point in different directions.

Central insight
Even when a user asks for Cathay or Cube by name, the bank shows up only 43.5% of the time — 32 of 78 such prompts return nothing. This isn't about wording. The bank's own pages aren't readable enough for the AI to pull them, even on request.

"Is AI choosing us at all?" is the bigger prize, since it's where new customers begin; for now, read it as indicative given the US market setting.

Part C · Action
What to do
The work the trial points to, in priority order. All of it is content and technical work on Cathay's own pages — the levers that move AI visibility regardless of the trial settings.
C1 The plan

Three priorities first, then the full list.

01

Make the Cube card pages AI-readable

/cards/cube and /cards/cube-list draw 6,700+ visits at 2–4% readability. A server-side-rendered or static, AI-readable version of the flagship pages lifts every downstream number — mentions, citations, share of voice. The single highest-leverage move.

02

Strengthen the Cube answer — owned and earned

Comparison sites (winvest, creditcards.com.tw, rich01, money101) do most of the citing on the Cube card, and largely favourably. The goal isn't to take that away, but to add a clear, AI-readable owned page the model can draw on directly — while keeping the earned coverage accurate and positive.

03

Show up on the generic questions

The decision-stage questions — "best cashback card", "card with airport pickup", "推薦信用卡清單" — are where prospects begin and Cathay is largely absent today. Answering them clearly on owned pages is where new-customer reach is won.
