1 • Product frame

What Adobe Brand Visibility means in practice

The easiest way to understand the product is to separate market intelligence from owned-surface activation and proof. Once that split is clear, the rest of the story stops feeling abstract.

Semrush AIO helps teams understand what is happening in the market and why: which prompts matter, where competitors are winning, which topics are under-covered, and how the brand is showing up across AI surfaces.

Adobe helps teams understand what to change on owned experiences and whether it worked: opportunity surfacing, optimization, monitoring, and connection to traffic or business outcomes.
Semrush role
Discovery and prioritization

It helps answer: "What matters in the market?" and "Where are we losing?"

Adobe role
Activation and proof

It helps answer: "What should we change first?" and "Did the work create impact?"

Best AIO output
Strategic clarity

Good AIO use results in smarter prioritization, not just another dashboard.

Best LLMO output
Operational evidence

Good LLMO use results in a tighter link between work performed and outcomes observed.

Simple teaching sentence: If someone is new to the space, say: "Semrush helps decide what deserves attention. Adobe helps improve it and prove it."

Example: imagine a brand discovers through AIO that it is rarely cited for prompts around "best AI image generator for commercial use," while two competitors are cited frequently. That is a market intelligence finding. Adobe then helps the team decide which owned pages to improve, whether to serve AI-friendly summaries at the edge, whether the source content needs revision, and whether those changes later show up in monitored prompt performance, agentic traffic, referral traffic, or conversions.

2 • Mental models

The six mental models that make everything else easier

People usually struggle because they try to memorize features before they understand the shape of the problem. These six mental models are the shape of the problem.

Mental model 1

This is not just SEO with a new label

SEO traditionally centers on search rankings and page performance. This category centers on AI answers: mentions, citations, answer framing, recommendation patterns, and visibility inside generative experiences.

Example: a page might not be the top organic result, yet the brand could still be cited positively in an AI answer. That is a different kind of win.

Mental model 2

Semrush AIO is the outside-in layer

Think of Semrush as the system that looks outward: market demand, prompt patterns, competitor strength, topic opportunity, sentiment, and share of voice.

Example: "Which non-branded prompts are competitors winning that we are missing?" is an outside-in question.

Mental model 3

Adobe is the inside-out layer

Think of Adobe as the system that looks inward and forward: which pages matter, which opportunities exist, what can be changed at edge or source, and what outcomes follow.

Example: "Which owned pages should we optimize first and did agentic traffic improve?" is an inside-out question.

Mental model 4

Prompt quality determines signal quality

The quality of any visibility score depends on the quality of the prompts behind it. A weak prompt set creates false confidence. A thoughtful prompt set creates actionable signal.

Example: a prompt set containing only broad informational prompts may show visibility, but miss the high-value commercial prompts that actually influence pipeline or conversion.

Mental model 5

Think in loops, not dashboards

Good teams do not just inspect visibility. They use it to decide what to do next. The real loop is discover → prioritize → activate → measure → repeat.

Example: if a dashboard shows a competitor gap but no workflow changes follow, the dashboard created awareness but not value.

Mental model 6

Keep market and operational metrics separate

AIO and LLMO are both useful, but they are not the same measuring stick. One is broader and strategic. The other is narrower and operational.

Example: a customer may see LLMO performance improve before broader AIO share-of-voice movement becomes visible. That is normal, not contradictory.

3 • Core terms

Vocabulary that matters — with plain-English meaning and examples

Most confusion in this space comes from people mixing levels of analysis. This glossary is designed to stop that.

Term Plain meaning Why it matters Example
AIO The Semrush intelligence side for AI visibility analysis. Used for market discovery, prompt research, competitor context, and strategic prioritization. "Which prompts are we missing across the category?"
GEO Generative Engine Optimization. The broader discipline of improving how brands appear in AI-generated answers. Improving citations, answer coverage, and discoverability in LLM-driven experiences.
Prompt A user question or query pattern. The core unit behind prompt research and prompt tracking. "What is the best PDF editor for teams?"
Prompt set The curated collection of prompts the team decides to monitor. This defines the operational measurement frame. A mix of branded, non-branded, comparison, and commercial prompts.
Topic A cluster of related prompts. Useful for prioritization and strategy, because teams usually optimize themes, not isolated questions. "Commercial AI image generation" could group many related prompts.
Mention The AI answer names the brand. Basic visibility signal. An answer says "Adobe Firefly is one option."
Citation The AI answer refers to the brand's page or source material. Often more valuable than a mere mention because it connects to owned content. The answer cites a page from adobe.com as supporting source material.
Share of voice Relative visibility versus competitors. Useful for category-level narrative and executive reporting. Brand A appears in 40% of tracked competitive prompts while Brand B appears in 24%.
Sentiment Whether the brand is framed positively, neutrally, or negatively. Prevents teams from confusing "more visibility" with "better visibility." A brand could be frequently mentioned, but in negative or cautionary contexts.
Domain intelligence Site-level intelligence about a whole web property. Important because domain, page, topic, and prompt are different levels. adobe.com is domain-level; adobe.com/firefly is page-level.
Agentic traffic AI agents and bots visiting owned pages. Useful as a leading signal that AI systems are reading content. An increase in crawler activity to a newly optimized comparison page.
Referral traffic Human traffic coming from AI-generated answers or citations. Helps connect visibility work to business impact. Users click through from an AI answer to the product page.
Optimize at Edge Serve AI-friendly changes at the CDN or edge layer. Fast and reversible. Serving a concise AI-facing summary without changing the visible page for humans.
Optimize at Source Change the origin content itself. Best when editorial, structural, and human-facing improvements are all desired. Rewriting a product page so the source content is stronger for both people and AI systems.
One of the most useful clarifications to teach:
Domain-level means the whole site, such as adobe.com.
Page-level means a specific URL.
Topic-level means a subject area.
Prompt-level means an individual question.
4 • AIO modules

What each Semrush AIO module is for — with practical examples

The best way to teach the modules is to anchor each one to the question it answers, the output it produces, and the mistake people commonly make when using it.

Visibility Overview

Primary job: establish the starting point. This is the module you use when you want a top-down view of current visibility across platforms, topics, and competitive context.

  • Best question: "How visible are we right now?"
  • Best output: baseline visibility, mentions, citations, sentiment, and platform spread.
  • Common mistake: jumping from overview directly into action without validating the prompt or topic selection behind it.

Example: a customer sees strong branded visibility but weak non-branded visibility in comparison prompts. That tells you the brand is recognized, but not yet strongly recommended in competitive evaluation moments.

Prompt Research

Primary job: narrow the universe. This is the module that helps teams decide which prompts actually matter enough to monitor and optimize against.

  • Best question: "Which prompts deserve attention?"
  • Best output: a tighter, better prompt set with less noise and stronger commercial relevance.
  • Common mistake: using too many generic informational prompts and assuming that equals good coverage.

Example: instead of tracking only broad prompts like "what is generative AI," a stronger set might include "best generative AI tool for brand-safe commercial images," "Adobe Firefly vs Midjourney," and "AI image generator for enterprise marketing teams."

Competitor Research

Primary job: identify where competitors are winning and where the brand is absent, weak, shared, strong, or uniquely advantaged.

  • Best question: "Where are competitors getting surfaced and we are not?"
  • Best output: a competitive gap map that informs priority topics and prompts.
  • Common mistake: treating competitor research as a scoreboard instead of as a source of next actions.

Example: if a competitor is repeatedly cited for "best AI image generator for ecommerce product photography" and your brand is missing, that is not just a ranking insight. It is a clue that your content, framing, or source authority may be insufficient for that use case.

Prompt Tracking

Primary job: monitor the selected prompt program over time. After prompts are curated, this module tells you whether performance is improving, flattening, or slipping.

  • Best question: "Are we improving on the prompts we chose?"
  • Best output: trend view for monitored prompts and topic clusters.
  • Common mistake: using Prompt Tracking as if it were the first discovery step rather than the monitoring step.

Example: after a team imports a prompt set focused on enterprise creative evaluation, Prompt Tracking can show whether visibility is moving from missing to weak, or from weak to shared, on those specific prompts.

Traffic Analysis

Primary job: add context about demand, momentum, and trend. This helps teams understand where opportunity may be expanding or where prioritization deserves adjustment.

  • Best question: "Where is demand or trend context telling us to look next?"
  • Best output: better prioritization, especially when market interest is shifting.
  • Common mistake: assuming that trend context alone proves business value.

Example: a topic cluster related to "brand-safe AI content" might show growing relevance before the customer's own first-party analytics fully reflect it. That makes it useful for prioritization.

Topics and prompts workbench

Primary job: turn research into operational selection. This is where teams inspect prompts, topics, response patterns, and source behavior closely enough to decide what should enter the monitored program.

  • Best question: "What exactly should we operationalize?"
  • Best output: a cleaner handoff into Adobe optimization workflows.
  • Common mistake: exporting noisy prompt sets without enough scrutiny.

Example: two prompts may look similar at first glance, but one might be high-intent commercial evaluation while the other is generic education. The workbench step helps you separate them.

Best memory hook for the module stack:
Visibility Overview shows the landscape.
Prompt Research decides what matters.
Competitor Research reveals where the battle is.
Prompt Tracking shows whether the chosen program is moving.
5 • Workflow

How the pieces fit together in the actual operating model

This is the simplest workflow to teach because it keeps Semrush and Adobe in the roles where each is strongest.

1

Baseline the market

Start with AIO to understand current visibility, category shape, and competitive position.

2

Reduce the prompt universe

Use prompt research to find the questions and intent clusters worth monitoring.

3

Prioritize the gaps

Use competitor research to decide which missing or weak areas deserve action first.

4

Activate in Adobe

Surface opportunities, improve pages or structures, and deploy changes at edge or source.

5

Measure in both lenses

Read operational lift in LLMO and broader market movement in AIO.

Example end-to-end: AIO identifies weak visibility for a set of comparison prompts in a high-value product category. The team curates those prompts into a program, uses Adobe to improve relevant comparison and solution pages, deploys targeted content changes, and then tracks two things: operational movement in the monitored prompt program and broader market movement in competitive visibility. Those two readings together form a much more useful story than either one alone.

6 • Measurement model

How to explain AIO vs LLMO without creating measurement confusion

This is one of the most important sections for internal enablement. The safest and clearest explanation is that both are retained conceptually, but they should be treated as distinct lenses, not as one merged scoring system.

Lens What it measures best Best audience Best use
Semrush AIO Market-wide visibility, topic context, competitor movement, share of voice, sentiment. Executives, category owners, strategy leads. Use it for the strategic narrative: where the market is moving and where the brand stands within it.
LLM Optimizer / ABV operational layer Curated prompt program performance, opportunity execution, owned-surface outcomes, traffic and business impact. SEO, content, operations, growth, implementation teams. Use it for the execution narrative: what changed, what improved, and what business signal followed.

Use AIO when the question is strategic

  • Are we gaining or losing share in the category?
  • Which competitors are winning the prompts that matter?
  • Which topics deserve more attention?
  • Is brand sentiment in AI answers strengthening or weakening?

Use LLMO when the question is operational

  • Which opportunity should we address first?
  • Did the selected prompt program improve?
  • Did agentic or referral traffic move?
  • Did this work connect to engagement, conversion, or revenue?
Best one-line explanation: AIO is the strategic market lens. LLMO is the operational proof lens.

Example: if a team optimizes five high-priority product pages tied to a curated prompt set, LLMO may show an earlier improvement in monitored prompt performance and referral traffic. AIO may later show a broader shift in citation share or competitive visibility across the topic area. Those are not conflicting readings. They are different distances from the action.

7 • Edge vs source

How to think about edge and source optimization — and what to track

Teams sometimes become overly focused on which layer is "the real one." That is usually the wrong question. The better question is which layer gets the intended outcome fastest, safest, and most sustainably.

Optimize at Edge

Use the edge layer when the goal is to change what AI systems can consume quickly and reversibly. This can be useful when teams want to test improved summaries, structured guidance, or AI-facing content signals without waiting for full origin content cycles.

Example: an enterprise team may want to expose a more concise product summary to AI agents immediately, even while the full editorial refresh is still being planned at source.

Optimize at Source

Use the source layer when the underlying content should improve for both humans and AI systems, especially when the change should be durable and editorially canonical.

Example: rewriting a long, vague product page into a clearer use-case-oriented page with better comparisons, FAQs, and proof points is a source-layer improvement.

Question Recommended interpretation
If edge and source optimizations both happen, should we track both? Yes — but track them in the two measurement systems for their different purposes: LLMO for operational lift and AIO for market movement.
Should edge and source each get a separate scorecard by default? No — not unless they were intentionally staged apart. Otherwise the signals usually represent combined program lift.
Which layer should get the credit if both changed at once? Usually neither in isolation. Report the outcome as a combined improvement unless sequencing or controlled testing allows layer-level attribution.
Practical rule: when teams ask "which layer should we track improvement in?", the better answer is: "Track operational impact in LLMO, track broader market impact in AIO, and only split edge vs source contribution if the rollout was deliberately staged."
8 • Attribution

Why LLMO opportunity impact maps more directly to LLMO than to AIO

This is a subtle point, but it matters a lot for expectation-setting. It is correct to say that LLMO-surfaced opportunities tend to be more directly attributable inside LLMO than inside AIO.

Direct linkage in LLMO

LLMO knows the curated prompt set, the owned pages, and the opportunity context. That proximity makes it easier to say: "We changed this page or structure, and the monitored program improved."

Example: if an FAQ or summary improvement is deployed to a page that sits inside the monitored program, and LLMO later shows better prompt-level performance and more referral traffic, the link is fairly direct.

Indirect linkage in AIO

AIO measures the brand across a broader market and competitive prompt universe. That means an improvement may show up as better share of voice, citations, or sentiment, but usually as broader market movement, not clean single-change proof.

Example: the same page improvement that clearly helps the monitored LLMO program may later contribute to better visibility in a related topic cluster across AIO. That matters, but the causality is less direct because competitors and market context are also moving.

LLMO opportunities usually have direct operational attribution.
Their effect on AIO is usually second-order: broader market lift, not neat single-opportunity causality.

Use LLMO to prove

  • This opportunity was deployed.
  • This monitored prompt set improved.
  • This page or cluster drove traffic or business lift.

Use AIO to prove

  • The category position improved.
  • Competitive gaps narrowed.
  • Topic-level market visibility strengthened over time.

When AIO attribution gets stronger

  • The change was staged clearly.
  • The targeted topic cluster is narrow and high priority.
  • The operational prompt set came directly from AIO research.
9 • Customer talk track

Customer-facing explanation of the Semrush side

The goal is to sound clear and useful without over-claiming. The strongest customer message is about market context and prioritization.

Semrush gives Adobe Brand Visibility the market-intelligence layer: it shows where your brand is winning or losing across AI search, which prompts and topics matter most, how competitors are performing, and where to focus optimization effort first.

Executive opener

AI is increasingly where customers begin discovery. The Semrush side helps brands understand how they appear in that market, which competitors are winning, and where the biggest visibility gaps are.

SEO / digital opener

If the team already knows AI visibility matters, the next question is where exactly they are losing, against whom, and on which prompts or topics. That is the gap Semrush helps close.

Cross-functional opener

Most teams have internal analytics, but not a reliable category-level view of AI visibility. Semrush fills that market-context gap.

Good discovery questions

  • How are you measuring your brand's presence in AI answers today?
  • Do you have a competitive view, or mostly an internal view?
  • How confident are you that your prompt set reflects real demand and real commercial intent?
  • Can you tell where competitors are being cited and you are not?
  • Which use cases matter most for AI-driven discovery this year?

Best framing to keep

  • Semrush = market context and prioritization.
  • Adobe = activation and measurement on owned surfaces.
  • Together = insight to action.
  • Do not oversell a fully unified metric model.
10 • Objection handling

Direct responses to common Semrush-side objections

The strongest responses do not argue. They clarify the job the Semrush side is there to do.

"We already have SEO tools."

That may actually strengthen the fit. The Semrush side here is not only about rankings. It adds AI prompt intelligence, competitive AI visibility, topic discovery, and market context that many traditional SEO stacks do not expose cleanly.

"Why not just use Adobe's side?"

Adobe is strongest at execution and measurement on owned surfaces. Semrush helps teams understand where the market opportunity is, where competitors are winning, and which prompts or topics deserve action first.

"Can't we just build our own prompt list?"

You can build one. The harder part is knowing whether it is complete, commercially meaningful, and aligned to how the category is actually behaving. Semrush helps reduce that guesswork.

"We care about outcomes, not more dashboards."

Exactly. The Semrush side is valuable because it improves prioritization. Adobe then helps connect those choices to execution and measurable outcomes.

"Is this one unified product now?"

The most accurate explanation is that it is a combined solution with complementary strengths: Semrush for market intelligence, Adobe for optimization and business proof.

"Do you have actual private prompts from ChatGPT?"

No platform has direct access to private LLM conversation logs. The value comes from high-scale market and intent signals that make prompt selection and competitive analysis much smarter.

Best objection-handling habit: first determine whether the customer is challenging value, signal quality, overlap, or feasibility. Then answer at that level instead of jumping straight into features.

Ten durable takeaways

  • This category is about AI answers, not only search rankings.
  • Semrush AIO is the market-intelligence layer.
  • Adobe is the activation and proof layer.
  • Prompt quality determines measurement quality.
  • Prompt Research and Competitor Research build the strategy.

  • AIO is best for strategic narrative.
  • LLMO is best for operational evidence.
  • Edge and source changes usually represent combined lift unless staged separately.
  • LLMO opportunity impact on AIO is often indirect but meaningful.

Best one-paragraph summary

Adobe Brand Visibility is easiest to understand as a two-part operating model. Semrush AIO helps brands understand how they are performing across AI-driven discovery at the market level: prompts, topics, competitors, sentiment, and visibility gaps. Adobe then helps teams act on that insight on owned surfaces and connect improvements to operational and business outcomes. Keep the strategic market lens and the operational proof lens separate, and the whole story becomes much clearer.
