Executive takeaway

KBank's current performance is strongest where the prompt explicitly leans toward promotions, card-tier decisions, or product-led intent. Prompt analysis shows the clearest growth gap is not volume, but coverage quality across generic comparison and investment education prompts. In short: KBank is already strong where the user is close to choosing a product, but weaker where the user is still learning, comparing, or screening options.

What prompt analysis changes

  • Prompt inventory size matters: the largest demand pools are not evenly distributed across topics.
  • Not all wins are equal: high visibility in a small prompt cluster matters less than moderate improvement in a large cluster.
  • KBank already wins branded/product intent: the next ROI sits in non-branded education and comparison prompts.
  • Owned citation gaps are prompt-specific: some themes show visibility without enough KBank-owned URL capture.

What prompt analysis says clearly

  • Branded prompts are fully won: 100% brand coverage, 100% owned URL coverage, and 93.7% average visibility.
  • Non-branded prompts are still solid but materially weaker: 38.1% average visibility, with only 44.9% owned URL coverage.
  • Deals / promotions are the strongest scalable prompt class, with 53.9% average visibility and 88 owned citations.
  • Comparison / vs prompts are the weakest clear gap, with only 8.3% average visibility and 0 owned citations.
  • Beginner / how-to prompts are under-owned: they still surface KBank often, but owned pages are cited in only 22.2% of those prompts.

What KBank is genuinely winning

  • Promotions and card-led decision prompts are the clearest strength zone.
  • Action and conversion prompts are consistently strong, showing KBank performs well near the bottom of the funnel.
  • Branded banking demand is effectively owned, which means the account has already solved the easiest visibility layer.
  • The strongest non-branded wins happen when KBank can answer a concrete product or eligibility need directly.

What should change next

  • Do not overinvest in already-won branded prompts.
  • Do invest in the largest underperforming prompt pools: investment education, comparison, and beginner guidance.
  • Do treat prompt mix as a prioritization lens, not just visibility as a single blended average.
  • Do use owned citation coverage as the operational KPI for whether KBank content is becoming the answer source, not just the mentioned brand.

Priority opportunities

Priority 1

Fix comparison-prompt weakness

Comparison prompts are the clearest structural gap: very low visibility and zero owned citations. KBank should publish more machine-citable comparison content for cashback, no-fee cards, points logic, and side-by-side product selection.

Priority 2

Expand investment education for the largest non-card prompt pool

Fund and investment prompts account for a major share of total prompt demand, but visibility is middling and many educational prompts still miss completely. This is the largest non-card growth surface.

Priority 3

Strengthen beginner and eligibility guidance

Beginner / how-to prompts often surface KBank, but owned citation capture is weak. KBank should add more explainer content for approval, requirements, minimum payment, and "what does this mean?" journeys.

Priority 4

Scale what already works in promotions into evergreen hubs

Promotion prompts are strong today, but many wins depend on timely deal pages. KBank should connect promo assets more tightly to evergreen product and educational hubs so LLM citation authority persists longer.

30-day action plan

  1. Create comparison hubs for cashback, no-fee, points, Starbucks, and travel-booking prompts.
  2. Expand fund education pages for ETF, Thai ESG, REIT, and risk-based product selection.
  3. Add stronger FAQ and explainer modules for eligibility, approval, and minimum payment topics.
  4. Use strong promo pages to funnel internal links into evergreen product and category hubs.
  5. Track prompt performance not just by average visibility, but by prompt-bucket performance and prompt inventory size.

Measurement to track next

  1. Non-branded prompt visibility: improve from 38.1%.
  2. Owned URL coverage: improve beyond 50.0%.
  3. Comparison prompt visibility: improve from 8.3%.
  4. Beginner / how-to owned citation coverage: improve from 22.2%.
  5. Weekly average visibility: restore from 41.1% toward the 44.9% level seen in wk19.
