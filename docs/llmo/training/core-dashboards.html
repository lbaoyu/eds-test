Brand Presence — how to read it

Strong vs weak representation

VisibilityValidationAccessExplanationImpact

Strong

  • Mentions on priority prompts
  • Citations from owned or favorable sources
  • Healthy sentiment, stable competitive position
  • Visible across branded and discovery prompts

Watch for

  • Mentions without citations
  • Competitor dominance in the answer
  • Negative or mixed sentiment
  • Visible only on branded prompts

Being present is not enough. The real question is whether we are represented in a way that is credible, useful, and competitive.

Input vs output

If Brand Presence is AI output, Agentic Traffic is AI input. Citation is the outcome; retrieval is the prerequisite.

Key idea

A page can work for humans and still fail for AI — bots may only see part of what a person sees after rendering.

09 / 19

Agentic Traffic — how to read it

Technically ready, or not?

VisibilityValidationAccessExplanationImpact

Strong

  • High interactions on prompt-relevant pages
  • High success rate — retrieval is reliable
  • Healthy TTFB during retrieval
  • High content visibility on pages that matter

Watch for

  • Failed requests and slow responses
  • Activity concentrated on the wrong URLs
  • Low content visibility where it matters
  • Humans can see it, but bots can’t

Strong means the site is technically ready to support visibility. Weak means the content exists, but AI isn’t reaching or seeing enough of it.

12 / 19

URL Inspector — how to read it

Strong vs weak pages

VisibilityValidationAccessExplanationImpact

Strong

  • Right page mapped to the right prompt
  • Clear, substantial content
  • Easy AI access and citation traction
  • Page intent matches the prompt

Watch for

  • The wrong page winning a prompt
  • Thin or fragmented content
  • Poor AI readability or technical barriers
  • A strong topic with no clear owned page

Optimization happens on specific URLs, not on averages — find the few pages that move the result.

Referral Traffic — how to read it

From AI attention to user movement

VisibilityValidationAccessExplanationImpact

Strong

  • Growing AI-referred visits
  • Traffic from priority sources
  • Strong landing-page engagement
  • Cited pages double as useful landing pages

Watch for

  • Visibility, but low referral volume
  • Traffic landing on weak pages
  • Strong citations, poor click-through
  • Arrivals that don’t carry intent forward

AI helps discovery; the landing experience decides whether interest becomes engagement.
