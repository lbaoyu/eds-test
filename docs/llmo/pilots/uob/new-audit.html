Section 6

Individual prompt review — flagged items

Most prompts are realistic and usable. The prompts below are the highest-value cleanup opportunities because they are either too broad, too leading, semantically overlapping, or slightly mis-positioned.

Prompt Issue Why it matters Suggested direction
What is the best business bank account in Singapore? Too broad Likely to generate generic, unstable answers with low diagnostic value. Narrow by audience or need, e.g. best business bank account for startups in Singapore or best business bank account for firms with cross-border payments.
What are the best digital business banking options in Singapore? Too broad This is a large-market prompt rather than a focused opportunity prompt. Add user segment or need state, e.g. payments-heavy SMEs, digital onboarding, or startups.
Why should I choose UOB for my business bank account? Leading Biases the prompt toward a favorable brand answer and weakens measurement neutrality. Rewrite as a neutral comparison or selection question, e.g. What should SMEs compare when choosing a business bank account in Singapore?
What is UOB Infinity? Overlap Overlaps with adjacent Infinity prompts and adds limited new measurement value by itself. Keep only if paired with a more differentiated purpose, or merge conceptually with a capability prompt.
What can I do with UOB Infinity? Overlap Very close to What is UOB Infinity? and How do I use UOB Infinity for business banking? Refocus on a concrete workflow, e.g. approvals, payroll, bulk payments, collections, or access controls.
Should I choose a local SGD business account or a multi-currency business account? Topic placement Good prompt, but it fits more naturally under cross-border / multi-currency decisioning than general comparison. Move to Multi-currency and Cross-border or create a specialized account-fit subtopic.
What is a good business account for SMEs in Singapore? Broad Useful as a market discovery prompt, but still wide enough to produce noisy answers. Constrain by company stage, payment profile, account needs, or international exposure.

Section 8

Final assessment

The prompt bank is strong enough to use for a pilot, especially because it stays focused on one market and one business line. The current set already has good commercial intent coverage and balanced topic sizing. The main quality risks are not structural failure; they are calibration issues: too much branded emphasis, a few broad prompts, and mild topic overlap.

  • Then hold the set reasonably stable to establish clean trendlines.
