UOB Card Services Prompt & Topic LLMO Review

This library is grounded in real customer language, but it is less effective for LLMO than it could be because the main quality issues are structural rather than linguistic. The strongest drag comes from duplicate waiver intents, topic fragmentation, scope leakage inside the Card Services category, and multi-intent prompts that force one answer target to cover several different user paths.

Snapshot

Key Takeaways

The library does not need a broad rewrite. The best gains will come from consolidating true overlaps, making topic boundaries cleaner, and splitting prompts that currently ask two things at once.

Highest-impact fixes

What to Change

These changes are the most likely to improve prompt discoverability, topic coherence, and answer precision without changing the underlying customer needs already represented in the file.

High Priority Duplicate intent cannibalization

Merge overlapping waiver prompts into canonical versions

The waiver section contains the clearest near-duplicates in the library. These should be treated as one canonical prompt per waiver type, not multiple prompts competing for the same answer territory.

How do I request an annual fee waiver for UOB?
How do I waive my UOB credit card annual fee?
Can UOB waive the interest on my credit card?
How do I waive interest charges on my UOB credit card?
How do I waive my UOB late payment fee?
How do I waive UOB late charges?

Recommended canonical prompts

How do I request a waiver for my UOB credit card annual fee?
How do I request a waiver for interest charges on my UOB credit card?
How do I request a waiver for a UOB credit card late payment fee?

Why this matters for LLMO

  • It removes multiple prompts that are chasing the same relevance signal.
  • It gives retrieval and ranking systems one stronger representative target per intent.
  • It reduces answer overlap and maintenance duplication.
  • It makes topic-level authority easier to build.
High Priority Category / topic leakage

Remove or relocate prompts that weaken the semantic center of Card Services

A prompt set can only build strong topical authority if the surrounding prompts reinforce roughly the same domain. This library mixes card-service intents with account, transfer, and bonus-interest intents that belong in a broader retail banking cluster or a separate transfer / account-benefits cluster.

Does my salary credit into UOB ONE Account qualify for bonus interest?
Why hasn't my UOB ONE Account bonus interest been credited this month?
Which UOB account is linked to my PayNow and which account receives transfers?
I transferred money using UOB but the recipient has not received it yet
Why is my UOB transfer blocked or on hold?
Recommended action: keep Card Services tightly centered on card lifecycle, card payments, card charges, card waivers, card disputes, and card transaction states. Move account bonus-interest and transfer-routing prompts into their own cluster so the card library stops carrying unrelated semantic weight.
Medium Priority Overlapping failure taxonomy

Separate "declined", "failed", "pending", and "disputed" more cleanly

The current taxonomy has overlap between transaction outcomes. In particular, Transactions — Decline and Transactions — Failed are not cleanly separated, and one prompt covers both in a single sentence. That makes topic assignment and answer routing less precise than it should be.

My UOB card was declined — could it be due to my credit limit or daily limit?
Why was my UOB bill payment or fund transfer declined?
Why was my UOB credit card declined or why did my transaction fail?

Problem

  • Declined usually implies a decision or limit condition.
  • Failed can imply technical, merchant, network, or processing failure.
  • Bill payment or fund transfer mixes two transaction types in one prompt.

Recommended fix

  • Create one topic for Card transaction declined.
  • Create one topic for Transfer / payment failed if that domain stays in scope.
  • Avoid prompts that mention two failure states in the same line.
Medium Priority Multi-intent prompt construction

Split prompts that ask multiple questions or enumerate multiple channels

Some prompts are realistic customer utterances, but they are still suboptimal as canonical prompt targets because they ask for multiple answers at once. LLMO works better when each prompt points to one primary intent and one answer path.

How do I pay my UOB credit card bill — AXS, bank transfer, or TMRW app?
What are the charges on my UOB statement — annual fee, late fee, or interest?
How long does a UOB fee waiver request take and should I pay while it is pending?
Which UOB account is linked to my PayNow and which account receives transfers?

Better structure

  • How can I pay my UOB credit card bill?
  • What does each charge on my UOB statement mean?
  • How long does a UOB fee waiver request take?
  • Should I pay my UOB card bill while a fee waiver request is pending?

Why it helps

  • Narrower prompts produce more consistent retrieval targets.
  • They reduce the chance that one answer becomes too broad or partially relevant.
  • They improve topic labeling because each prompt maps to one dominant need.
Lower Priority Topic fragmentation

Fourteen topic labels contain only one prompt. That is a sign that the taxonomy is more granular than the prompt volume can support. In LLMO terms, the topic map is too thinly distributed to build strong reinforcement around each label.

Consolidation guidance

Suggested Topic Structure

The goal is not to flatten everything. It is to create a topic model where each label has enough density to matter, while still preserving separations that lead to different customer answers.

Current area Suggested action Reason for LLMO
Waivers — Annual Fee / Interest / Late Fee Keep these as separate topics, but merge duplicate prompts within each one. Different fee types still need different policy answers, even though wording overlap should be reduced.
Transactions — Decline and Transactions — Failed Redefine with cleaner boundaries or merge only if the answer truly does not differ. Current overlap weakens routing precision and creates label ambiguity.
Payments — Channels / Charges / Waiver Process Retain as separate topics only if prompts are rewritten into single-intent forms. Right now the prompts are broad enough to spill across several answer paths.
Rewards — ONE Account and transfer-related prompts Move out of Card Services. They dilute the semantic center of a card-focused library.
Cancellation and Cancellation — Instalments Keep separate. Cancelling a card and understanding instalment consequences are adjacent but not the same customer task.
Self Service — Login / Digital Token / Singpass Keep related but separate, or nest them under a broader App Access topic. They touch the same channel, but each maps to a distinct troubleshooting path.
Do not over-merge

What to Keep Separate

Not every overlap is a duplication problem. Some prompts are adjacent but should remain distinct because they trigger different guidance, workflows, or troubleshooting steps.

Prompt area Why it should stay separate
Annual fee vs interest waiver vs late-fee waiver They are all waiver intents, but the fee types are materially different and should not collapse into one topic.
Pending transaction vs unauthorised transaction dispute A pending charge normally requires status clarification, while an unauthorised charge requires dispute guidance and risk handling.
Card cancellation vs instalment-plan consequences One is a card-lifecycle action; the other is about downstream billing implications after cancellation.
Login issues vs digital-token setup vs Singpass verification All are app-related, but each points to a different troubleshooting flow and different source content.
Overall verdict

Final Assessment

The prompt library is directionally solid, but it is currently less efficient than it could be for LLMO because too much signal is being spent on overlap, off-cluster prompts, and thin topic labels. The best outcome is selective cleanup, not wholesale rewriting.

Keep most prompt language natural The library already uses realistic customer phrasing. The main issue is not tone or realism.
Consolidate true duplicates aggressively Waiver prompts are the clearest place where canonicalization will improve performance immediately.
Tighten the topic map Fewer, cleaner, better-supported topics will make the prompt set more coherent and easier for retrieval systems to interpret.
