Foundations: GEO, LLMO, prompts, citations, and AI visibility

Build the mental model before the tooling model.
What actually changes from SEO to GEO?

Traditional SEO asks: Do we rank in search results?
GEO asks: Do AI systems mention, recommend, or cite us when users ask relevant questions?

That change matters because AI answers can compress discovery, comparison, and evaluation into a single response. Instead of winning a click from a list of links, the brand now needs to win a place in the answer itself.

Old center of gravity New center of gravity What it implies
Rankings Answer presence Need to track mentions, citations, and position inside AI answers
Keywords Prompts Need to understand fuller buying questions, not just phrases
Clicks from SERPs Citations and referrals from AI surfaces Need to care about retrievability and AI trust
Key terms: GEO, LLMO, AI Visibility, ICP

GEO Generative Engine Optimization — optimizing for visibility in AI-generated answers.

LLMO Large Language Model Optimization — similar family of work, but more explicitly tied to LLM behavior.

AI Visibility A practical measurement layer: are you present, how often, on which prompts, in what position, and with which citations?

ICP Ideal Customer Profile — the type of customer you most want and are best suited to win.

ICP example

If your product is enterprise CRM, then prompts like best free crm for freelancers may be real, but they are probably low ICP fit.

Prompts vs keywords: why prompts are usually the better unit

Keywords are shorter abstractions. Prompts are richer expressions of real user intent.

Keyword-style input Prompt-style input
best crm software "What CRM is best for a growing SaaS sales team?"
mailchimp alternative "What are the best Mailchimp alternatives for smaller ecommerce brands?"
project management tools "Which project management tool is easiest for client-facing agencies?"
Memory hook: prompts are usually closer to the moment of decision.
Mentions vs recommendations vs citations
Level What it means Commercial value
Mention Your brand appears in the answer Useful, but often shallow
Recommendation Your brand is suggested as an option Stronger
Citation Your page is used as evidence or support Usually strongest
Instructor note: if someone remembers only one thing from Module 1, make it this: mentions are nice, citations are better.

Prompt Research: from seed topic to usable prompt set

Where many GEO programs either become strategic — or become clutter.
What "expand from a seed" really means

Seed expansion is not just finding longer versions of a keyword. It is expanding across intent, scenario, and decision context.

Seed Useful expansion path
crm software best-for, alternatives, comparisons, roles, team size, implementation ease
endpoint security enterprise, remote workforce, deployment ease, vendor alternatives
retinol serum beginners, sensitive skin, anti-aging, alternatives, ingredient questions
Simple definition: seed expansion = topic expansion + intent expansion + buying-context expansion
The four prompt buckets to build first

Category prompts

Define the broad market conversation.

Example: "What is the best CRM for small businesses?"

Comparison / alternative prompts

Usually among the highest-intent and highest-value prompt types.

Example: "What are the best HubSpot alternatives for mid-market teams?"

Use-case prompts

Map the product to a job-to-be-done.

Example: "Which payroll tool is best for remote startups?"

Evaluation prompts

Surface buying criteria and differentiators.

Example: "Which CRM is easiest to implement?"

Research set vs tracking set
Set Purpose Shape
Research set Explore the market prompt universe Broad, messy, intentionally expansive
Tracking set Run the operating program Narrow, curated, commercially important
Rule: research wide, track narrow.
How to make prompt sets reflect real customers better

Semrush prompt discovery is best treated as market-grounded directional intelligence, not as a literal transcript of private LLM chats.

Validate prompt candidates against:

  • sales calls
  • support language
  • site search
  • first-party analytics
  • known ICP priorities
Good teaching line: Semrush gives you candidate demand. First-party data gives you customer truth.

Prompt Tracking: interpreting movement correctly

The measurement layer that is easiest to misuse.

What it is for

Prompt Tracking is not "did we appear?"
It is "are we becoming more visible, more prominent, or more cite-worthy on the prompts that matter?"
Metric What it roughly tells you
AI Visibility Overall strength across the prompt set
Mentions Whether you show up at all
Average Position How prominently you appear
Trend lines Whether direction is improving or deteriorating over time

False positives

  • One prompt improved, so the whole program works
  • Mentions went up, so success
  • One strong week means strategy shift
  • Branded prompts are up, so category GEO is healthy

Better reading habits

  • Track a portfolio, not vanity prompts
  • Review by cluster
  • Interpret monthly, not daily
  • Connect changes to known actions

Cluster reading example

Cluster Observed trend Likely meaning
Comparison Strong improvement New comparison content may be landing well
Use case Moderate improvement Scenario-specific content is helping
Category Flat Brand still weak in the broad market conversation

Cited Pages and competitor analysis

Where Semrush becomes genuinely useful for content planning.
What Cited Pages tells you

Cited Pages shows which of your URLs AI systems actually use as support. That makes it one of the strongest practical indicators of AI trust.

Better question: not "which page should matter?" but "which page already matters to AI?"

Common citation winners:

comparison pages FAQ pages glossaries use-case pages buyer guides stats pages

How to interpret competitor wins

Don't stop at "competitor X appears more." Ask:

  • Which prompts do they win?
  • Which page types seem to support those wins?
  • Are they winning by breadth or depth?
  • Are they strongest in category, alternatives, use cases, or evaluation?
Does Semrush produce roadmap work items automatically?
Mostly no. Semrush surfaces the evidence, but customers still translate that evidence into work items.

Semrush can show:

  • prompts you miss
  • competitors that win them
  • pages that are cited
  • patterns in winning content

But the team still decides:

  • create vs rewrite vs reformat
  • which content cluster gets priority
  • who owns execution
How to convert Module 5 into a roadmap
Observed pattern Likely response
Competitor wins alternatives prompts Create or strengthen alternatives pages
Your glossary pages get cited, but evaluation pages do not Add buyer guides, pricing explainers, implementation FAQs
Important page exists but is never cited Improve structure, proof, specificity, or format
No asset exists for a high-intent cluster Create the missing asset

Site Audit for AI Search: technical readiness

The technical layer of GEO, without the hype.

What matters most

  • Access — can AI bots reach the page?
  • Discovery — can they find the page easily?
  • Structure — is the content organized clearly?
  • Clarity — is the meaning explicit enough to use?

What people over-focus on

  • llms.txt as a silver bullet
  • novelty checks over core crawlability
  • homepage readiness over high-value supporting pages
If AI crawlers cannot access important pages, content work gets undercut before it starts.
Priority Technical issue type
High Important page blocked or inaccessible to AI agents
High High-value page buried, orphaned, or poorly linked
Medium Weak structure, ambiguous content layout, missing helpful schema
Low Hygiene issues on low-value or non-supporting pages
Instructor note: the right teaching tone here is calm. Technical GEO is usually not about chasing every shiny AI-specific artifact. It is mostly about making valuable content reachable, discoverable, and understandable.

Practical weekly GEO workflow

How to operate Semrush without drowning in dashboards.
Operating loop: Discover → Measure → Diagnose → Fix → Review
Cadence What to do Why it matters
Weekly Review prompt movement, citation changes, competitor shifts, technical blockers Stay current without overreacting
Monthly Interpret cluster performance and roadmap impact Separate signal from noise
Quarterly Refresh prompt universe and operating set Keep the program aligned to current demand

Weekly GEO review format

  1. What improved?
  2. What worsened?
  3. Which competitor moved?
  4. Which page or prompt cluster matters most next?
  5. What 1–2 actions will we take this week?
Rule of thumb: one or two meaningful actions per cycle beats ten scattered tweaks.

Common mistakes and how to avoid them

The main failure modes in Semrush GEO work.

The biggest mistakes

  • Confusing prompt research with literal prompt truth
  • Tracking everything you research
  • Watching mentions while ignoring citations
  • Assuming the homepage should be the citation winner
  • Turning every gap into "write a blog post"
  • Overvaluing llms.txt
  • Ignoring technical blockers
  • Spending more time monitoring than optimizing

The fix pattern

  • Validate with first-party reality
  • Track a curated prompt portfolio
  • Prioritize cited pages and prompt clusters
  • Choose the right page type
  • Fix access before polishing copy
  • Turn every review cycle into action
One-line summary: most Semrush GEO mistakes happen when teams confuse visibility intelligence with completed optimization.

Appendix — Semrush vs Adobe LLM Optimizer

The most honest explanation is not "there is no overlap." The overlap is real.

Best clean distinction:
Semrush = intelligence and discovery
Adobe LLM Optimizer = activation and optimization
Question Semrush LLMO
What prompts matter? Strongest for broad prompt universe discovery Better as the curated operating set
What is happening in the market? Core strength Secondary
What is happening on our owned properties? Limited compared with Adobe Core strength
What should we change next? Signals and inferred priorities Stronger prescriptive / activation story
Can we tie this to business outcomes? Weaker Stronger
Best prompt-overlap explanation:
In Semrush, prompts are mainly a discovery and benchmarking instrument.
In LLMO, prompts are mainly an operating control set.
