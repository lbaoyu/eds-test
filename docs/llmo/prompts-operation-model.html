Core Product Principles

1. Prompts are test cases, not keywords

In Adobe LLM Optimizer, prompts should function as repeatable measurement instruments. They should be realistic, specific, and stable enough to produce meaningful trend lines.

2. Discovery and tracking are different jobs

The system should encourage broad discovery, but reserve tracking for fewer, more intentional prompts tied to business goals and reliable measurement.

3. The library is an asset, not a dump

A prompt library should preserve knowledge, provenance, ownership, and structure. It should be broader than the tracking set, but much cleaner than the universe.

4. Stability matters for before/after measurement

Tracking sets should not churn constantly. Frequent prompt changes reduce comparability and weaken confidence in optimization impact.

Most important operating rule: do not let the tracking set become a brainstorming scratchpad.

Recommended End-to-End Workflow

Step 1 — Build the Universe

Start broad. Pull candidates from Semrush signals, GSC, AI visibility trends, site content, product themes, competitor language, and team input.

Step 2 — Curate into the Library

Retain the prompts worth keeping. Deduplicate, tag, classify, assign topic and intent, and preserve source metadata.

Step 3 — Create a Research Set

Pull a focused subset into a working space where analysts can refine, cluster, score, compare, and decide what should move forward.

Step 4 — Promote a Tracking Set

Move only the strongest prompts into active monitoring. This should be the set used for visibility trends, optimization opportunities, and impact reporting.

Step 5 — Review on Cadence

Revisit prompts regularly, but deliberately. Add or retire prompts based on evidence, not on impulse or weekly experimentation.

In One Sentence

Discover widely, store carefully, research selectively, and track only what is strategic enough to measure over time.

What to Encourage vs What to Prevent

Encourage

  • Ground prompts in real demand and real customer language
  • Group prompts by intent and topic
  • Keep the library broader than the tracking set
  • Use research sets for refinement before promotion
  • Maintain stable baselines for measurement windows
  • Version prompt changes and record rationale

Prevent

  • Uploading every brainstormed prompt directly to tracking
  • Mixing unrelated intents into one topic
  • Frequent changes to active tracking immediately before or after optimization
  • Keyword-stuffed or robotic prompts
  • Over-indexing on branded prompts only
  • Treating the live prompt bank as a temporary scratchpad
Main platform risk: paraphrase inflation — the library grows in size without adding distinct signal, because multiple prompts say the same thing in slightly different ways.
