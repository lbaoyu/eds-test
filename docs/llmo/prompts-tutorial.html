What Prompts Really Are in LLMO

In LLMO, a prompt is not just a question. It is a test case used to track how AI assistants answer a real customer need.

What a strong prompt helps you evaluate

  • whether your brand or content appears
  • how relevant the answer is
  • whether the answer is accurate and useful
  • how results change over time
Simple definition:
A prompt in LLMO is a repeatable customer-style question used to track and improve how website content performs in AI answers.
If the prompt is weak, the signal is weak.

Why this matters

Prompts are how you measure visibility, relevance, usefulness, and consistency. If the input is vague or unnatural, the tracking becomes noisy.

Speaker Notes

In LLMO, prompts are not just inputs. They are test cases. We use them to see how AI assistants respond to real customer questions, whether our content shows up, and whether the answer is actually useful. That means the prompt itself has to be strong enough to test something meaningful. If the prompt is vague, broad, or unnatural, the tracking becomes noisy. If the prompt is strong, it gives us a much clearer signal about how our content is performing in AI-generated answers.

Key Qualities of a Strong Prompt

  • Natural — sounds like something a real customer would ask
    How do I sign a PDF online?
  • Clear — easy to understand on the first read
    How can I edit text in a PDF?
  • Specific — points to a real need or use case
    What's the best PDF editor for a small business?
  • Focused — asks one main thing, not several at once
    How does Photoshop compare with Canva for social media design?
  • Repeatable — can be used again later and still mean the same thing
    How do I reduce PDF file size without losing quality?

Core principle

Strong prompts sound real, mean one clear thing, and are stable enough to track over time.

Speaker Notes

Strong prompts usually share the same basic qualities. They sound natural, like real customer questions. They are clear enough that the intent is obvious. They are specific enough to point to an actual need. They stay focused on one main thing. And they are repeatable, so you can use them again later and still be testing the same intent. That is what makes them useful in LLMO. You are not just trying to ask a decent question once. You are trying to create a prompt that can serve as a reliable test case.

Slide 3

A Simple Formula for Building Strong Prompts

User + Need + Context

Use it as a guide, not a strict rule.

User

Who is asking?
student, freelancer, small business owner, marketer

Need

What are they trying to do?
edit, compare, sign, compress, create

Context

What makes the question clearer?
for work, for social media, without losing quality, for a small team

Examples

What's the best PDF editor for a small business owner who needs to edit and share files?

How does Photoshop compare with Canva for a marketer creating social media posts?

What's the best e-signature tool for a freelancer sending client contracts?

User, need, and context is a simple way to make prompts stronger — not a rule every prompt must follow word for word.

Speaker Notes

A practical way to improve prompt quality is to think in three parts: user, need, and context. Who is asking? What are they trying to do? And what detail makes the question clearer? This is not a rigid template that every prompt must follow every time. Some prompts are already strong without all three parts being fully spelled out. But when a prompt feels too thin or too broad, adding user and context is often the easiest way to make it stronger.

Speaker Notes

Weak prompts can fail in different ways, and it helps to show those differences clearly. Some are too short and read like labels. Some are too broad and could mean almost anything. Some combine too many needs into one question. Some are so vague that the real intent is missing. And some sound unnatural, more like internal wording than customer language. Strong prompts fix those problems. They make the question sound more real, more specific, and more focused. That is what makes them better test cases in LLMO.
