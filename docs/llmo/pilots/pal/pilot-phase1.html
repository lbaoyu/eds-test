Prompt Strategy

Prompt Design Standard

Prompts are written as realistic customer test cases rather than short keyword fragments.

User + Need + Context
  • One clear intent per prompt
  • Natural customer wording
  • Stable topic grouping
  • Measurable before-and-after comparison

Topic Architecture

  • 4 topics under Booking & Destinations
  • 4 topics under Baggage & Travel Policies
  • 25 prompts per topic
  • 8 topics total / 200 prompts total

Branded vs. Unbranded Prompt Mix

The prompt mix is not based on a single generic ratio. It is determined by topic intent. Discovery and comparison topics are weighted more heavily toward unbranded prompts to test natural category visibility. Policy and operational topics retain stronger branded coverage because answer quality depends more directly on airline-specific rules.

Measurement Framework

Pilot measurement should use a fixed before-and-after method. The same prompt set, market scope, competitor set, and reporting filters should remain constant across the baseline and post-change periods.

Measurement Setup

  • Baseline window: 2-4 weekly snapshots before content changes
  • Post-change window: 4 weekly snapshots after content deployment
  • Prompt set: same 200 prompts throughout the pilot
  • Scope: PH market and the same competitor set throughout
  • Priority URLs: 8-10 URLs mapped to the two pilot categories

Primary Pilot Metrics

  • Visibility Score
  • Unique Prompts with Owned Citations
  • Total Times Cited
  • Sentiment Distribution
  • Referral Hits from LLMs
  • Citation Rate
  • CRD (Citation Rate Delta)
  • CCS (Citation Consistency Score)
Metric Definition Use in pilot evaluation
Visibility Score Overall strength of brand presence across the tracked prompt set Top-line indicator of visibility improvement
Unique Prompts with Owned Citations Number of prompts that cite a Philippine Airlines URL at least once Measures citation breadth and category coverage
Total Times Cited Total citation volume across the prompt set Measures citation frequency and repeatability
Citation Rate Prompts citing a URL in a week ÷ prompts relevant to that URL Measures URL-level citation performance
CRD Average post-change citation rate − baseline citation rate Measures URL-level improvement after optimization
CCS Weeks cited post-change ÷ total post-change weeks Measures durability and consistency of gains
Referral Hits Visits originating from AI-generated citations Measures downstream traffic value where volume is sufficient
