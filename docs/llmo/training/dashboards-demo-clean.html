LLMO Demo Framework by Dashboard and Section

This structure is designed for live demos in the current LLM Optimizer UI. It follows the latest product semantics: Brand Presence is the visibility scorecard, Data Insights is a drill-down within Brand Presence, and the remaining domain views carry the story from AI-agent access to citation outcomes to human traffic.

Brand Presence Brand Presence → Data Insights Agentic Traffic URL Inspector Referral Traffic
Core framing: Brand Presence tells you whether the brand is visible and how it compares in market. Data Insights validates topic and prompt performance inside Brand Presence. Agentic Traffic tells you whether AI agents can reach, succeed on, and read your content. URL Inspector shows which exact URLs are winning or missing citations. Referral Traffic shows whether those citations send real humans and, when analytics is connected, business outcomes. For content visibility gaps, optimization status, and fixes, pair Agentic Traffic and URL Inspector findings with Opportunities.

1. Brand Presence

Use this as the brand visibility scorecard for presence, perception, and market comparison in AI answers.

Primary role: visibility scorecard

Sharpened message

Brand Presence is the scorecard for how your brand shows up in AI responses. It tells you how visible the brand is, how that is trending, and how that compares with the market.

Important boundary

It is not the deepest answer-quality inspection layer. For topic-level and prompt-level analysis, continue into Data Insights inside Brand Presence.

1

Filters + overview metrics

Where to navigate

Open Brand Presence and stay at the top of the page.

What to highlight
  • Visibility Score
  • Brand Mentions
  • Citations
  • Trend movement versus prior period
Why demo it

Fastest answer to: "Are we showing up in AI answers, and is that improving or declining?"

2

Market Tracking

Where to navigate

Move to the Market Tracking section on the same dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Weekly mentions comparison
  • Weekly citations comparison
  • Whether the brand is gaining or losing ground
Why demo it

Shows whether visibility is strong relative to others, not just in isolation.

3

Sentiment Trend Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to Sentiment Trend Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Positive / neutral / negative trend
  • Notable perception changes over time
Why demo it

Visibility matters, but quality of representation matters too.

4

Share of Voice

Where to navigate

Go to the Share of Voice area on the same dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Where the brand leads
  • Where competitors dominate
  • Topic-level competitive standing
Why demo it

Best topic-level summary of whether the brand truly owns the conversation.

Suggested talk track

"I start with Brand Presence because it is the scorecard. It tells us whether the brand is visible in AI answers, how that is trending, and how we compare in market before we drill into the underlying topics and prompts."

2. Brand Presence drill-down: Data Insights

Use this as the topic-level and prompt-level analysis layer inside Brand Presence.

Primary role: topic and prompt analysis

Sharpened message

Data Insights is where Brand Presence becomes actionable. It breaks the scorecard down into topics and prompts so you can see where visibility is strong, weak, early in answers, or ceded to someone else.

What it validates

Are we showing up in the right topics, with the right prominence, and are citations going to our content rather than another source?

1

Data Insights table scope

Where to navigate

Stay inside Brand Presence and move to the Data Insights table.

What to highlight
  • Topic-level versus prompt-level analysis
  • Market or region context
  • Search and export controls
  • Core table columns such as Popularity, Visibility Score, Mentions, Sentiment, Position, All Citations, and Owned Citations
Why demo it

This is where the scorecard becomes an inspection layer instead of a headline only.

2

High-popularity, low-visibility topics

Where to navigate

Sort or filter for high Popularity with weaker Visibility Score.

What to highlight
  • Popularity
  • Visibility Score
  • Mentions
Why demo it

Best quick way to show where demand exists but brand presence is underperforming.

3

All Citations vs Owned Citations

Where to navigate

Look for rows with strong All Citations but weak Owned Citations.

What to highlight
  • Topics where answers are well sourced overall
  • But those citations are going to someone else
Why demo it

Best authority-gap story: the answer space is active, but the grounding is going to the wrong source.

4

Prompt drill-down

Where to navigate

Click from a topic into a specific prompt.

What to highlight
  • Position
  • Sentiment
  • All Citations
  • Owned Citations
Why demo it

This is the clearest proof of how a broad score turns into a specific topic or prompt opportunity.

Suggested talk track

"Brand Presence gives us the scorecard. Data Insights is the drill-down inside Brand Presence that shows which topics and prompts are driving the score, where we are underrepresented, and where citations are going to the wrong source."

3. Agentic Traffic

Use this as the AI-agent access, success, and machine-readability layer for your site.

Primary role: can agents reach, succeed on, and read the site?

Sharpened message

Pages cannot influence AI systems if agents cannot access or interpret them. Agentic Traffic shows whether AI agents are reaching the site, succeeding on requests, and seeing content that is actually usable.

Important boundary

This is not the citation-landscape dashboard. Use URL Inspector for citation performance, and use Opportunities for remediation workflow, optimization status, and fixes.

1

Filters + top-line metrics

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Agentic Traffic and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Site, Date Range, Category, Platform, Agent Type, User Agent, Success Rate, and Content Type filters
  • Agentic interactions
  • Success rate
  • Average TTFB
  • Content Visibility
Why demo it

Establishes both technical health and whether AI agents are actually able to use the content.

2

Traffic distribution + response code breakdown

Where to navigate

Stay on the top chart area of Agentic Traffic.

What to highlight
  • Hits By Market
  • Hits by Category
  • Hits by Page Type
  • Response Code Breakdown
Why demo it

Shows where AI attention is concentrated and whether redirects or errors may be limiting access.

3

Top Movers / Bottom Movers + User Agent Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll past the headline charts into Top Movers, Bottom Movers, and User Agent Analysis.

What to highlight
  • URLs gaining or losing agentic traffic
  • Crawler versus chatbot mix
  • Which page types are getting visited by which agent types
Why demo it

Best way to show how AI-agent behavior is shifting and whether the mix of traffic is meaningful.

4

URL Performance Analysis

Where to navigate

Move into URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight
  • Total Hits
  • Unique Agents
  • Top Agent
  • Top Agent Type
  • Success Rate
  • Category
Why demo it

Best page-level technical view before moving into Opportunities for fixes or into URL Inspector for citation performance.

Suggested talk track

"Agentic Traffic tells us whether AI agents can reach, succeed on, and read the site. Content Visibility is the key bridge from raw bot traffic to actual machine readability, while Response Code Breakdown and page-level analysis show whether technical issues are limiting that access."

4. URL Inspector

Use this as the citation landscape and exact URL citation-performance layer.

Primary role: exact URL citation performance

Sharpened message

URL Inspector is where the story becomes URL-level and citation-specific. It shows which exact owned URLs are being cited, which ones are being evaluated but not selected, and which outside URLs are winning the same answer space.

Important boundary

These tables read the citation landscape, not page internals. For content visibility, optimization status, and fixes, pair URL Inspector with Agentic Traffic and Opportunities.

1

Filters + overview metrics + trend charts

Where to navigate

Open Domain → URL Inspector and stay at the top of the dashboard.

What to highlight
  • Unique prompts with owned citations
  • Total unique prompts
  • Unique cited URLs
  • Total times cited
  • Citation Attempts and Referral Hits from LLMs trends
Why demo it

Best URL-level summary before drilling into owned or third-party citation winners.

2

High Citation Attempts, low Times Cited

Where to navigate

In Your Cited URLs, find pages with high Citation Attempts and weaker Times Cited.

What to highlight
  • Citation Attempts
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
Why demo it

Strongest opportunity story: demand exists, but citation conversion is weak.

3

Your Cited URLs + URL Details

Where to navigate

Go to Your Cited URLs, choose an owned page, and open URL Details.

What to highlight
  • Times Cited
  • Prompts Cited In
  • Agentic Hits
  • Referral Hits
  • Sentiment Distribution, Citation Trends, and Prompt Analysis in details
Why demo it

Best owned-page analysis flow and the clearest bridge between visibility, traffic, and explanation.

4

Cited Third Party URLs + Cited Domains

Where to navigate

Move to Cited Third Party URLs and Cited Domains.

What to highlight
  • Which outside URLs are winning
  • Which domains dominate
  • Competitor versus earned versus owned mix
Why demo it

Best competitive authority story at the page and domain level.

Suggested talk track

"URL Inspector is the citation landscape view. It tells us which exact owned URLs are winning, which ones are being considered but not cited enough, and which outside pages or domains are taking authority in the same answer space."

5. Referral Traffic

Use this as the human clickthrough and business-outcomes layer.

Primary role: real human traffic and value

Sharpened message

Referral Traffic measures what happens after AI citations drive human clicks. It shows which referrers send traffic, which landing pages engage visitors, and whether that traffic produces measurable business value.

Important boundary

These views measure human referral performance, not citation generation or page internals. For citation opportunity use Brand Presence and URL Inspector; for bot access, readability gaps, and fixes use Agentic Traffic and Opportunities.

1

Filters + Traffic Insights metrics

Where to navigate

Open Domain → Referral Traffic and stay on Traffic Insights.

What to highlight
  • Total Referral Traffic from LLMs
  • Consent Rate
  • Bounce Rate
  • Hits By Referrer
Why demo it

Best opener for proving that LLM citations are sending real human traffic.

2

Referral Sources Details

Where to navigate

Stay on Traffic Insights and scroll to Referral Sources Details.

What to highlight
  • Source
  • Visits
  • Bounce Rate
  • Channel
Why demo it

Best table-based source quality comparison beyond the headline charts.

3

URL Performance Analysis

Where to navigate

Scroll to URL Performance Analysis.

What to highlight
  • URL
  • Traffic
  • LLM Traffic
  • Bounce Rate
  • Consent Rate
  • Page Intent
Why demo it

Best landing-page action story and the clearest place to inspect whether AI-referred traffic actually engages.

4

Business Impact

Where to navigate

Switch from Traffic Insights to Business Impact if Adobe Analytics or GA4 is connected.

What to highlight
  • Page Views
  • Entries
  • Revenue
  • Conversion Rate
  • Avg. Session Duration
  • Pages per Visit
  • Total Orders
Why demo it

Strongest executive close: it answers whether AI-driven discovery is commercially meaningful.

Suggested talk track

"Referral Traffic is the human clickthrough layer. It tells us whether AI citations are sending real visitors, which referrers send the most engaged traffic, which landing pages work, and—when analytics is connected—whether that traffic produces business outcomes."

Recommended full-demo flow

This sequence tells the cleanest story from brand visibility to real business value.

Step 1

Brand Presence

Are we visible?

Start with scorecard metrics, Market Tracking, sentiment, and Share of Voice.

Step 2

Brand Presence → Data Insights

Which topics and prompts explain that score?

Drill from the scorecard into topic-level and prompt-level performance.

Step 3

Agentic Traffic

Can agents reach and read the site?

Show access, success, Content Visibility, and response-code patterns.

Step 4

URL Inspector

Which URLs win or miss citations?

Move from abstract scores to exact URL-level citation performance.

Step 5

Referral Traffic

Did citations create human and business outcomes?

Close on clickthrough, landing-page performance, and business impact.
